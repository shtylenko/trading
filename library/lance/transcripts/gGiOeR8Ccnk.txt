Title: One of the biggest mistakes traders make during headline-driven markets 👎
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=gGiOeR8Ccnk
======================================================================

One of the biggest mistakes traders make during headline driven markets is trading them like normal markets. They're not. When markets become dominated by headlines, like wars, tariffs, Fed comments, or other geopolitical news, the environment changes completely. Why? Because the next major move is often dictated by information you cannot predict. One random headline can instantly invalidate a beautiful daily chart setup. That's why in these types of macro environments, I generally focus much more heavily on shorter time frames and [music] reduce overnight exposure. The combination of massive ranges plus unpredictable creates a market that is often better suited for move-to-move trading rather than longer-term conviction holding. Headline driven volatility carries much more randomness, and randomness increases overnight risk dramatically. The goal in these environments is not to predict every headline. It's to adapt quickly once the headline hits the wires. Follow me for more trading lessons. [music]
