Title: Why I Teach Traders to Trade “with Urgency” #trading #tradingpsychology #tradingmindset
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=KjmwtCKwPFs
======================================================================

Most traders think showing up is the work. They sit in the chair, stare at the screen straight hours, place a few trades, and call it a day. Being present, though, is not the same thing as progressing, and confusing the two is one of the biggest reasons traders stagnate for years. I'm going to explain why you need to swap out that mindset and instead develop a mindset of what I call trading with a sense of urgency, which is what I emphasized for my trainees while I was managing Trillium's Chicago office. And that didn't mean that I needed them to urgently place trades the second they showed up in the morning. What I meant was that every single day I needed my new trainees to show up with an urgency to improve and an urgent desire to be better than they were the prior day. Here's why it matters. Whether you realize it or not, there is an hourglass ticking down the days until you can no longer continue trying your best to make it at trading. At Trillium, we advise trainees to come in with the mindset of a 2-year learning curve. And no matter whether you work for a trading firm or on your own, you need to realize that pursuing trading has an opportunity cost. No matter who you are, you are giving up time and money elsewhere to pursue this craft. You are giving up hours that could be spent making money at a different job or hours that could be spent with friends or family. Another reason why this urgency is important is that most retail traders, especially part-time ones, counterintuitively suffer from having no defined window to make it work. Parkinson's law argues that work expands to fill the time available for its completion. So, what happens when your time to succeed at trading is open-ended? The answer is often apathy rather than urgency.
