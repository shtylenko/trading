Let me explain first a couple different types of buyers and sellers. First, buyers and sellers can show all of their size. Meaning, if they want to buy 5,000 shares, you will see a bid for 50 lots. Remember, all of the windows being discussed always display shares in terms of lots. One lot's 100 shares, so when you see 50, it really represents 5,000 shares. Buyers and sellers can also use a refreshing order, also known as an iceberg order. Refreshing orders are orders that repeatedly reload after getting partially or fully executed. For example, a trader may only display 1,000 shares on the level two, even though they actually want to buy 100,000 shares. Every time the visible 1,000 shares get hit, another 1,000 immediately appears again at the same price. Traders watch for this because it can signal a large institutional buyer or seller absorbing supply or demand. Hidden orders are orders that do not publicly display their size at all on the order book. They still exist and can execute, but other traders cannot fully see them on the level two. You often detect hidden orders indirectly when traders keep executing at a price level despite little or no visible size being shown there. Both refreshing and hidden orders are commonly used by large players trying to avoid revealing the full size of their position to the market. That being said, most retail traders have access to all of these order types. Especially if you are using proper trading software, you should be able to choose your order menu, as well as have options for hidden orders or refreshing orders. Reading the tape, which in modern times is a con- junction of analyzing the level two box along with the prints window, allows us to better identify how large your players are buying or selling stock.
