# Anchored VWAP Pullback Strategy — Martin Luk
## 2025 US Investing Champion — 969% Return

### Overview
Martin Luk, a 23-year-old from Hong Kong, won the 2025 US Investing Championship with a **969% return** (following 283% in 2024). His strategy: buy temporary weakness (pullbacks) within strong uptrends using **Anchored VWAP** (AVWAP) and **key EMAs** as support levels. He risks just 0.5% per trade and keeps stops at 1-4%.

### Core Concept: Buy Weakness in Strength
- **Don't buy weakness in weakness** (stocks in downtrends)
- **Buy weakness in strength** (temporary pullbacks in strong uptrends)
- Wait for multiple support levels to align before entering
- Enter when price **reclaims** support (not while falling)

### Key Indicators
1. **Anchored VWAP (AVWAP):** Anchored to swing highs, swing lows, or major gap days
2. **9 EMA:** Fastest trend line, primary trail
3. **21 EMA:** Intermediate trend support
4. **50 EMA:** Medium-term trend support
5. **Horizontal support levels:** Prior highs, lows, unfilled gaps

### Support Confluence
**The best setups have MULTIPLE supports at the same price:**
- AVWAP + 9 EMA at the same level
- AVWAP + 21 EMA
- AVWAP + horizontal support
- Gap support + moving average support
- The more the merrier

### Entry Rules (Enter on Reclamation)
1. Wait for a strong stock (30%+ over 1/3/6 months, higher highs/lows)
2. Identify consolidation zone (preferably 8+ weeks)
3. Find confluence of AVWAP + EMA + horizontal support
4. Wait for an intraday flush below support
5. **Enter when price RECLAIMS support** (closes back above AVWAP/EMA)
6. Best entry: when stock turns positive for the day after dipping

### Stop Loss Placement
- **Typical stop:** 1-4% (often under 2%)
- Place at: low of entry day, low of 5-min entry candle
- **The Parabolic Effect:** Tightening stop from 3% to 1.5% doubles the R multiple
  - 25% gain with 3% stop = ~8R
  - 25% gain with 1.5% stop = ~16R

### Position Sizing
- **Base risk:** 0.5% of portfolio per trade
- Position size = (0.5% × Account) ÷ Stop distance
- Max portfolio exposure: 35%
- Reduced exposure during drawdowns

### Exit Strategy
**Primary: 9 EMA Trail**
- Exit when price closes below 9 EMA
- In strong trends, use 21 EMA or 50 EMA trail
- **Sell into strength:** Trim 10-15% at 3R+
- Sell when euphoric / position is oversized

### Three-Scanner System
1. **Potent Scan:** Previous day's high performers (30 min/day)
2. **Pre-market Scan:** Stocks gapping up pre-market
3. **Leader Scan:** Weekend scan of biggest movers in past 30 days

### ADR Requirement
- Prefer stocks with **ADR > 5%** (Average Daily Range)
- Stop should be less than 50% of ADR

### Key Statistics
- Win rate: **23%** (wins are rare but massive)
- Average win: ~5x average loss (5:1 reward-to-risk)
- Average loss: -3%
- His discipline: Most trades are small losses; a few are life-changing wins

### December Disaster (26% Drawdown)

### Adapting for $25-100k Account
- Focus on 3-5 high-ADR stocks at $15-100/share
- Use 0.5% risk per trade ($125-500 max loss)
- Keep max 5 open positions
- Weekly chart to confirm trend, daily/hourly for entry
- Practice with small size until you master the flush-buy mechanic

### Common Mistakes
1. Buying before price reclaims support (catching falling knife)
2. Trading weak stocks (not checking 30%+ performance criteria)
3. Overtrading (Luk's own December disaster)
4. Using stops wider than ADR/2
5. Not checking weekly chart for trend context
