Title: My Pre-Market Trading Routine #trading #tradingpsychology #premarkettrading
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=r4UF6Gk3NS0
======================================================================

On to around 8:30 a.m. I'm in my seat checking the markets and news. I'm typing up tickers from the prior day becoming aware of what I need to prepare for. I'll be looking at earnings, IPOs, macro events, upgrades, downgrades, anything that could be moving stocks. And here's the key. I am not trying to predict anything. I never am committing to trading any specific ticker or sector. I am just asking, what is in play today that I should keep a close eye on should it set up according to my playbooks. From there, I build my watchlist. Stocks with news catalysts, stocks making crazy moves, and stocks with volume. I mark key technical levels, prior highs and prior day lows. I place trend lines. I set alerts for everything. There's no excuse to miss something important when there are so many easy ways to set price alerts. By 9:00 a.m., I do what I call my temp check and start my daily report card. This is one of the most important habits of my career. I ask myself a series of brutally honest questions. How do I feel today? Did I sleep well? Am I tired or I distracted? Am I sick or hungover? Emotionally off-center? Is anything bothering me outside of trading? Based on that, I grade myself from an F to an A. And that grade directly determines my risk for the day. If I am a C or worse, my sizing comes way down or I don't trade at all. If I am an A and the market is opportunistic, I can press hard.
