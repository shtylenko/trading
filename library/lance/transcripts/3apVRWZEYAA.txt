You always want to trust the data. Our memories can lie, but numbers don't. This will then allow you to find all sorts of conclusions and patterns to optimize your trading. Maybe certain strategies need to be cut out until they're refined further. You thought you were making money, but they were losing. Maybe other strategies you need to be sizing up far more aggressively. Learning from the past is what can give you edge in the future. Here are some key details I would always be looking for when you analyze your data. For breakouts, was there a fresh news catalyst or not? How does that affect your win rate? For reversal trades, were you trading on the front side or back side of the trade? Was the ticker in play at the time? Were you trading in no man's land? To find true insights, you often need to be spending real time with your data. This is a project that should be taking up hours over many days rather than minutes.
