Title: Stop Worrying About PnL #trading #tradingpsychology #investing
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=1pAUCtWPXOo

Let's say a trader grows an account from 200,000 to 5 million during an incredible run. That peak account value now becomes psychologically embedded in their brain as their money, even if a huge portion of those gains came during unusually favorable market conditions or if they were unrealized. The problem is that once the account pulls back, maybe down to 3.5 million or 3 million, the trader no longer emotionally feels wealthy. Instead, they feel like they've lost $2 million. That shift is incredibly dangerous because humans hate losses far more than they enjoy gains. Behavioral finance research consistently shows losses create disproportionately strong emotional reactions. So, now the trader isn't calmly trading the market anymore. They're emotionally fixated on getting the account back to the high-water mark. Every trade becomes psychologically tied to recovery. The brutal irony is that the larger the account once became, the harder this trap becomes psychologically. A trader who once saw their account at 10 million may emotionally struggle accepting life with a $6 million account, even though that amount is objectively life-changing wealth. Their reference point has been distorted upwards. Because the mind anchors so heavily to peak values, many traders would rather risk catastrophic damage trying to recover the old peak rather than emotionally accepting to operate below it.
