Urgency also means that you show up each day with a very specific goal. Not 10 goals, one. Something concrete that you are intentionally working on. Maybe it's better entries, maybe it's not chasing, maybe it's cutting your losers faster. Whatever it is, that goal dictates what you're paying attention to all day. And at the end of the day, you grade yourself on it honestly. If you don't measure your behavior, you're guessing about your progress. And here's the part that gets so often overlooked. Urgency does not disappear when the market is slow. In fact, that's when it matters most. When there aren't great opportunities, you're not bored and checked out. You're expanding your playbook, you're studying past trades, and you're researching new setups. You might even be back testing, and you're finding ways to be better prepared for when the market heats back up. Slow markets are tuition-free classrooms if you treat them that way.
