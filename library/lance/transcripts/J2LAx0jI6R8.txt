Reason number one, they don't give themselves a long enough runway. Most people who get into trading have been sold a dream. Make a few grand a month trading options from your phone, quit your job, and trade from the beach in 30 days. It's all total garbage. The reality, to even make a dime consistently in trading, it takes years. And that's if you're doing it the right way with mentorship, with structure, working full-time hours, and treating it like a real craft. Think of it like basketball. When you shoot a three-pointer, chances are it might go in. But if you make one shot, you don't think you're the next Steph Curry. But when it comes to trading, people think after one or even after a few successful trades, they have it all figured out. If you're part-time learning on your own, no guidance, no framework, you're looking at four to 5 years minimum before you even sniff profitability. Far and away, the most common reason traders fail is due to unrealistic expectations. There are so many well-intentioned, smart, capable people who give up, not because they weren't talented, but because they didn't realize how long the road was. If you can only commit a few hours a week and you're treating trading as a hobby, that's totally fine. But if you're trying to make this your livelihood, you better be ready for thousands of hours of real work.
