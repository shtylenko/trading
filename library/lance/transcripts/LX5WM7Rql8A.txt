So we're going to get into the behind the scenes process about how I was actually selected for the books. As many people on the internet guessed, it starts with wiring Jack and George a $50,000 fee for getting featured in the books. No, I'm just kidding, obviously. The real process is actually way more intense than people expect. Before any interview happens, there's a full audit process where you have to submit all of your trading records, both in the case of me from my time at a trading firm and my personal accounts. So for me, I sent all my data from my career at Trillium, along with my personal brokerage account statements. And then we compiled all of that into a spreadsheet to estimate annual returns. From what I understand, they were looking for at least a 10-year track record along with what they consider exceptional performance. And I actually respect that they don't explicitly define exceptional too rigidly because it comes in different forms. Some traders are hyper-consistent, others are more volatile but with huge upside. And both can be valid depending on how they're achieved. One of the more nuanced parts of the process was normalizing the data. For example, when I was at Trillium, I was trading in terms of buying power, which doesn't directly translate to capital in the way most people think about it. So we had to adjust for that context. Once they were comfortable with the track record, that's when the interview start. And that's where it got really fun and interesting. The first interview was long, like hours long. I don't remember how long it went, but I know I started the call in the early evening and by the time we finished, my apartment was completely dark. What stood out to me was that they weren't focused on breaking down every trader pattern. They were much more interested in the psychology, the motivations, and the decision-making process. And that lines up with in my view what a lot of the best parts of the books are. Where the real value isn't just in the setup, it's in how these traders developed into who they are.
