# **Comprehensive Specification and Audit of an Intraday Gap-and-Go Feature Library**

## **Introduction to Intraday Market Microstructure and the Gap-and-Go Framework**

The quantitative analysis of equity market microstructure provides a uniquely fertile ground for systematic trading, particularly within the highly active opening moments of the regular trading session. The "gap-and-go" strategy represents a specialized execution framework designed to capture intraday momentum generated by overnight supply and demand imbalances.1 This structural phenomenon typically manifests when a security opens significantly above its prior day's high or close, an event often catalyzed by exogenous factors such as earnings reports, macroeconomic data releases, or distinct corporate announcements.2  
Within this specified framework, the architecture evaluates a highly constrained, rule-based universe for admitting candidate equities into a long-only, intraday portfolio. The core strategy mandates that each morning, the system must admit tickers that gapped up and printed a "green" (close \> open) first 5-minute candle.1 The execution trigger is a breakout above that initial 5-minute candle's high, with a predefined 1R (one unit of risk) stop-loss placed precisely at the candle's low, and a corresponding 1R profit target enforced alongside a time-based exit prior to late morning.1 This parameterization seeks to isolate pure directional momentum before the intraday mean-reversion forces, which dominate the mid-day session, can overwhelm the initial imbalance.1  
Because the ultimate audience for this feature library is an offline search algorithm tasked with identifying robust, multi-year statistical edges through combinatorial filtering, the foundational data engineering process must be mathematically pristine.7 The resulting pipeline captures a broad feature vector—comprising 81 distinct measurements—strictly at 09:35 ET, the precise moment the first regular 5-minute candle (09:30–09:35) of the trade date fully closes.3 Every candidate event is thereby described by a fixed-key record alongside its realized ![][image1] outcome. This rectangular dataset serves as the empirical bedrock for out-of-sample walk-forward validation and rigorous Probability of Backtest Overfitting (PBO) assessments.7  
This exhaustive report details the mathematical construction, theoretical justification, and microstructural nuances of these 81 features, scrutinizes the data primitives and temporal guardrails, and proposes advanced extensions derived from contemporary quantitative finance literature to enhance the predictive capacity of the downstream machine learning optimizer.

## **Data Primitives, Temporal Integrity, and the Eradication of Look-Ahead Bias**

The foremost vulnerability in any quantitative backtesting environment, particularly within high-frequency or intraday paradigms, is look-ahead bias—a critical form of data leakage where future information inadvertently contaminates the historical feature state.10 Within the context of the 09:35 ET decision matrix, the cardinal rule dictates an absolute temporal barrier: a feature may only be derived from data knowable prior to or exactly at that timestamp.11

### **Temporal Alignment and Multi-Timeframe Engineering**

Multi-timeframe (MTF) analysis introduces significant leakage vectors if historical data is not rigorously timestamped, aligned, and sequestered.10 The underlying architecture strictly isolates the trade\_date's first 5-minute candle (first\_bar) and enforces a strict inequality (\< trade\_date) for all trailing daily bars and prior intraday sessions. By anchoring all temporal calculations explicitly to the America/New\_York (Eastern Time) timezone, the system preemptively neutralizes misalignments that frequently occur when normalizing disparate data feeds across global exchanges, or when failing to account for daylight saving transitions.14  
The explicit exclusion of the trade\_date's own daily bar ensures that the eventual closing price, maximum high, or daily volume of the current session cannot leak into the morning decision logic.11 Even the integration of broad market proxies, such as the SPY (S\&P 500 ETF) and matched sector ETFs (e.g., XLK, XLF, XLE), is subjected to this rigorous temporal boundary, ensuring that broad market beta computations rely solely on prior sessions or the concurrent 09:30–09:35 ET slice.12

### **The Data Primitives Matrix**

The feature generation engine relies on a highly specified set of raw inputs. Deep history—approximately 420 calendar days, equating to roughly 290 trading days—is provided for the daily series to ensure that macro-trend indicators, such as 200-day simple moving averages, have sufficient runway to compute without generating null values for recently listed entities.

### **Degenerate Handling and the Rectangular Dataset**

Machine learning models and downstream offline search algorithms are highly sensitive to unhandled degenerate states, such as NaN values or infinities resulting from division by zero.19 The library specification correctly mandates returning a static None for uncomputable metrics—whether due to zero intraday range, zero mathematical variance, or insufficient trailing history. This convention ensures the output is a flat, fixed-key record, guaranteeing that the capture across many tickers and days forms a perfectly rectangular table. This structural uniformity allows downstream search algorithms to systematically treat missingness as a distinct categorical state, or impute it via median cross-sectional values, without corrupting the objective function.19

## **The Imperative of Raw Data and Corporate Action Guardrails**

A profound architectural decision within this feature library is the exclusive utilization of raw, unadjusted price and volume data.20 Standard financial datasets provided by major vendors are retroactively adjusted for corporate actions, including forward stock splits, reverse splits, and massive special dividend distributions.20 While split-adjusted data is an absolute necessity for calculating long-term total returns and maintaining continuous backtests over decades, it fundamentally corrupts point-in-time, intraday trading simulations.21  
For instance, calculating the precise number of shares to purchase based on a strict 1R risk metric requires knowledge of the exact nominal spread between the entry price and the stop-loss price as it existed in reality on the specific trade date.21 If a backtest utilizes adjusted data for a stock that subsequently underwent a 10-for-1 forward split, the historical 1R spread might be compressed to fractions of a penny, triggering rounding errors and unrealistic execution assumptions regarding minimum tick sizes and slippage.7

### **The Mathematical Mechanics of the Split Guard**

While raw data preserves the integrity of intraday risk modeling, computing trailing statistics (such as 60-day volatility, beta, or 200-day moving averages) on unadjusted data across a corporate action event results in catastrophic mathematical artifacts.26 If an equity undergoes a 4-for-1 forward split, the raw close price will artificially drop by 75% from Friday to Monday.22 If an algorithm unknowingly computes a 20-day rolling variance or a 50-day moving average across this boundary, the resulting features will be massively corrupted, registering as unprecedented volatility shocks or profound fundamental collapses.26  
To neutralize this without introducing the look-ahead bias inherent in utilizing retroactive split factors, the architecture implements dynamic, unsupervised split guards (split\_63 and split\_252). These guardrails systematically invalidate and return None for features spanning long windows if any close-to-close absolute move exceeds 40%, or if an open-versus-prior-close move exceeds the 40% threshold.26  
This 40% absolute boundary is theoretically sound and empirically validated. Idiosyncratic, single-day equity moves exceeding 40% in large-cap or mid-cap equities are exceedingly rare without a corporate action.26 Furthermore, even if an organic price movement of 40% does occur—perhaps due to an unprecedented bankruptcy filing or a massive buyout acquisition—such an event represents an extreme microstructural regime shift. Following such a shock, historical baseline metrics (like 60-day momentum or 252-day volatility percentiles) become entirely unrepresentative of the asset's new equilibrium state. Therefore, nullifying historical features in the wake of a 40% jump is an optimal mathematical response, regardless of whether the jump was caused by a nominal stock split or a legitimate fundamental shock.

## **Exhaustive Taxonomy of the 81-Feature Vector**

The 81 features defined within the specification are meticulously categorized to capture divergent dimensions of the equity's state space exactly at 09:35 ET. These dimensions blend structural price action, volume liquidity dynamics, relative market strength, and heavily smoothed momentum oscillators.

### **Category A & E2: Gap and Overnight Structure**

The magnitude, context, and relativity of the overnight gap serve as the primary catalyst and gating mechanism for the gap-and-go thesis.1 The library evaluates the gap from multiple baselines to discern true breakouts from standard overnight noise:

* **gap\_pct\_vs\_prior\_high and gap\_pct\_vs\_prior\_close:** Measuring the opening print against the prior day's absolute high identifies structural breakouts from localized resistance.1 Conversely, measuring against the prior close merely identifies the raw overnight imbalance. The former is a significantly stronger signal, as clearing a previous high forces the capitulation of trapped short sellers, fueling the momentum necessary for the "go" component of the strategy.1  
* **gap\_atr and gap\_range\_frac:** Normalizing the raw percentage gap is mathematically essential for cross-sectional comparison. A 3% gap in a low-beta, low-volatility utility equity represents a multi-sigma shock, whereas the exact same 3% gap in a highly speculative, high-beta biotechnology firm constitutes standard daily noise.30 By dividing the gap distance by the 14-period Average True Range (ATR14) or the prior day's explicit range, the algorithm translates nominal price movements into volatility-adjusted multiples.31  
* **gap\_vs\_spy\_gap\_diff:** This metric isolates idiosyncratic alpha by subtracting the broad market SPY gap percentage from the individual stock's gap percentage. The specification wisely employs this arithmetic difference rather than a geometric ratio (rel\_spy\_gap). Ratio-based relative metrics become mathematically unstable, tending toward infinity, when the denominator (the SPY gap) approaches zero.16  
* **prior\_gap\_fill\_fraction:** For equities that experienced a gap-up on the prior session, this feature measures the retracement depth. Formulated by analyzing the prior open versus the prior low relative to the original gap distance, a value of 0.0 indicates a gap that never retraced—suggesting overwhelming, sustained institutional demand. A value approaching 1.0 indicates complete indecision and a filled gap, rendering consecutive momentum highly improbable.

### **Category B: First-Candle Microstructure**

The 09:30–09:35 ET candle represents the market's initial, chaotic processing of the overnight catalyst.9 The microstructural shape of this candle provides profound insights into order flow dynamics.

* **first\_body\_frac, first\_upper\_wick\_frac, and first\_lower\_wick\_frac:** These fractional features decompose the opening battle. The body fraction (|c \- o| / range) measures absolute directional conviction. A large lower wick fraction indicates aggressive dip absorption by institutional buyers stepping into early retail panic.1 Conversely, a massive upper wick demonstrates severe selling rejection at the highs, a highly bearish structural warning for a strategy attempting to buy a breakout above that very high.9  
* **first\_close\_pos:** Defined mathematically as ![][image2], this calculates where the 5-minute candle closed relative to its own extreme range. The library notes a critical contamination caveat here: this feature was reverse-engineered from in-sample loss analysis. While closing at the absolute high (1.0) might seem bullish, intraday mean-reversion mechanics suggest that an extreme close implies exhaustion.30 If the candle closes at its absolute peak, the subsequent breakout entry is forcing the algorithm to chase a highly extended, unbroken local move, drastically increasing the probability of an immediate pullback that triggers the tight 1R stop-loss.1  
* **breakout\_distance:** Calculated as ![][image3], this metric quantifies exactly how far above the opening print the entry trigger sits. An excessively large distance implies the intraday move has already expended massive energy, fundamentally altering the risk-to-reward asymmetry and potentially placing the 1R profit target at an unreachable intraday extreme.9

### **Category C & F2: Volume and Liquidity Dynamics**

In market microstructure theory, volume is the ultimate validator of price action.36 An aggressive overnight gap without elevated volume participation is highly prone to fading, representing a low-liquidity vacuum rather than genuine demand.1

* **first\_vol\_frac\_of\_prior\_day:** By dividing the opening 5-minute volume by the entire prior day's cumulative volume, the model generates an instant climax proxy. If a security trades 40% to 50% of its normal daily volume within the first 300 seconds of the session, it is definitively "in play," drawing the attention of algorithmic momentum scanners and retail crowds alike.37  
* **opening\_rv and rvol\_20d:** Relative Volume (RVOL) compares current opening participation against historical opening baselines.37 High RVOL acts as a distinct institutional footprint, verifying that the gap is driven by significant capital deployment.37  
* **opening\_volume\_zscore\_20d:** While RVOL provides a ratio, standardizing the opening volume into a Z-score allows for precise cross-sectional comparison across the entire equity universe, identifying which specific tickers are experiencing the most mathematically profound standard deviation shocks on the given morning.13

A security gapping in the direction of an already established macro-trend exhibits a statistically higher probability of continuation, as it aligns with the prevailing path of least resistance.39

### **Category E, D2 & C2: Relativity, Beta, and Volatility Regimes**

Isolating the asset's specific performance from the broad market beta is essential for identifying true idiosyncratic alpha.35

* **beta\_60d and stock\_sector\_corr\_60d:** Calculated as ![][image7], a lower beta combined with a low sector correlation strongly implies that the asset is reacting to an idiosyncratic corporate catalyst (such as clinical trial results or specific earnings) rather than merely riding macroeconomic tides.33 Idiosyncratic catalysts exhibit significantly less correlation to mid-day index mean-reversion, making them ideal gap-and-go candidates.  
* **realized\_vol\_percentile\_252:** Absolute volatility metrics are difficult to compare across sectors. By standardizing the current 20-day realized volatility against its own trailing 252-day distribution, the feature frames the current volatility regime.46 Volatility compression (indicated by extremely low percentiles) preceding an opening gap often signals a structural volatility expansion event—mechanically akin to a Bollinger Band squeeze—yielding explosive, uninhibited opening range breakouts.46

### **Category A2 & A3: Prior-Session Intraday Structure**

The psychological and financial positioning of market participants from the previous day's session heavily influences the current morning's open.5

* **prior\_late\_volume\_share and prior\_afternoon\_return\_pct:** High volume participation coupled with positive returns in the final two hours of the prior session (14:00–16:00 ET) indicates concerted institutional accumulation heading into the close.30 This late-day urgency often forecasts continued, aggressive demand at the subsequent open.

### **Category F & G2: Calendar and Seasonality**

Temporal positioning generates highly predictable liquidity distortions driven by mechanical market mechanics rather than fundamental news.5

* **is\_opex and days\_since\_opex:** Options expiration dates—specifically the third Friday of the month—force massive dealer hedging, gamma unwinds, and the rolling of derivatives positions, which fundamentally alter the underlying equity's volatility profile.50 Tracking the specific distance in days from OPEX aligns the algorithm with the predictable ebb and flow of market maker exposure cycles.5  
* **is\_first\_trading\_day\_of\_month:** This boolean feature captures the systematic, algorithmic inflows originating from massive institutional portfolio rebalancing and retail retirement account capital allocations that reliably execute at the turn of the month.5

## **Advanced Feature Proposals and Theoretical Augmentations**

While the current 81 features constitute a highly robust predictive matrix, quantitative research into high-frequency time series data and advanced volatility estimators suggests several leak-free augmentations that could substantially refine the downstream model's predictive accuracy.8

### **1\. High-Efficiency Range-Based Volatility Estimators**

The current specification relies predominantly on close-to-close realized volatility (realized\_vol\_20d) and smoothed ATR for risk baselining. However, academic consensus demonstrates that standard close-to-close variance estimators are statistically inefficient because they entirely ignore the asset's intraday path.46 The feature library should be augmented with advanced range-based estimators over the prior daily bars array:

* **Parkinson Volatility:** By utilizing the daily high and low prices, the Parkinson estimator captures the intra-period extremes. Under the assumption of continuous trading, it is approximately 5.2 times more efficient than a traditional close-to-close metric.51  
  ![][image9]  
* **Garman-Klass Volatility:** This estimator incorporates all four points of the OHLC bar. By capturing both the intraday high-low range and the open-to-close drift, Garman-Klass achieves roughly 7.4 times the statistical efficiency of close-to-close estimators.46 It is heavily utilized for assets operating in non-trending, zero-drift environments.  
  ![][image10]  
* **Rogers-Satchell Volatility:** A critical limitation of Garman-Klass is its assumption of zero drift (Brownian motion). The Rogers-Satchell estimator corrects this by explicitly accounting for non-zero mean returns, making it superior for evaluating equities experiencing strong directional trends.46  
  ![][image11]  
* **Yang-Zhang Volatility:** Recognized as the optimal multi-component estimator, Yang-Zhang synthesizes overnight variance (close-to-open), open-to-close variance, and the Rogers-Satchell drift-adjusted estimator.46 This structure elegantly handles both overnight jump risks (gaps) and continuous intraday drift.48 By engineering a feature that tracks the ratio of Yang-Zhang volatility to standard close-to-close volatility, the model can explicitly identify equities where overnight gap shocks account for the vast majority of historical variance, isolating true "gappers" from continuous intraday runners.47

### **2\. Inferring Microstructure Friction: The Roll Spread Estimator**

Because the current architecture cannot access Level 2 NBBO (National Best Bid and Offer) order book data or point-in-time spread snapshots due to deliberate leak-free data constraints, true liquidity execution costs are entirely hidden from the model.55 However, effective spreads can be mathematically inferred strictly from the available hist\_5m OHLC data utilizing the **Roll Spread Estimator**.55  
The Roll model posits that in the absence of new fundamental information, observed trade prices will bounce back and forth between the prevailing bid and the ask.57 This mechanical "bid-ask bounce" induces a measurable, negative serial covariance in successive short-term price changes.57  
![][image12]  
If the calculated covariance is positive, the Roll spread is mathematically constrained and set to zero.57 By applying the Roll estimator to the hist\_5m series over the prior 5 days, the algorithm generates a perfectly leak-free proxy for the implicit execution friction and order book depth of the candidate.56 This allows the offline search optimizer to heavily penalize or outright filter out assets with prohibitively wide inferred spreads that would otherwise instantly consume the tight 1R profit parameters.57 A complementary augmentation is the Corwin-Schultz high-low spread estimator, which operates on the premise that daily highs are generally buyer-initiated (ask) and daily lows are seller-initiated (bid), extracting liquidity proxies strictly from broad daily bars.55

### **3\. Intraday Return Autocorrelation and Reversal Dynamics**

Deep market microstructure research demonstrates that intraday returns exhibit highly specific autocorrelation profiles depending heavily on the observation time horizon.5 While sub-minute high-frequency returns often show momentum continuation due to the algorithmic splitting of large institutional block orders, 15-minute and 30-minute horizons frequently exhibit significant mean-reversion (negative autocorrelation).5 This reversal effect is primarily driven by inventory unwinding by market makers attempting to neutralize their exposure.5  
By computing the **first-order autocorrelation coefficient** of the prior day's continuous 5-minute returns, the algorithm can mathematically identify whether the specific asset operates in a trending (positive autocorrelation) or deeply mean-reverting (negative autocorrelation) microstructure regime.61 Because the gap-and-go strategy inherently demands rapid, unidirectional continuation, targeting assets exhibiting positive historical intraday autocorrelation drastically enhances the statistical probability of the breakout sustaining itself to the 1R target.61

## **Downstream Machine Learning Dynamics: Collinearity, CSCV, and PBO**

The ultimate utility of this meticulously engineered feature library lies strictly in its application within an offline, combinatorial search environment.7 The architecture dictates a static, historical capture of the feature vector, outputting a rectangular ledger.7

### **Addressing Dimensionality and Collinearity**

The specification wisely acknowledges the pervasive presence of collinearity within the feature space—for example, measuring the exact same overnight gap magnitude through five highly correlated mathematical lenses (gap\_pct\_vs\_prior\_high, gap\_pct\_vs\_prior\_close, gap\_atr, gap\_range\_frac, gap\_zscore\_60d).16 While modern deep learning architectures and tree-based ensemble models (like XGBoost or Random Forests) can internally partition and weight correlated features without severe degradation, traditional combinatorial search algorithms or linear regression models will rapidly waste degrees of freedom and artificially inflate standard errors if collinearity is not actively penalized.16  
During the offline optimization phase, dimensionality reduction techniques such as Principal Component Analysis (PCA) or feature-selection protocols utilizing Lasso (L1) regularization must be deployed.16 These techniques compress redundant dimensions into orthogonal vectors, ensuring the search algorithm identifies the true, underlying causal drivers of momentum rather than overfitting to duplicated statistical noise.16

### **Combinatorially Symmetric Cross-Validation and PBO**

Because the library contains 81 highly engineered features, the combinatorial space for generating filtering rules is astronomically large.8 Searching a space of this magnitude will inevitably yield hyper-specific, highly complex subsets that perform flawlessly in-sample due purely to random variance and historical data mining.7  
To systematically combat this illusion of alpha, the pipeline must rely entirely on walk-forward out-of-sample folds combined with the computation of a PBO (Probability of Backtest Overfitting) statistic.7 PBO utilizes Combinatorially Symmetric Cross-Validation (CSCV) to simulate thousands of dynamic train/test splits across the dataset. It determines the explicit mathematical probability that an "optimal" in-sample strategy will actually underperform the median baseline strategy when deployed out-of-sample.7 Features that carry robust, theoretically grounded structural logic—such as ADX for trend velocity, the Kaufman Efficiency Ratio for noise reduction, or normalized Relative Volume for institutional footprints—will consistently survive CSCV perturbations.31 Conversely, mathematically fragile or heavily overfit metrics (such as the admittedly contaminated first\_close\_pos) will collapse during rigorous walk-forward validation.8

## **Synthesis and Architectural Implications**

The detailed feature library constitutes a highly sophisticated, institutionally viable framework for executing intraday gap-and-go strategies.9 By strictly tethering all calculations to the 09:35 ET timestamp and enforcing absolute adherence to look-ahead bias prevention protocols, the architecture guarantees temporal integrity.11 Furthermore, the explicit exclusion of retroactively split-adjusted data—paired effectively with an unsupervised 40% threshold split guard—demonstrates a profound, practical understanding of how corporate actions silently corrupt historical algorithmic execution systems.26  
The 81 individual features successfully map the multidimensional state space of equity momentum. They balance raw price action geometry (structural gaps, moving average distances), micro-structural order flow (candle wicks, close positioning), momentum efficiency (Wilder's ADX, Kaufman's KER), and broad statistical relativity (Z-scores, rolling historical percentiles).31 To push the predictive boundaries of this library even further without violating the strict leak-free constraints, the integration of mathematically derived liquidity proxies (such as the Roll Spread estimator) and advanced, range-based variance estimators (Rogers-Satchell, Yang-Zhang) will provide the downstream machine learning optimizer with vital, hidden insights into the true execution friction and intrinsic volatility profiles of the underlying assets.47  
Ultimately, this comprehensive specification successfully distills the chaotic, high-velocity environment of the equity market's opening bell into a stable, highly quantifiable matrix, establishing an immaculate foundation for rigorous mathematical inquiry, robust out-of-sample validation, and systematic alpha extraction.
