## 1. Stock Selection — The 5 Pillars (Non-Negotiable)

**Canonical Rule (repeated almost verbatim across all high-view trainings):**

A stock must pass **all five** pillars on the day you consider trading it. No exceptions for "almost" or "close enough."

| Pillar              | Threshold (Main)          | Small Account Notes          | Notes |
|---------------------|---------------------------|------------------------------|-------|
| **Price**           | $2 – $20 (best $5–$10)   | ~$1.50–$6                    | Below $2 often unreliable; above $20 hard to size |
| **% Gain Today**    | ≥ 10% (ideally leading gainer or >> 25%) | Same                        | Stocks already up 10%+ have highest continuation odds |
| **Relative Volume** | ≥ 5× average (min 2×)    | Same                        | Liquidity + participation confirmation |
| **Catalyst**        | Breaking news required   | Same                        | News is the spark; sector sympathy is secondary/riskier |
| **Float**           | < 20M (hot), < 10M preferred, < 5M cold | Same                   | Low supply + demand = explosive moves |

**Verbatim examples from corpus:**
> "Number one, I do the best on stocks that have five times above average volume today... Number two... up more than 10% today... The fifth pillar... float of less than 10 million shares."

> "If a stock doesn't meet the criteria for each of these... I will not trade it."

**Practical Scanner Filter (daily use)**
- Sort by % gain or custom "5 pillars alert" scanner.
- Require news headline (or very obvious pre-market strength).
- Confirm float (many scanners show it).
- Reject anything that fails even one pillar.

**What he explicitly avoids**
- No catalyst / "mystery" movers
- High float (> 50–200M)
- Very low-priced penny stocks
- Stocks that are only up 3–7% on the day

## 2. Risk Management & The Icebreaker System

**Icebreaker Rule (the single biggest contributor to long green streaks)**
- Begin every day at approximately **25% of your normal position size**.
- Scale up to full size **only after** booking a profit milestone (commonly $1,000 on the main account; proportionally lower for small accounts).
- Cold day and never hit the milestone? Stay small the entire day.
- This mechanically keeps red days small and lets you go big only when the market is giving you confirmation.

**Daily Max Loss**
- Set a hard daily loss limit (examples in corpus range $5k–$7.5k+ on main account; always scale to your account).
- The "30 cent rule" is frequently referenced as a baseline per-share risk on typical setups.

**When to Walk Away (repeated rules)**
- You have given back more than half your daily profit.
- Three sizable losses in a row.
- No obvious A-quality setup for an extended period.
- You have hit your daily profit goal — protect it.

**Position Sizing Formula (universal)**
Shares = Dollar risk you are willing to lose on this trade ÷ (Entry price – Stop price)

**Never average down.** Add only to winners (scaling).

## 3. Core Entry Patterns

### 3.1 Flagship: First Candle New High (ACD-style)
**Setup conditions**
- Clear momentum / uptrend or powerful catalyst-driven move.
- Pullback or consolidation (micro pullback or flat top).
- Meets all 5 pillars + volume confirmation.

**Entry**
- The **first 5-minute candle** to make a new high after the pullback.
- Enter **immediately** on the break of the prior candle's high — do **not** wait for the candle to close.

**Stop**
- Low of the pullback/consolidation candle (not the entry candle).

**Target / Exit**
- Primary target: retest of the day's high.
- Exit on first red candle closing below the prior green candle's low.
- Or trail once significantly profitable.

> "The first candle to make a new high. That is my entry. The moment the price breaks that candle, I do not wait for the candle to close."

### 3.2 Micro Pullback
Designed for fast movers that never give a deep pullback.
- Brief 1–2 candle shallow pullback in a strong runner.
- Entry on the first green that breaks the micro-pullback high.
- Still requires the stock to pass the 5 pillars.

### 3.3 Other Strong Setups
- Red-to-green (especially into/after halts).
- Sub-VWAP trap / VWAP reclaim after washout (one of his favorites).
- Break of pre-market highs or opening range on leading gainers.

## 4. VWAP Strategies

- **Support bounce**: Strong stock pulls back to VWAP and bounces → buy the curl.
- **Sub-VWAP trap** (favorite intraday): Stock spikes hard → washes below VWAP (shaking out longs) → reclaims VWAP with volume. Enter on the reclaim.
- VWAP as resistance (occasional shorts).

## 5. Market Condition Adaptation

**Hot market**
- Icebreaker succeeds quickly → become aggressive.
- Multiple names can work.
- Focus on the clearest, highest-volume leaders.

**Cold market**
- Stay small and extremely selective.
- Often only the very best news-driven stocks are worth trading.
- Easier and smarter to walk away.

He says repeatedly: be aggressive when the market is hot; take your foot off the gas when it is cooler.

## 6. Psychology, Discipline & Daily Process

- Daily pre-market internal + external checklist (sleep, emotional state, recent performance, yesterday's strength, scanner picture, FOMO level).
- Track **your own** data obsessively. The rules above came from analyzing tens of thousands of his own trades.
- "If it's not obvious to you what stock you should be trading, then there is not an A-quality setup."
- Successful red day = you lost within your rules and maintained discipline.
- Results are never typical. Simulator practice until the rules are automatic.

## 7. Small Account Specifics (from multiple challenges)

- Start very small (even 1–10 shares) to condition yourself to real-money emotions.
- Target consistent small base hits.
- Icebreaker + strict risk is even more critical.
- The same 5 pillars apply (sometimes price range is compressed lower).
- Goal progression examples exist from $583 → $100k+ → millions, but the process is the same.
