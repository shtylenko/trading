Title: Why Trading “Win Rate” Isn’t That Important #expectedvalue #trading #tradingstrategies
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=yxXb1mKxMYU
======================================================================

What win rate does a trader need to be successful? The astute ones would see the trap. You do not need a high win rate. You just need positive expected value. I've known worldclass traders who only win 20% of the time because their reward to risk ratio was massive. I've also known traders who win 90% of the time, but risk $1 to make 25, managing losses perfectly. Both types can be extremely profitable if the expected value is positive. This is why expected value isn't just math. It's also mindset. You must think in probabilities, not outcomes. Most traders think, "I lost money, so it was a bad trade." Or, "I made money, so it was a good trade." Wrong. You can lose on a good trade if the odds didn't play out this time. You can win on a terrible trade that had negative expected value. The individual outcome means nothing. What matters is the quality of your process and how it performs over hundreds of repetitions.
