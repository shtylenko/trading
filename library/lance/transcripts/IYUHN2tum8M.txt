What's the difference between an uptrend and a downtrend? An uptrend makes higher highs and higher lows. Each pullback finds support above the last low. Downtrends make lower lows and lower highs. Each bounce [music] fails below the last high. An uptrend turns old resistance into new support. Price breaks [music] through, comes back and holds above it. Downtrends turn old support into new resistance. Price breaks down, tries to bounce and fails under that zone. An uptrend stays above VWAP moving averages. Pullbacks into those levels hold and bounce. [music] Downtrends stay below VWAP and falling moving averages. Pops into those levels fail and roll over. An uptrend has controlled pullbacks on lighter volume than sharp moves higher when volume returns. Downtrends have weak bounces on lighter volume than heavy selling that flushes price lower. An uptrend sees breakouts hold and extend higher, often building flags instead of snapping [music] back.
