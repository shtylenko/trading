Title: 5 Signs to Continue Pursuing Trading #trading #tradingpsychology #stocks
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=cY1k2PpD64k
======================================================================

The first sign you should not quit is if you are able to set goals and see progress even if you do not yet see the P&L. This was a big one that saved my career. If you are able to look back at prior months and tangibly describe what areas of your trading have improved, maybe your discipline's better or your entries are improving, maybe your emotional control's also better. You are moving in the right direction even if the money has not arrived yet. The second sign you should not quit is a big one. And this is if you conceptually understand these trading concepts. This was also a huge one that kept me from leaving Trillium. One of my more experienced co-workers recognized that I understood everything conceptually even if I was not yet capitalizing in real time. He could tell from my ticker call-outs and my questions that I was on the right track. I understood expected value and technical analysis. And cognitively, things were starting to click even if I was still too slow to execute in real time. The solution to that problem is often just reps and practice. The third sign you should not quit trading is if not only do you love the challenge and struggle of cracking the code, but all your work as a trader is actually making you a better person. In many ways, I believe trading to be a microcosm for life. And trading helps refine who we are. If trading is forcing you to become your best self, you're getting good sleep, have undistracted focused work, you're going to the gym, eating healthy, and doing whatever it takes to perform at your best each day, then that is a really good sign. The fourth sign you should not quit is that whenever you struggle or face a tough period, it actually motivates you to come back stronger and work harder. I think one thing that really helped me persevere as a trader is that every time I struggled, it made me hungrier. Anytime I made a huge mistake or took a painful loss, all those negative emotions made me obsessed with never making that same mistake again. Even if that meant going into the office on Sundays or staying late after work, I did not want even another minute to go by before I analyzed what happened and built systems to prevent it from happening again. The final sign you should not quit is if you have a highly defined process to get better and the speed at which you iterate and improve after mistakes is high. One thing Mike Bellafiore of SMB Capital once said stood out to him about me was that anytime I wanted to improve in area of my trading, not only was I obsessed with finding a solution, but the ferocity and speed at which I implemented that solution far exceeded most other traders he'd worked with. I think that is a huge separator in this profession. The traders who improve the fastest are often the ones who close the feedback loop the fastest as well.
