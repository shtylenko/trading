Ross is absolutely not in the same bucket as the worst trading frauds. He absolutely is a real trader with a decade of real experience. His understanding of the micro cap space, reading the tape, and his own trading habits are clearly real, and his transparency on providing statements deserves recognition. At the same time, I think it's totally fair to question the purity of the record and put a disclaimer on his profits in this specific case. The same way that Mark McGuire and Barry Bonds had the steroids asterisk. In plain English, Ross may be a very good trader, but also maybe not since he is not trading under laboratory conditions. I totally think his following has a lot to learn from him, especially if you are a beginner. His content's great, but as a consumer, you need to question how transferable that edge is to you as the audience, and also how transferable that edge is outside of the market of micro caps. And maybe the biggest lesson for beginner traders is this. Even when someone is legitimate, that does not automatically mean their edge is cleanly replicable to you.
