The traders who try to get rich the fastest are often the slowest learners. They're too outcome-focused. They never develop emotional stability because they attach survival-level importance to short-term outcome. It's a horrible environment for growth. When I was developing as a trader, one of the biggest shifts came when I started focusing on improving my expected value instead of obsessing over P&L. That mindset change is enormous. You need to judge yourself based on the quality of your decisions and process, not short-term monetary outcomes because good traders can lose money in the short run, and terrible trading can make money temporarily. Markets are probabilistic. The only thing you truly control is the process.
