So many people in the trading community believe in equal bet sizing. Meaning whether it's an A or a D-grade setup, you size the same every time. Personally, I don't believe that is the most optimal way to do it. Let's take a prime example out of poker. For the sake of simplicity, imagine sitting at the poker table with one other player over the course of two hands and this is what you draw. First 92 and then pocket aces for the other hand. The estimated win percentage or pre flop equity for the above hands looks something like the following. 92 is 38.9% and pocket aces is 85.3%. If you as the player sitting at the table were to bet equally across each hand above, do you think you'd walk away with any winnings in the long run? The answer is a resounding no due to the fact that you're not taking advantage of exponential bet sizing. When you draw pocket aces, you know that although it's not guaranteed, you have the strongest hand possible to start with and it's time to start raising your bets. This is when you have the highest chance to make some money. The question begs, how often do you get dealt aces? Statistically, the probability of being dealt aces is 1 in 221 hands. Given how rarely this happens, if it happens, you need to not only recognize it, but capitalize on it. If you have less than a half percent chance of drawing pocket aces that give you close to an 85% probability of winning, you go for it every time. Does it mean you'll always win? Of course not. But over the long run, this will prove to be profitable. That's the equivalent of an A+ trade. You need to be able to recognize and capitalize by betting exponentially bigger.
