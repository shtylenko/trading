Title: My First $100K Trading Loss #trading #tradingpsychology #stocks
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=ZWGWia4_Djg
======================================================================

I was starting to be consistently profitable and starting to even become hopeful. It was a normal quiet day at work when Allergan started ripping higher into the close. It felt like it was just some order flow into the closing imbalance. A move likely to revert back lower. I started to short into the close with the intention of holding a small position overnight. The market closes and then not long after the stock just absolutely explodes higher in the afterhours. An unfathomable move for a huge large cap pharma company. What was driving it? The news of a hostile takeover by Bill Ackman. Even by the time I had the headline, the stock was already up so much by the time I could even process it. I ended up losing over a hundred thousand dollars when my daily risk limit was something like five grand. A great month for me at the time was maybe 20K. That is months of work gone. All of sudden you start questioning, will it take me six months to get out of the drawdown? A year? Or maybe never? How long will it take for me to get a bonus check again? Is it even worth staying? My whole trading group got banged up in this trade and I remember speaking to my boss once the dust settled. The combo was in some unwavering display of confidence that I would get over this and survive the drawdown. He did his best to comfort me but ultimately it ended with him saying, "Look, I have no idea what's going to end up happening. Hopefully one day you look back on this in the rearview mirror and it was just a speed bump." Obviously we know now how the story ended but it's hard to impress upon you just how scary that was at the time.
