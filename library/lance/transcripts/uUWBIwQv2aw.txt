Title: One of the biggest realizations I’ve had after mentoring traders for over a decade 💡
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=uUWBIwQv2aw
======================================================================

One of the biggest realizations I've had after mentoring traders for over a decade is this. Mentorship is incredibly powerful, but only when the student is truly ready to be helped. A great mentor is probably [music] the closest thing that exists to a cheat code in any performance-based field. Why? Because good mentors don't just give information. They teach you how to think, how to improve, and how to avoid wasting years going down dead-end paths. They compress the learning curve. The right mentor can save someone years of unnecessary pain and inefficiency. But, here's the truth that even I struggled to accept for a very long time. Many people don't actually want to be helped, at least not fully. What they really want is to succeed their way while protecting their ego. So, they selectively listen, ignore feedback, and refuse accountability. They fight the process, then they blame the mentor, the market, or everybody else when they fail. And for years, I took this personally. I would spend thousands of hours trying to push people towards success harder than they were willing to push themselves. [music] That never works. Eventually, you realize something incredibly important. You can only help people who fully buy in and genuinely want to change. The mentee [music] has to care more than the mentor, always. And here's another reality that people ignore. Good mentorship must be reciprocal. It doesn't always have to be financial, but there has to be some form of fair exchange. Effort, gratitude, helpfulness, commitment, because without reciprocity, the relationship eventually breaks down. The best mentorships are partnerships, not one-sided extractions. And this applies far beyond trading. Follow me for more trading lessons and life lessons.
