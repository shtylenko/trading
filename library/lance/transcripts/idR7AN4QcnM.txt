Here is the basic analogy for beginners. If I want to buy a TV outside regular business hours, my options are going to be far slimmer or I might need to pay a higher price for a store willing to sell me the TV outside of normal business hours. Same goes for stocks. That means at a minimum, it is going to be more expensive getting in and out of positions and also that price can move a lot on very little volume. In other words, there's less liquidity. Especially if you're trying to trade large size, it might take a lot of incentive to find someone to transact with you. That incentive comes in the form of favorable prices for them and less favorable prices for you. Additionally, due to the thinner order book, you will need to be far more careful about using stop orders and market orders. If you use a stop limit order, you will need to queue the order much more aggressively through the trigger price if you want the fill. I would urge you generally not to use market orders at all during any of these sessions. One of the biggest mistakes newer traders make is not accounting for that increased leverage before taking a trade.
