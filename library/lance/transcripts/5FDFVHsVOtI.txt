Now, let's get into two critical advanced tips. These took me years and years to learn and internalize, paid for by a lot of tuition to the markets. The first is that the lower time frames are the most important for your trading decisions. If you are an intraday trader, the intraday chart will always be more important than the daily chart. If I had to approximate it for my intraday trades, the intraday chart gets an 80% waiting for my decisions. Even if the daily chart is screaming short, if the stock is steadily grinding on the intraday chart, I will almost never fight that. Similarly, if a chart has a mediocre daily chart but an amazing intraday chart, I will still likely take the trade. That is because the lower time frame wins and importance. It speaks more to the price action in this specific moment in time. My second huge tip that I learned as I expanded my playbook to incorporate swing trading. If you place a trade due to the intraday chart, you often want to be using a stop on the intraday chart. If you place a trade due to the daily chart, you often want to be using a stop on the daily chart. In other words, I want my stop and system to be relevant to the time frame I'm placing the trade on. For example, take this breakout in ION Q above $50 or so. If I get long on the breakout due to that beautiful daily consolidation, my initial stop will be the lows of the daily bar. Then for my simple system, I'm going to use a trailing stop of prior bar lows, which keeps me in the trade until about $71 or so. Notice how I am not giving it some tight intraday stop because it is a daily chart trade. Trust me, those two lessons alone are worth their weight in gold.
