One in four traders said psychology is their number one obstacle. That's huge. Some of the issues mentioned included hesitation pulling the trigger, hesitating when it's time to size up, cutting winners too early, letting losers run, or just plain tilt trading after a bad beat. Because these are different issues, I'm going to break some of them down separately. The bulk of these issues, while they seem psychological in nature, they really stem from not having done a lot of the hard work building your trading system. I can tell that a lot of these traders don't have a playbook and they haven't spent the time to predefine their entries, their exits, and their sizing. The reason why most people are hesitating or freezing up is because they're relying on too much subjectivity in the moment. A good trading playbook has you list out your plays. It includes all the qualities you look for in a stock. It has your entry rules. It has your exit rules, and it has your sizing rules. Trading in real-time has so much information being thrown at us all at once. It's easily overwhelming. That is why you want to reduce those decisions as much as possible. You want predefined game plans. If X happens, I will do Y. On this type of setup, I'll risk $100. On that type of setup, I'll risk $200. Build the system and rules beforehand so that you reduce as much of the decision-making in the moment as possible.
