If you read one book in 2026, make it this one. Atomic Habits by James Clear. This is my Bible and the book that resonated with everything I learned on my own. This book is the best framework for how to turn daily actions into success. The core philosophy is about how focusing on the process and making consistent, effective 1% improvements compounds into huge progress over time. Success is the product of daily habits, not once-ina-lifetime transformations. You do not rise to the level of your goals. you fall to the level of your systems. For that reason alone, this book is required reading at every trading firm I've ever worked with, and I've gifted literally hundreds of these copies. You're welcome, James Clear. And PS, you still owe me lunch. Most traders obsess over their goals or their daily P&L rather than focusing on the daily actions they can take to get to where they want to be. Another critical concept from the book that applies to beginner traders or really any trader working on expanding their skill set is the plateau of latent potential. Most traders quit in their first year because they don't see immediate results. You might be doing everything right, studying charts, journaling, managing risk, but your P&L remains flat. This is not necessarily failure. It's latent potential being stored. James Clear uses the analogy of a boiling pot of water. For a long time, you do not see any progress, but then all at once, it all starts to happen. This was my experience breaking out as a trader. For almost 2 years, I saw minimal progress. Then all those small changes over time finally started to click together and result in what appeared to be sudden sharp improvement.
