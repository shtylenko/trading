There used to be a trader I knew who we nicknamed Wreck-It Ralph. It would start with the mumbling. A few minutes later, it would escalate to the desk pound. Then, you knew it was coming. Not long after that, you'd get the keyboard slam, and traders around him would be showered by keys. The best way to prevent being in a state of tilt or rage like that when trading is to take proactive and preventative steps before you reach level 10 keyboard slams. Reflect on what your triggers tend to be. Find ways to remove those triggers from your environment. And identify the first signs of when you start to lose control. A lot of that requires training your introspection and self-awareness abilities. If needed, step away from the desk and get outside. But Jonathan cautions, "No returning back to your seat if you haven't resolved your mental state, or else you'll be right back to where you started." How have I applied this rule? First, I've become aware of my tells. If I ever start cursing to myself, or if I ever start muttering, "Who the [ __ ] is on the other side of this trade?" Those are [clears throat] signs I'm starting to lose my cool. Normally, I'd keep on trading and keep fighting until I really lose it, and the mistake then cost me big P&L. When I notice those tells or notice my internal dialogue is getting frustrated, that's when I make sure I step away from the screen and get outside before it escalates beyond the point of no return.
