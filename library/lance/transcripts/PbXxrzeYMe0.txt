Forex sucks. But if I'm going to say that on the internet, I owe you the real explanation. Number of opportunities. On paper, it's always moving. It's 24 hours, 5 days a week. But most of that movement is total noise and unpredictable chop. The currency markets don't move much. And before all the Forex bros say that the lack of movement isn't a big deal because Forex markets provide easy leverage, you need to understand that leverage does not make up for that. Leverage does not create edge. It just scales whatever edge you already have. In an efficient market, your edge is basically zero after cost. Meaning your expected value is about zero before fees and usually negative after fees. So if your expectancy is negative, leverage just makes you lose faster. Now for liquidity and scalability in Forex, it's massive. Forex is the largest market in the world, estimated at about $9.6 trillion per day in trading volume. That sounds like a dream until you realize what it implies. The competition is unreal. As far as this quality of competition, it's the killer. Forex is dominated by banks, dealers, macro funds, and systematic players. You aren't trading against someone who thinks their stock went 10x on a reverse stock split. In forex, if you aren't a pro, you're easy exit liquidity. Like I mentioned, the leverage allowed here is incredibly high. If you have edge in forex, and that's a big if, that's amazing because you really can scale your bets massively given the leverage and liquidity. Fees. Forex fees tend to be very cheap in that regard. Now, broker safety. This is mixed. In top tier jurisdictions, there's regulation and risk disclosure, but globally, retail forex is also where people wander into the wild west. So, Forex ends up being extremely scalable, but extremely efficient with extremely high quality competition. That is a bad combo for most discretionary traders.
