This one's a killer. Most traders want the quick fix, the secret indicator, the golden setup, the one perfect YouTube video that changes everything. But trading does not work like that. The only way to improve is to do the hard and sexy work, studying thousands of charts, testing different strategies, tracking your stats, reviewing your bad trades, asking yourself the hard questions. What was I thinking emotionally here? Why did I ignore my stop? What pattern was I hoping for? And was it even there? This kind of reflection, this kind of deep work, it's what separates real traders from hobbyists. One of the best ways to do this is by creating a daily report card to track your progress, but most people would rather download some Reddit indicator than take an honest reflection of what the hard work they need to improve is. And that's exactly why they fail. So yeah, 95% of traders fail, but it's not because trading is impossible. It's because most people never even give themselves a fair shot. They quit too soon. They don't know why they're doing it, and they're not willing to grind through the hard work that actually leads to mastery. If you can fix just those three things, your expectations, your why, and your work ethic, you'll be more informed than most that fullheartedly attempt to tackle this profession. Armed with better information, that is how we can beat the odds and be the 5% of traders who succeed.
