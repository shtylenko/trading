At a high level, trading comes down to two main approaches. One approach is continuation, which means you're going with the trend and expecting the stock to make another move in the direction of the bigger picture trend. The second approach is mean reversion or betting on a counter trend during which the stock will retrace a portion of the bigger picture trend. And here's the rule that can completely transform your trading. First, when there is fresh breaking news, I want to be biased towards being with the trend. Second, when a stock makes a big sharp move on no news, I want to be biased towards the possibility of mean reversion. That's it. That one filter can flip you from losing money to actually having edge.
