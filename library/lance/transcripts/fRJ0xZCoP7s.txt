URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=fRJ0xZCoP7s

One of the biggest reasons traders lose money is because they outsource their thinking to the mainstream narrative. The news says the economy is strong. The analysts say earnings were bad. Twitter says the market is doomed. Reddit says a stock is obviously going higher. And meanwhile, price is telling an entirely different story. This is one of the hardest lessons in trading. The narrative is often lagging. Price [music] action is the only truth. Markets are forward-looking discounting mechanisms. So, by the time a story becomes consensus, the move is often already over. That's why so many traders get trapped trying to rationalize what should happen instead of simply reacting to what is happening. The best traders I know are not married to opinions. They listen to price. If a stock keeps absorbing bad news and refuses to [music] go down, that matters. If a stock has great news and still can't rally, that matters, too. [music] Price action is the ultimate truth because it reflects real positioning, real supply and demand, and real money at risk. Narratives can help provide context, but price pays.
