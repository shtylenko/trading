The number one value traders provide price discovery. At its essence, markets reward traders for correctly betting on the correct value of a security. Essentially, they are compensated for casting their vote weighed by the dollar amount of the bet in a way that is proven right over time. That is important because capital markets allow capital to flow to its most productive uses. When companies, governments, and individuals can access efficient debt and equity markets, it allows productive businesses to grow. Apple, Nike, hospitals, schools, municipalities, and governments all need efficient capital markets to function. Traders help decide what the best riskadjusted bet is for society and in turn get compensated for that. Warren Buffett bought companies that increased massively in value. He cast his vote by buying stock.
