# Contrarian Swing Trading (Algo & Ego) — Bob Desmond
## The Contrarian Trader — 13 Years Track Record

### Overview
Bob Desmond (The Contrarian Trader) identifies **disconnects between institutional algorithm behavior and fundamental reality.** When sentiment reaches extremes (panic selling or euphoric buying), contrarian traders go the opposite way. Documented 226-trade track record.

### The "Algo and Ego" Framework
**Algo:** Institutional algorithmic trading dominates intraday moves. Algorithms feed on:
- Fixed technical levels (VWAP, moving averages, round numbers)
- Order flow and liquidity
- Momentum and trend

**Ego:** When algorithms create mispricing (overreacting to news, stop-running), the contrarian steps in.

### Key Sentiment Indicators
- **Put/Call Ratio:** Extreme levels (>1.2 = bullish contrarian, <0.6 = bearish)
- **VIX:** Spikes above 30-35 = fear = potential bottom
- **Fear & Greed Index:** <20 = extreme fear = buy, >80 = extreme greed = be cautious
- **AAII Sentiment Survey:** Extreme bearish = buy signal
- **Bullish Percent Index:** Below 30% = oversold

### Entry Rules
1. Identify an extreme sentiment reading (VIX spike, fear index)
2. Wait for stabilization (price stops making new lows)
3. Look for **institutional volume** returning (confirmation)
4. Enter when price closes above the panic day's high
5. Use wider stops (contrarian trades can be early)

### Stop Loss
- **Type:** Structural (below the panic low)
- **Distance:** 5-10% (wider than momentum strategies)
- **Adjustment:** Tighten once thesis is confirmed

### Position Sizing
- **Start small:** 25% of intended size on initial signal
- **Add on confirmation:** 50% after first target hit
- **Complete position:** 100% on significant follow-through

### Profit Targets
- **Target 1:** Move back to fair value (prior support, VWAP, mean)
- **Target 2:** Move back to pre-panic levels
- **Target 3:** Potential trend extension if fundamentals support it

### Weekly Preparation
- Sunday: Review weekly chart structure
- Identify key levels (support, resistance, where stops cluster)
- Check sentiment indicators
- Plan trades for the week

### Adapting for $25-100k Account
- Trade mid-cap liquid stocks and ETFs (SPY, QQQ)
- Contrarian signals on indices work well: buy SPY when VIX peaks
- Use limit orders to enter at extreme levels (not market orders)
- 2-4 contrarian positions maximum

### Common Mistakes
1. Being contrarian too early (markets can stay irrational)
2. Not waiting for stabilization (catching falling knife)
3. Using tight stops on contrarian trades (gets stopped out)
4. Being contrarian in strong trending markets (just go with the trend)
