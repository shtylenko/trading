This is the trade that takes me over $20 million on the year. The trade that cements my name in the trillium record books. I shorted some stock as it was still going higher. Given the size of the move already seemed so extended, we exploded from around $180 or so all the way to $385, a whopping 115% move. But then once we finally turned lower, we broke prior bar lows on the intraday chart. It was time to strike. So I went big. Too big. I wasn't just trading the ticker. I was trading my P&L and my aspirations. I let the gold dictate the size, not the edge. And even though the chart was stretched beyond belief, and even though I had every reason to believe a snapback was coming, I put on a position size that left me way too exposed if the trade didn't work immediately. I had no buffer, no breathing room. At first, it looked like I timed it right. The stock started to fall. I began building in, working into a huge position. My P&L rocketed green and I was up hundreds of thousands of dollars already. We even then halted for volatility after a sharp move lower, often a good indicator that the top was in. And I thought I was going to reach Valhalla. But a greater reversal never came. Instead, it consolidated not far from highs. And then it reignited for a second leg. I had no choice but to cut my losses and take my stop at the highs of $385. Just like that, in less than an hour, I locked in the biggest loss of my career. A huge sevenf figureure red number staring back at me. Over $2 million lost. I was definitely not going to set a new firm record now, but thank God I cut my losses when I did because the stock ended up soaring much higher all the way to $550 before coming back to verse.
