## 3. Entry Patterns — Complete Playbook

### 3.1 ACD / First Candle New High (The Flagship Entry)

This is THE core pattern, taught identically across 7+ multi-hour training series covering ~2 years of content.

**Setup (before entry):**
1. Stock is in a clear uptrend (confirmed by higher highs, higher lows)
2. Price has either consolidated (flat top) or pulled back (micro pullback)
3. Volume is above average, MACD is positive and expanding
4. Stock has breaking news catalyst

**Entry Trigger:**
- The first candle (on the 5-minute chart) to make a new high after the pullback or consolidation
- Enter IMMEDIATELY when price breaks the prior candle's high — do NOT wait for the candle to close

> *"The first candle to make a new high. That is my entry. The moment the price breaks that candle, I do not wait for the candle to close — the moment the price breaks that trigger."*

**Stop Loss:**
- The low of the pullback candle (NOT the entry candle)
- This is a fixed point: if price goes below that low, the pattern has failed

> *"My stop is the low of that pullback. It's as simple as that. Max loss, that's my entry."*

**Profit Target:**
- Primary: retest of the day's high
- Secondary: hold for extended move if momentum continues

**Exit:**
- First red candle that closes below the prior green candle's low
- Or when price reaches the day's high (at minimum)

**Confirmation Filters (all must be met):**
1. Volume on the entry candle must be above average
2. Volume bars on green candles must be > volume bars on red candles
3. MACD must be positive and expanding
4. Stock must have a clear news catalyst
5. Float must be below threshold

### 3.2 Micro Pullback Entry

A variant of the ACD pattern, used when the pullback is brief (1-2 candles):

1. Stock surges up on momentum
2. Brief 1-2 candle shallow pullback
3. Entry: first green candle that breaks above the pullback high
4. Stop: below the low of the pullback
5. Target: retest of day's high

### 3.3 Opening Range Breakout (ORB)

1. First 5-15 minutes after 9:30 AM establish the opening range
2. Entry when price breaks above the opening range high
3. Stop below the opening range low
4. Works best on high volume days

> *"During the opening range, volume starts to come into the market. After that opening range — around 10:30, 11 — it really cools down."*

### 3.4 Flat Top / Consolidation Breakout

1. Stock rallies, then moves sideways in a tight range
2. Entry when price breaks above the flat top of the consolidation
3. Stop below the consolidation low
4. Often produces explosive moves because the pause built up buying pressure

### 3.5 Breaking News Immediate Entry

1. Stock gaps up on breaking news pre-market
2. DO NOT chase the initial spike — wait for the first pullback
3. Entry on the first pullback after confirming the news catalyst
4. Stop below the pre-news level
5. Target: follow momentum while volume is strong

### 3.6 Short Squeeze Entry

1. Stock with high short interest starts accelerating
2. Entry on breakout above resistance
3. Target: take profits fast — squeezes reverse violently
4. Requires stronger volume confirmation than standard ACD

> *"Short sellers will see the initial pop as a great opportunity to take a short. If that stock then keeps ripping 200%, 300%, 400% higher, it will certainly squeeze out early short sellers."*

### 3.7 The "Washout Long" (His Favorite Setup)

> *"My two favorite strategies are a washout long after a catalyst-driven parabolic move and then sub-VWAP traps."*

A stock that spiked on news, pulled back sharply (washing out late buyers), then sets up for a second leg. Entry on the first candle making a new high after the washout.

## 4. Exit Strategies & the Bailout Philosophy

### The Breakout vs. Bailout Philosophy — Central Principle

> *"If I get into a trade, it's because I'm looking at a very specific pattern and I'm expecting almost immediate resolution."*

There is no "wait and see." There is no "hold through the pullback." There is:

- **Breakout (pattern resolves upward):** Hold, add, profit
- **Bailout (pattern does NOT resolve within 1-2 bars):** BAIL — exit immediately at small loss

### Why This Works

1. **Small losses are easy to recover from** — bailing at $0.10/shr loss vs. holding for $1.00/shr
2. **Pattern must confirm itself quickly** — if the setup is correct, it should work NOW
3. **Mental energy** — no agonizing over "should I stay or should I go"

### The Bailout Decision Tree

```
Enter on 5-min candle new high
  |
Candle closes green AND above the breakout level?
  +-- YES → Hold, look for add-on-next candle
  +-- NO  → Exit immediately (bailout)
       |
       Next candle:
         +-- Makes new high?  → Add size
         +-- Pulls back to VWAP? → Hold, if VWAP holds
              +-- Breaks VWAP? → Exit (bailout)
```

### Standard Exit Plan — Scale Out in Thirds

1. **Sell 1/3 at initial target** (1:1 reward-to-risk)
2. **Sell 1/3 on extended move** (when momentum continues through first target)
3. **Let 1/3 run** with a trailing stop

### Trailing Stop Rule

> *"I want to see the price move up at least 10 cents within the first minute of getting in. Because then, I can set my stop at break even and I have essentially a free trade."*

Trail by 10-20 cents depending on volatility.

### Final Exit

- First red candle that closes below the prior green candle's low → sell remaining

> *"If price starts going down and we start seeing a red candle forming, I don't have to wait for the candle to close. I'll see the current candlestick shape is red and I'll exit my position. I can always get back in if there's another setup."*

### The 1:1 Ratio Reality

> *"My average winners are about $1,300, average losers are about $1,300-$1,400. It's about a one-to-one ratio. But you can be profitable with a one-to-one ratio as long as your accuracy is higher than 50%."*

**This is critical:** He targets 2:1 reward-to-risk on each trade but averages ~1:1 in practice. The edge comes from win rate > 50%, not from R:R. The 2:1 target acts as a ceiling, not the mean.

### Exit Methods (In Order of Frequency)

1. **Immediate stop loss** (most common) — "30 cent max loss rule"
2. **Scaling out into strength** — take 1/3 at first target, 1/3 at next, trail the rest
3. **Profit target at known resistance** — identified from pre-market daily chart analysis
4. **Trailing stop** — once stock is up significantly, trail by 10-20 cents
5. **Time stop** — if stock hasn't moved within 30-60 minutes, exit: "If it's just sitting there, I'm out."

### When to Walk Away (Hard Rules)

1. After giving back > **20% of daily profit**
2. After **3 sizable losses in a row**
3. After **no trade opportunity in over an hour**
4. Once **daily green target is hit** — shut it down

## 5. Position Sizing & the Icebreaker Rule

### Position Sizing Formula

> *"Position Size = Maximum Dollar Risk ÷ Risk per Share"*

**Example from the $583 story:** Max loss $500, risk $0.10/share → 5,000 shares

### The Icebreaker Rule — The System That Produced the 76-Day Streak

From `n8aZGjWAgdE` (161K views):

> *"I cap my share size at 5,000 shares, which is a quarter of my full-size position, until I make my first $1,000 in profit."*

**The Complete Protocol:**

1. **Start each day at 25% of normal position size**
   - Main account: 5,000 shares instead of 20,000
   - Small account ($2K): 25-75 shares instead of 100-300

2. **Scale to full size only after earning first profit milestone**
   - Main account milestone: $1,000 in profit
   - Small account milestone: ~$50-200 (first winning trade)

3. **If the market is cold and you never hit the milestone → stay at 1/4 size ALL DAY**

4. **Micro-version for small accounts:**
   > *"On the first trade you make $50. Now you've got a little bit of a cushion. So now you increase your size on the next trade to about half size. And now on that next trade you make $100. Now you increase to full size."*

### Why It Works

- On cold days: you lose at 1/4 rate. Red days become small.
- On hot days: you quickly make the milestone, scale up, capture full upside
- The first-profit hurdle prevents scaling up early on choppy days
- Boosts confidence: knowing you can only lose small on bad days lets you trade aggressively on good days

> *"The result was that on the best days in the market, I would make maybe $1,000 on that first trade instead of $3,000 or $4,000. But what's more important is controlling risk on the cold days. So on the cold days, I start with quarter size. I never hit the thousand goal, so I never size up. I don't expose myself to high risk."*

## 6. Risk Management — Full Frame

### The 4-Layer Risk Stack

**Layer 1: Stock Selection** — "Trade the best, leave the rest." Quality stocks are the first line of defense.

**Layer 2: Fixed-Dollar Max Loss Per Trade** — NOT percentage-based. Set a dollar amount you will lose at most.

**Layer 3: Icebreaker Rule** — Start at 25%, scale up after first profit.

**Layer 4: Daily Loss Limit** — Hard stop:
- Small account ($2K): 10% = $200 max daily loss
- Main account: $5,000-$10,000 max daily loss

### Stop Distances (from Live Data)

| Scenario | Stop Distance | When Used |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Standard small account | $0.10-$0.30/shr | $2-$20 stocks |
| Standard main account | $0.20-$0.50/shr | $5-$50 stocks |
| Tight (low float, volatile) | $0.05-$0.10/shr | Hot market, fast movers |
| Wide (higher float, liquid) | $0.50-$1.00/shr | Higher-priced stocks |

### The 30-Cent Rule
Standard max loss for his main account: $0.30 per share. When position sizing, 30 cents is the default.

### Hard Rules (Never Violate)

- **Never average down** — adding to a losing position is the #1 way to blow up
- **Never chase a missed move**
- **Never trade outside your system**
- **Never hold through pullbacks** (bailout philosophy)
- **Never use leverage as a beginner**

> *"A lot of beginner traders do the exact opposite — they add to their losing positions. The stock starts dropping, and rather than just cut the loss, they add to the position to reduce their cost basis. That is the fastest way to lose all your money."*

### Pre-Trade Risk Checklist (from the 5-Step System)

1. Did I sleep well?
2. Am I emotionally centered and grounded?
3. How has my trading been in the last few weeks?
4. What's the market condition today? (hot/cold/neutral)
5. Does this stock meet all 5 pillars?
6. What's my stop distance? What's my max dollar loss?
7. Is the reward-to-risk at least 2:1?

### The "Successful Red Day" Concept

> *"Successful red days are critical to long-term success as a trader. A successful red day is a day where you are red, however what's very important is that these are days where you followed the rules of your strategy. You did not exceed your max loss. You did not go rogue and become emotionally hijacked."*

> *"Success in trading is not just about accuracy and picking the right stocks. It's really about losing less."*

## 7. Market Condition Adaptation — Hot, Cold, Bear & Everything Between

### Hot Market

**Characteristics:**
- 5+ stocks up 50% or more daily
- Strong follow-through on breakouts
- High volume, FOMO carries B and C-quality setups
- Accuracy on A-setups: 75-80%
- Trades per day: 15-25+ on A-setups, 25-50 on B-setups
- Daily goal multiplier: 4-5x cold market

> *"In a hot market, buying sentiment and FOMO — the fear of missing out — are so high that B and even C quality setups can work."*

### Cold Market

**Characteristics:**
- Few stocks up 50%+ (often zero)
- Weak follow-through — even A-setups fail
- Lower volume, reduced liquidity
- Accuracy drops significantly
- Fewer quality setups — you'll run out before making your goal

> *"In a cold market, I can run out of quality setups. In a hot market on quality setups, my accuracy can be 75-80%. When it's really cold, even A-quality setups — my accuracy goes down because there's just not as much momentum."*

### The Cold Market Playbook

| Parameter | Hot Market | Cold Market |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Float threshold | < 20M shares | < 5M shares |
| Price focus | $2-$20 | $2-$4 |
| Position size | Full (after icebreaker) | 25% all day (icebreaker only) |
| Daily goal | $5K+ | $1K (1/5 of hot) |
| Trades per day | 15-25+ | 3-8 |
| Setup quality required | A, B, sometimes C | A ONLY |

### How to Gauge Sentiment (Step by Step)

1. Before market open, scan pre-market movers
2. Count how many stocks are up more than 50%
3. 5+ stocks up 50% → hot market — trade aggressively
4. Nothing cracking 50% → cold market — reduce size, be defensive
5. 1-2 stocks up 50% → neutral — moderate size, pick your spots

### The Bear Market Philosophy

From `trcKOZnoPPs` (46K views):

> *"I think of cold markets as training for the next hot market. It's kind of like the Olympics, except instead of every four years, the cycles are much tighter."*

His 2020 experience: market crashed (COVID) → unprecedented volume surge → his best year ever ($5M, 10x previous best). His take: major market dislocations create opportunity, but you need to survive the cold to be ready for the hot.

### Market Regimes He Identifies

| Regime | Characteristics | His Response |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| Strong Bull | Many gappers > 10%, broad market up | Full size, aggressive scaling |
| Choppy/Sideways | Gappers fade, patterns don't resolve | Reduce size, one entry/exit only |
| Bear/Gap Down | Market opens down, few gappers above 5% | Very small size or sit out |
| Low Volatility | Stocks barely move, scanners return few | Skip trading entirely |

## 8. VWAP Strategies — Complete Set

### 8.1 VWAP as Support (Trend Continuation)

**When it works:** Stock has been consistently trading ABOVE VWAP in an uptrend.

1. Stock rallies, then pulls back toward VWAP
2. VWAP acts as support — price bounces off it
3. Entry: on the candle that curls up from VWAP
4. Stop: below VWAP (typically $0.05-$0.10)
5. Target: retest of pre-pullback high

> *"The stock should always be priced well above VWAP when it's trending higher. It's very common that when we see a sharp pullback, we'll see the price bounce off the VWAP."*

### 8.2 Sub-VWAP Trap (His Favorite Intraday Setup)

From `92,304v` (dedicated episode):

> *"My favorite intraday setup would be a stock that is spiking up on momentum, making a strong extension on a catalyst, and then it washes out below VWAP. That sub-VWAP trap is where I want to be a buyer when it reclaims VWAP."*

**The Full Setup:**
1. Stock spikes on news, trades well above VWAP
2. It pulls back sharply, breaks BELOW VWAP
3. Late longs get stopped out (the "trap")
4. Price reverses sharply back above VWAP
5. **Entry:** When price reclaims VWAP with volume
6. **Stop:** Below the false-break low (the washout low)
7. **Target:** Retest of day's high

> *"The sub-VWAP trap occurs when the stock is trading below VWAP and typically the stock needs to have pre-existing momentum — it needs to have already shown it wants to go higher."*

### 8.3 VWAP as Resistance (Rare — Short Bias)

- Stock below VWAP, approaches it, fails to break through
- Used as a short entry (rare in his long-biased trading)

## 9. Technical Indicators & Chart Analysis

### Primary Chart Timeframes

| Timeframe | Primary Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| 5-minute | PRIMARY — ACD breakout entry, daily routine |
| 1-minute | Micro pullbacks, VWAP bounces, fine entry timing |
| Daily | Big picture context (support, resistance, trend) |

### MACD — Trend Filter Only

> *"Don't trade when the MACD is negative. We're trading against a headwind."*

- Only take longs when MACD is **positive and expanding**
- When MACD curls down toward negative → avoid new entries
- When MACD crosses into negative → stop trading that stock entirely
- MACD positive but price failing → volume imbalance may override the signal

### Volume Profile

- **Green volume > red volume** = healthy uptrend, take entries
- **Red volume > green volume** = distribution, avoid entries
- Light volume on red + heavy volume on green = best scenario
- Heavy volume on both sides = indecision, avoid

> *"If the volume is not there, the pattern is not there."*

### Candlestick Anatomy (from `3hmDWJfZ0_0` — 3.9M views)

Each candlestick communicates: Open, Close, High, Low.

**Bullish signals:**
- Strong green bodies — buyers dominated
- Bottoming tails / long lower wicks — buyers stepped in after dip
- Continuation candles — consistent green with higher closes

**Bearish signals:**
- Long upper wicks / topping tails — sellers rejected high price
- Large bearish (red) bodies — sellers dominated
- Doji (open = close) — indecision, potential reversal

### Candlestick Shapes Reference

| Shape | Name | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| Long green body | Bullish Marubozu | Strong buying, no resistance |
| Long red body | Bearish Marubozu | Strong selling, no support |
| Small body + long lower wick | Hammer | Buyers stepped in after dip |
| Small body + long upper wick | Shooting Star | Sellers rejected high price |
| Tiny body or cross | Doji | Indecision, potential reversal |
| Three green candles in row | Three White Soldiers | Strong continuation |
| Three red candles in row | Three Black Crows | Strong selling |

## 10. Scaling (Pyramiding) — The Complete 5-Step System

From `KzVbXzkoZkA` (347K views):

### Step 1: What Is Scaling?

> *"Scaling is a position management strategy designed to help maximize profits. A trader who does not scale will have one entry and one exit for each trade. A trader who scales will add several times until reaching full position, and then when they sell, they'll sell several times until they've closed the position."*

### Step 2: When to AVOID Scaling

**Never as a beginner:**
> *"Focus on first timing with one entry, one exit, getting consistency, getting confidence, and then add the scaling on top."*

**Also avoid when:**
- Market is cold
- The stock has low liquidity
- Price action is choppy and unpredictable

### Step 3: When to Scale

- The market is hot
- The stock has high volume and liquidity
- You have proven consistency on single-entry trades
- The pattern shows strong follow-through

### Step 4: How to Scale In (Adding to Winners)

**The averaging-up method (NEVER average down):**

1. **Starter position** — 1/3 of full size at initial breakout entry
2. **Add 1/3 on confirmation** — when first candle closes above entry
3. **Add final 1/3 on follow-through** — when momentum continues

> *"You could continue to scale in. So now your average is $6.15. Let's say you add again at $6.65 and you double your position. Once again, now your average is going up."*

**Ongoing example from the scaling math:**

> *"A non-scaling trader enters 1,000 shares at $9.50. Stop at $9.40. Risk $100. Exit at $10.00. Profit: $500. A scaling trader enters 1,000 at $9.50. Adds 1,000 at $10.00 (now up 50 cents on first 1,000). Average cost basis: $9.75. Holding 2,000 shares. If the stock goes to $10.50, profit: $1,500. Same initial risk ($100), but 3x the profit."*

### Step 5: How to Scale Out

1. Sell 1/3 at initial target (1:1 reward-to-risk)
2. Sell 1/3 on extended move
3. Let 1/3 run with trailing stop

> *"I want to scale out slowly on the ask to get the best price. Because I'm still holding a core position, I don't want to dump it all at once."*

## 20. Edge Cases, Resolved Contradictions & Nuances

### 20.1 Stop Loss Placement Conflicts

| Source | Stop Rule |
|--------|-----------|
| "Timing Entries & Exits" | "Below the consolidation range" |
| "Scalp Trading" | "Below VWAP by a few cents" |
| Green Day Recap | "30 cent max loss — bail if down more than 30 cents" |
| Small Account Day 4 | "10-20 cents per share max loss" |
| Level 2 | "Below the hidden buyer's price level" |

**Resolution:** Stop depends on setup type and stock volatility. Common thread: SMALL, FIXED dollar amount based on share price and account size, NOT a percentage. Rule of thumb: 10-30 cents per share regardless of stock price.

### 20.2 How Many Trades Per Day?

| Context | Number of Trades |
|---------|-----------------|
| Small account challenge | 1 per day (one round-trip) |
| Main account live streams | 5-20+ (scaling entries + exits) |
| Red day recaps | 2-5 |
| Typical green day recap | 3-8 round trips |

**Resolution:** "One trade" in small account = one round trip. Main account has many more due to scaling. Varies wildly by market conditions.

### 20.3 Does He Use Stop Loss Orders?

He uses **mental stops**, not hard stop-loss orders. He watches the tape and manually exits when his mental stop is hit. Occasionally uses hard stops for very large positions.

### 20.4 Win Rate

He implies ~55-65% based on the 1:1 P/L ratio discussion: "You can be profitable with a 1:1 ratio as long as accuracy is above 50%." Some days 100% win rate, some days 0%.

### 20.5 Average Loser Size

The $1,300-$1,400 figure is the AVERAGE, which includes both small ($100) and large ($5,000+) losses. In recaps he frequently shows losses of $19, $30-$50, $300-$1,000.

### 20.6 News vs. Technicals

**Both required:**
- News for the gap — without it, the move is unreliable
- Technicals for timing — without it, you have no entry/exit plan

### 20.7 2:1 Target vs. 1:1 Reality

**What he teaches:** Target at least 2:1 reward-to-risk.
**What his numbers show:** ~1:1 realized average.

**Resolution:** The 2:1 rule is the IDEAL per trade. But because not every trade reaches full target, he scales out, and he bails quickly on losers, the blended average settles at ~1:1. The edge comes from win rate > 50%, not from R:R.

### 20.8 1-Minute vs. 5-Minute Chart

**Training says:** Use 1-minute chart.
**Live trading shows:** Primarily 5-minute for ACD entry, 1-minute for fine entries.

**Resolution:** Both used. 5-minute is PRIMARY. 1-minute for micro pullbacks and scalping. Beginners should start with 5-minute.
