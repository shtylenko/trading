Here's how to get ahead of 99% of traders in 2026. Step one, learn what EV is. No, it's not your Tesla, it's expected value. You can know a trade is good if the average outcome over many repetitions is positive, not because one single trade was a winner. Step two, set the setup. You want to write down your entry, stop, and target before the trade so you can measure win rate, average win, and average loss over a data set. Step three, this is where you do the back testing. Gather past charts that match your setup, log every win or loss in a spreadsheet and calculate whether the strategy would have made money or not. Step four, if seemingly profitable, you then want to do some forward testing. If you can't find past charts, live trade in a demo going forward to see if the setup behaves the same in real time. Then you want to update your win rate and average reward as you go. Step five, assuming all this proves profitable, you want to start executing the trade and size it based on expected value. You want to size up only when the setup shows a strong positive expected value and cut the trade instantly if the reward, win rate or conditions start going down.
