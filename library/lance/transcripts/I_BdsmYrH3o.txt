In trading, stocks might move based on earnings reports or tariff headlines or news of a big AI deal. This leads to discrete jumps in price. And if you're prepared, focused, and fast, that is where great money can be made. The exact same thing happens in sports gambling and prediction markets. A star player gets injured or a news headline comes out affecting an election and markets need to reprice quickly. In fact, there are also algos in all of these markets that the second a bet is a lock, they try to be as fast as possible to price that in and get the final meat on the bone. The same way that when a cash buyout deal gets approved, you might have seconds before that stock trades at the perfect price. Similarly, with sports betting, when the outcome of the game is decided, or with prediction markets, when the outcome of the contract is determined, algos are capturing those mispricings and taking money from slower market participants. Same mechanics, same game, different interface.
