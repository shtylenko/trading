You're not bad at trading. You've just never been taught how to think like a trader. Here's the one concept that has helped me take trades like this, this, and this. Step one, the 20% heristic. Treat every assumption such as win rate, reward, or risk as 20% worse than you think. This way, you only take trades that are still strong even after the cut. Step two, filter out marginal plays. Remove every setup that becomes weak after that cut. This forces you to only act on clearly favorable trades and it makes you much more selective. Step [snorts] three, build better feedback loops. Use a 20% buffer each day to trade less, keep your data clean, and avoid getting overconfident. This helps you build a strong, consistent trading routine.
