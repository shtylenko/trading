There are two traditional ways to play a trend line and it mimics the way we play support and resistance lines. You can either buy ahead of an uptrend line or short the break of it much like we do for a support line. Similarly, you can short ahead of a downtrend line or buy the break of it much like we do for a resistance line. Now, let's also dismiss one of the biggest traditional technical analysis myths. A lot of traders traditionally believe that the more times a trend line is touched, the stronger it becomes. That's half right. The more touches make it more significant as it becomes more apparent and more and more traders might use it as a stop over time. That being said, the more times that the trend line gets touched and price can't push away, the more likely it is to break.
