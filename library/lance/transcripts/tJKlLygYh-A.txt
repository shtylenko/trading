Best traders don't just manage risk, they manage themselves and their mindset. And that $2 million loss, as painful as it was, forced me to relearn one of the oldest trading lessons of all time. You cannot impose your will on the market. The market does not care about your P&L goals. It does not care about the heroic finish you want. It is unattached, emotionless. Do I regret the trade? No. I would take that setup again, no questions asked. But I do regret the motivation behind it and my sizing. I let an external milestone override my internal discipline. The setup was without a doubt worth trading, but never for that size. I obviously didn't break the firm record that day, but I did break the dangerous mindset that had been creeping into my trading. I wrongfully thought that because I'm experienced and have succeeded with this trade time and time again that I could ignore some of the sizing rules in my trading playbook that I've spent a decade building and the lessons that got me here. Lessons like stay humble, stay emotionless. You can never force the market to your will. It doesn't matter if you have a rent payment coming up. It doesn't matter if you want a new car.
