Let me give you a few practical examples of such rules I've used or seen implemented on the trading floor. If a stock is still trading within the range, any trades I take will be small size and at max I want to give it two attempts. That seems like a super reasonable rule and way to adapt some of this. Another few ideas can be, I'm not allowed to short a stock after a large down leg unless it's consolidated for more than 30 minutes and then breaks to intraday lows. Or I will avoid all mid-range continuation plays unless we break to fresh intraday highs. Trust me, no matter how good you think you are, this concept applies to you. Regardless of skill level or style, understanding this concept is crucial because it helps us identify another key variable in our trading, whether a stock is still in a range or breaking to new prices. Almost all the traders I've worked with don't differentiate their trading enough based on this concept. Once you actually take the time to look over your trading and reflect, it is almost universal that somewhere you find yourself blind to these nuances with massive room for improvement.
