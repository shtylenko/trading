Title: MY STORY: Why I almost quit trading #trading #tradingpsychology #tradingmotivation
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=cEFC22R8q2M
======================================================================

Nobody knows how incredibly close I came to quitting trading. >> What? >> This was before making over hund00 million on Wall Street. The first two years of my career, I had all the benefits of working for a top trading firm. Yet, I could not make a single dollar. Now, [snorts] over 15 years ago, I was at Indiana University studying finance, and I had absolutely zero background in trading. Going into my sophomore year, I started reading about the stock market, and I found it interesting. From there, I read the Market Wizard series by Jack Schwagger, a series of books that interview some of the top traders on Wall Street. I found it absolutely fascinating how these traders were able to beat the market using all sorts of different skill sets. From that moment on, I consumed as much information as possible about the markets and applied to every job on Wall Street that I could find. I turned down a $100,000 plus job offer to day trade stocks with a nearly non-existent salary. What I did my research enough to know that most traders fail and I had very reasonable expectations going into the job. I also knew that for those that do succeed, some succeed in a very big way. By the end of the first year, I was towards the back of my trading class. Not a glimmer of hope in sight. Not only was I not making money, but I lost money for the firm and would need to make that money back before ever getting a bonus check. I was worse off than where I even started. It was about 18 months in when I then began applying for other jobs. It wasn't that I didn't love trading. I absolutely did. There was nothing more that I wanted than to succeed at the job. But I also knew I had to face reality. I might not have what it takes. Fortunately for me, I was too dumb for the top quantitative firm, Dee Shaw. Fun fact, that's where Jeff Bezos started his career. I was also too dumb for a few other firms. So, I would get beat up by the market during the day and then I would receive rejection emails from the firms I was interviewing at by night. I was debating whether to quit the job so I could better use my time to network and apply to other jobs. I was too afraid to have the conversation with my boss, so I spoke privately with the second highest up in our group. In our conversation, [music] he urged me to give it more time and that if I didn't, there would be a strong chance I'd end up regretting it. Over time, I started to make marginally better and better decisions. I'd avoid a loss I'd otherwise have taken or connect on a trade for better size than I used to. And sure enough, the wins started to outweigh the losses. The P&L started to roll in. Before long, I had lifetime positive net P&L. And then, miraculously, we walked [music] into a hot IPO market. Towards the end of year 2, I managed to finally hit my 100K net P&L milestone, which at Trillium, in the eyes of [music] the firm, is how you graduate from being a rookie.
