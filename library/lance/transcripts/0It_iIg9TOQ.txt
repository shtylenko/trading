Title: 7 Steps to Trade Full Time #trading #fulltimetrader #quit9to5
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=0It_iIg9TOQ
======================================================================

So, you want to quit your job and start trading full time, but if you don't do it right, you'll be broke and begging for your job back in 6 months. Step one, understand what quitting your 9-5 actually means. You're not just replacing income, you're replacing structure, benefits, and stability. Trading full time is running a performance business. Goodbye salary and bi-weekly paycheck. If you're chasing freedom, make sure you understand the weight that comes with it. Step two, build your knowledge and try to develop your trading skills as much as possible while you're still employed. Try to get as far ahead on the learning curve before taking the leap. Take your trading as seriously as possible. Block off the same time each week and treat it as if it was a second job. Make it fit into your schedule the best you can. Future you will thank you. Step three, stress test your data. How much runway do you have? Calculate your monthly expenses, minimum required trading income, your average monthly P&L with worst case drawdown. Then stress test it. What if you don't make money for 3 to 6 months? What if you don't make a dime for the first year? Both of which I think are very reasonable, mind you. Step four, transition smart. Don't rip the cord. Ideally, go part-time before going full-time. Also, don't make any rash fast decisions. Have you been passionate about this for at least a year or multiple years? Is your interest increasing? You want to make sure this isn't a passing fad because once you get in the trenches, it doesn't get any easier. Step five, develop real trader habits before you quit. Daily review, journaling, constant iteration, build emotional discipline because that paycheck safety net is gone. Step six, prepare for the psychology shift after you quit. Income isn't guaranteed anymore. It's earned through execution. Pressure to perform and external pressures can hurt performance if you're not ready for it. Trading is hard enough even when you don't have a mortgage, a family, and a lender breathing down your back. The second those outside pressures start impacting your trading decisions, your psychology and ability to trade becomes toast. Step seven, don't be scared to make the jump if you aren't yet profitable. Most traders aren't profitable when they take the leap. Trying to find profitability while still working a different job is a tough task. I find trading is one of those jobs where once you are full-time and can be all-in, you don't just learn and grow a little bit faster, you grow magnitudes faster. It's the same as that golf analogy. It's probably impossible to ever go pro if you only practice a few hours each week. The difference in progress between full-time pursuit and part-time hobby is incomparable. So, don't think just because you aren't profitable now, doesn't mean you won't find profitability.
