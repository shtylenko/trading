I had built a response to check the markets the moment I woke up. Doing this created a habit response where my mind would immediately be stimulated upon waking. After a decade plus of doing that, it was severely impacting the depth of my sleep, the length of my sleep, and overall quality of sleep I was getting. Once I was awake, if I got that mental stimulation by checking the markets, I could never go back to sleep. I'd always wake up at 6:00 or 6:30 a.m. regardless of what time I went to bed. to add, I've never been a napper, so that was becoming a big problem. So, what did I do? I implemented some new best practices, and my sleep score has been up 15% in counting since then. I've been able to slowly override and reverse those bad habits over time. To save you the time and money, I'll share these best practices. Here's what I consider the non-negotiables of sleep if they're within your control. Number one, every single night, you want a cold, dark, and quiet room. Make it as similar to sleeping in a deep cave as possible, probably sub68 degrees, and to reiterate, very dark. What they say is you don't want to be able to clearly see your hand in front of your face. Number two, quiet. You don't want any noise waking you. If you live in a noisy cityscape environment, many people recommend a white noise machine because that tends to block that out from your brain. Then comes number three, zero alcohol. I know a lot of people love a glass of wine with dinner or maybe a beer after work, but truly zero alcohol is the optimal amount for sleep. If you do happen to drink, the earlier before bed, the better. But keep in mind, every drink is incredibly damaging across all your sleep metrics. I tend to see a 10% hit just from one or two drinks around dinner time. Number four, no caffeine after 1:00 p.m. Sorry, coffee lovers. Even if you're able to fall asleep, caffeine late on the day impairs the quality and depth of sleep. Number five, a daily exercise practice is so important, as is morning sunlight as soon as you wake up or going on a walk or doing some stretching. Dr. Andrew Huberman has touched on the topic of morning sunlight if you are interested in reading up more on that. Number six, one of the other big non-negotiables, no cell phone, computer, or stressful items in the bedroom. That was one of the biggest changes for me. You are trying to take anything stimulating out of the bedroom and make the bedroom for sleeping only and maybe some sex. That's fine, too. Mostly because we're traders, not monks.
