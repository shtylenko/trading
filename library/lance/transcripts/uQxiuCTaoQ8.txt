You know, trading is tough, right? And you're going to have you're gonna have good weeks, bad weeks, good months, bad months, good years, bad years, even perhaps. And that is part of trading. That's part of the profession. And so accepting that and then allowing yourself to be disappointed is fine because we're all disappointed with different things that happen in our lives and we can manage disappointment pretty well. What you don't want to do as a trader is allow that disappointment to devolve down into discouragement. Big difference between being disappointed and getting discouraged. And here's why. If you think about trading, you want it to be a cognitive activity, not an emotional activity. Being disappointed is cognitive. Being discouraged is emotional. And it's very hard to recover from discouragement as opposed to recover from disappointment.
