The second major use case is continuation plays. This is where opening range breaks become absolutely deadly in strong momentum environments, especially on day one after big catalyst like earnings, FDA approvals, major guidance, or AI announcements. Really any truly material catalyst. This is the type of stuff traders like Christian Kwalemagi have mastered through concepts like episodic pivots. A stock reports major news, and institutions suddenly need exposure. The estimates re-rate and price discovery begins anew. The key thing beginners fail to appreciate is that institutional money often cannot fully position immediately. Large funds scale into positions over days or weeks. So, the opening range essentially becomes a launch pad. The stock gaps on earnings, it consolidates during the opening range while buyers and sellers battle. Then, once that opening range breaks higher, momentum ignites because institutions continue deploying capital, and shorts start realizing they were trapped against the real catalyst. This happened recently in Sandisk. Sandisk reported earnings after hours on April 30th. The first day of trading following earnings, we were gapping down after what had been a massive run higher in the stock. After forming an initial opening range and attempting a leg lower, we ended up resolving higher. And that break higher was the entry to catch a move that was then off to the races.
