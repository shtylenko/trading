Regardless of experience level, I think many traders wonder how can we get over the fear of loss or losing money. I love this question because it highlights such an important concept which is prospect theory. I love behavioral finance and prospect theory is a core tenant in that our losses feel so much worse than our gains. Any trader knows that's the epitome of trading. So how do we get over this? So I think the first step is recognizing that bias. We are human and we are uh kind of at the will of our just feeble human psychology. But if we know about this bias, we can do our best to also focus on the gain. It's not just what we might lose, but how much might we make in the trade. And traders that are paralyzed by fear of loss are often underemphasizing and underfocusing on that gain. The other thing we can do is make sure that any amount we are risking is an amount we are willing to lose. As long as we do that over the long run, we need to recognize that we will capture positive expected value if we place our bets in the right spot. And the only way to make money over the long run is by being willing to accept those losses.
