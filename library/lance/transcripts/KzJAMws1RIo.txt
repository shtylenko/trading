If you're such a great trader who's made millions, why do you sell a course? You're right to be skeptical and to ask the question because there really is so much out there. Fake gurus selling people on a fake dream while using trading courses to pay for their green lambos. It's true. Selling a trading course or promoting affiliate links is infinitely easier than making money trading. Part of why I created trading course is to have something authentic out there. But the larger answer is twofold. And I promise the second reason is not what you're going to expect. First, I'm good at trading. Like really, really good at it. I spent years of my career running a trading floor for Trillium, training and building six and seven figure P&L per year traders. I continue to mentor dozens of traders, including those at SMB Capital. Suffice to say, unlike all the grifters, I have the credentials and the receipts to actually be teaching this stuff. Most importantly, I enjoy doing that mentorship work. I enjoy teaching. I enjoy sharing the knowledge I spent so many years accumulating. That is why years ago I started freely sharing my trading knowledge on X and on YouTube. I eventually started a Patreon for some of my more timeintensive content. 100% of the proceeds went to charity. The whole model was fun to do and the users benefited from it. 96% of members said the content positively impacted their trading. Those same Patreon members told me I should create a product out of the content I was building. This product isn't some sham course like the rest of them. I spent thousands of hours and hundreds of thousands of dollars to create my course and fill it with real knowledge. There you have reason one. I have the skill set and enjoy making content that shares the knowledge.
