It all started when I read this book back in college. I was in college reading Market Wizards by Jack Schwagger. And I remember thinking, [music] if these traders could make millions just by mastering what seemed like a complex, beautiful video game, why couldn't I? I started trading with zero experience. I lost money. I made every rookie mistake, chasing hype, ignoring risk, thinking I was smarter than the market. Then I realized the best traders aren't necessarily the smartest. They're often the most disciplined. [music] They're the ones who treat trading like a real craft. That shift changed everything. Within a few years, I was leading a trading desk for Trillium and would go on to make over $und00 million in P&L. [music] But when I looked online years later, all I saw were people selling dreams instead of teaching real trading. That's why I built Magnum Opus to show what trading actually takes. To this day, I still tell new traders, [music] the goal is not to get rich quick. This is not the right job for that. The goal is to master your craft. [music] The money follows the process. Most traders quit because they never learn the craft.
