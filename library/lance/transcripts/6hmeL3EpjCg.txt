Title: Stop Worrying About Your PNL #trading #daytrading #tradingpsychology
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=6hmeL3EpjCg
======================================================================

We don't want to focus on our P&L over the next day, the next 2 days, or even the next 5 days. We want to focus on the next 5 years, or even the next 10. What this allows you to do is progress every single day and raise your expected value with the big picture in mind. As counterintuitive as it seems, your lifetime P&L is really only two variables. One, what's your average daily expected value? And two, the number of days traded. If you truly focus on just increasing your bobblehead each day, you're maximizing the expected value of your time in that seat, but in a much more sustainable manner, you're removing your frustrations. You're minimizing your stress, and you're focusing on the journey and process rather than having to live with the ups and downs that are dictated by a P&L swing. So, what does this mean in practical application? It means that in the daily life of running an office, I would always say, "Hey guys, let's stop comparing P&L and let's go back to moving our bobblehead higher." So much time after the market close is spent asking, "Oh, what P&L did you put up or what did you make in XYZ?" You know my answer by now. It's not about that P&L, right? It's really about what can I learn from what I did today or what can I learn about these opportunities? The more time we can move away from asking or caring about P&L and more about what we can learn, the more effective we will become.
