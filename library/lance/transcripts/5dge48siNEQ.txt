Title: Trading at Prop Firm VS. Trading on Your Own #trading #propfirm #tradingcareer
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=5dge48siNEQ
======================================================================

While working for a pro firm, you often get a salary or a stipend. While at home, not only do you get no salary, but you're risking your own capital. As a result, the risk is way higher on your own. Now, on to the most critical variable in this decision. The biggest benefit of working for a trading firm is that your success rate is significantly higher. I would argue that most top discretionary trading firms are roughly able to retain about 20 to 30% of traders they hire. In other words, your odds of success goes from a measly couple percent if you try on your own and increases by a factor of 10. That is massive and in a nutshell is the ultimate reason why starting out working for a trading firm is a no-brainer to me. Yes, I'm biased in that I think given the opportunity, a hungry young trader should do everything possible to work for a top trading firm, especially if they are the type that has a pedigree to do out.
