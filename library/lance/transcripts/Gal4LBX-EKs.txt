Title: All great traders know their “WHY” #trading #daytrading #tradingpsychology
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=Gal4LBX-EKs
======================================================================

Trading is an incredibly hard job with a steep multi-year learning curve, during which you're frustrated and making minimal money. If you don't have a powerful why, when you find yourself staring into the abyss, and that will inevitably occur as a trader, in those key moments of doubt, you're going to convince yourself to go get that easy accounting gig. Or just to become an influencer. Or maybe even better, become a trust fund baby. I know this because I was there, too. It took me almost 2 years to become profitable, and I was so close to leaving the job. The only thing that kept me with it was my powerful why. Many get into trading for the money, and actually, there's nothing wrong with that. The desire to be financially free can be extremely powerful. The desire to provide for your family. This was certainly a huge factor for me. Other people have a true love for markets. They simply love trading. They love the game. They, like myself, can't imagine a job more fun than trying to beat the market. Others are just insanely competitive. It absolutely kills them not to succeed at trading. Failure isn't an option. They thrive on the struggle, pushing themselves to get better each day. The truth is, we don't have just one why. It's a mix of reasons. But I wanted to make sure that the candidates' reasons, whatever they were, were exceptionally strong. Could they relay a cohesive story as why they had that hunger to give it at least 2 years at the job? That was key at Trillium. Finding traders who would stick it out at least that time period, because anything shorter isn't giving it a proper chance.
