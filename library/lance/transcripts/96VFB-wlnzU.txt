Another big risk is selling options on stocks that are seemingly boring. A lot of these influencers love to talk about how you can sell these puts on Apple or Nvidia or other big names. So, let's say you sell puts on a boring blue chip company and have the cash to buy the stock. Should the stock decline in price, this is what people call a cash secured put. You think worst case, I get assigned. I own a good company and I'll just hold it. That sounds reasonable. That's why these strategies get sold to the masses, but it's missing the real risk. The real risk is the gap on a news event that makes you no longer believe the company is so good, but you've already been blown out. Take for example, the massive meltdown United Healthcare had in 2025. Nobody would have readily imagined the company would lose over 50% of its value in the span of a few months. An attempt at making a little supplementary income leaves you catching a falling knife and down multiples of what you stood to make.
