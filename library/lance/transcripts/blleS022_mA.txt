Let's start with why people fall in love with option selling. When you sell options, you often get this beautiful equity curve early on all based on small frequent wins. You feel like a genius. You sell a put on a stock that you don't mind owning. You collect a few hundred bucks. More than likely, you end up keeping the premium. You do it again and again and again, even increasing your bet sizes. Your win rate is high. Your P&L feels smooth. It feels like you finally found the strategy that will eventually bring you to Valhalla. And that is the trap. Because you're mistaking win rate for edge. A high win rate does not mean a strategy is safe. A strategy can win 90% of the time and still be a terrible strategy if the 10% of the time the loss is catastrophic. This is the classic picking up pennies in front of a steamroller problem. >> [screaming] >> Now, here is how the influencers trick you. They very naively assume that options prices give you enough cushion to offset the occasional big losses. The reality though is that markets are very efficient. In other words, you might make $1 90% of the time, but the other 10% of the time you lose $9. If you calculate that, the expected value is $0 before fees.
