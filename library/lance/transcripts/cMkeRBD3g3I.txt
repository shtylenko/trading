When I create hotkeys, I'm focused on two variables. How frequently I'm taking an action, as well as how critical speed is to that action. What do I mean by that? Buying 1,000 shares a few cents through the offer is an action you're likely doing very, very frequently. Because it is a frequent action, we want a hotkey. For me, given how common this order was, I would use shift G, which is a super easy key combination to access given the standard typing position for our hands, and my default tier size was set to 1,000 shares. So, shift G would queue up an order to buy one tier three cents through the offer. Similarly, shift H was my key to queue up an order to sell one tier three cents through the bid. On the other hand, there might also be actions that aren't as frequent, but they're critical for speed. For example, what happens if breaking news comes out against your position? Do you have a hotkey that aggressively and immediately gets you out of that position? That is an example of what you'd want a hotkey for because of its critical importance in protecting you and your P&L. Do you have a key that aggressively initiates a position for you? As an example of this, I would use the shift B key combo to immediately send a three times tier buy order 3% through the offer, and the shift N key combo to immediately send a three times tier sell order 3% through the bid. This was always used in quick-moving stocks where I knew I wanted liquidity.
