Lesson six, chat GPT is a tool that can amplify you as a trader and if you don't take advantage of it, you will be trading far below your potential over time. Let's be very clear, chat GPT is not your edge. It does not magically make you profitable, but it can compress time and increase throughput. It can help you summarize catalysts faster, organize your journaling, generate checklists, create trade plan templates, and more. The traders who use tools like this well are not using it to predict price. They're using it to do more high-quality work per minute. More prep, more review, more clarity with less wasted effort. Lesson seven, don't waste too much time trying to understand or listen to the fundamentals. Your time as a trader is better spent understanding sentiment and studying the chart. I'm not saying fundamentals don't matter in the long run. I'm saying your paycheck as a trader comes from price action. The chart is the only truth. My best trades happen when I'm listening to the chart. My worst trades happen when I'm fighting it. Lesson eight, advanced traders should be trading all products. Not because you need to be a master of everything overnight, but because the best opportunities rotate. As I explained in my video on expected value, which you can watch here, I am for the most part an expected value maximalist. Every decision you make should be thought of in terms of expected value. It is foolish to assume that the best expected value trade will always be in the product class that you are most familiar with. Some years, there are incredible opportunities in crypto. Some years, there are incredible opportunities in futures and options. Over the last four years, I've done a really great job of expanding my comfort zone into all those products and I've been pushing the traders I work with to do the same. Lesson 10, just kidding. This is lesson nine. I just want to make sure you're still with me. Beware of the comparison game. This year, I got the privilege of mentoring more traders than I ever have before. I worked with three trading firms and hundreds of traders across all experience levels. Want to know the most universal issue I heard from everyone? It didn't matter whether they were six months in or six years in, unprofitable or an eight-figure P&L for your trader. Most everyone struggles to some degree with the comparison game. Especially in the age of social media, where the most successful people are the most visible ones, so many traders develop skewed expectations of this job. I mentored beginner traders that didn't know that trading is a multi-year learning curve to find profitability. Lesson 10 and my final lesson for this video. This year had some enormous lows for me and some enormous highs. It is during the lows that we must keep on pushing, for there will be better days ahead. And it is during the highs that we must be grateful for what we have achieved. Wherever you find yourself now, remember that you always have control over your future. You might have underperformed your expectations this year or you might have outperformed them. That is in the past now. Nobody knows whether 2026 will be incredible trading or a dull, boring market. Ultimately, it is out of our hands anyways.
