Here's the key though. You're not logging every trade. You're curating only the best A+ setups. The goal is not volume. It's quality and repetition. In the book, Bella walks through the process SMB uses to coach their prop traders from zero to seven and even 8 figure P&L per year. The first step is you want to identify a setup that works for you. Maybe it's an earnings breakdown, a panic capitulation, a VWAP reclaim, or a trend day breakout. Step two is you want to capture the context of that play. What's the bigger picture? What's the catalyst? What's the sentiment? Who's trapped? Step three is to then screenshot the trades and the charts. You want to have notations with clear entries, exits, risk levels, and your thought process. Step four is you grade it. Was it an A+, B, or C? Why? And how would you play it bigger next time you trade it? You then want to repeat as step five. Refine the setup, track the data, and when you see it again, size the hell up if the data justifies
