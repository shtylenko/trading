Fibonacci retracements are complete BS. First of all, the math is arbitrary. There's nothing special about 38.2% or 61.8% when it comes to human behavior in financial markets. The market does not care about Leonardo Fibonacci. The market cares about liquidity, positioning, and psychology. The second reason why Fibonacci retracements are is charts don't consistently respect these levels with any level of precision beyond randomness. And as a result, no system allows me to capture the move with edge. As traders, the ultimate arbiter of truth is whether we can build a system around it that leads to consistent edge over the long run. Let's take this example from one of the most popular Fibonacci YouTube videos I could find. The creator discusses how these points around the 50% retracement level would be appealing shorts. And guess what? The chart ended up going lower. At first blush, all of this seems great, right? Not so fast. The wicks violate these levels multiple times. You don't know what bars will wick or will keep going. Additionally, if I short in this specific spot, don't I get stopped out later on from that other wick? The issue here is that the creator never mentions what your entry or your stop should actually be to capture any edge. It isn't about simply knowing what might happen.
