I would give myself a grade A through F each morning on what I called a temperature check. This was largely based on my sleep score and it set the tone for the day. I have a separate video diving deeper into the daily report card. Going back to the temperature check, if I slept poorly the prior night, like really bad, I simply wouldn't trade that day and I would do productive work instead. If I was really well-slept, I might consider increasing my risk and sizing up if it was an active day. Why shouldn't sleep be a factor in your daily risk? And if you're not doing that, why not? The difference in my decision-m, my emotional control, and my performance was unbelievable when I was very well-rested versus otherwise.
