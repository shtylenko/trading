This is the part nobody wants to hear. Prediction markets are not the future of easy money. I've said it before and I'll say it again. I think about how common it has become to be able to bet on anything and everything and that this is detrimental to our society. These prediction markets turn every outcome into some gambling opportunity and it normalizes speculation in ways that serve no real function to society. We are already a society that is hooked on cheap dopamine. Social media, TikTok reels, and prediction markets are turning everything into an addiction. Like sports betting, these markets disproportionately hook people who are vulnerable to being sold a dream all while transferring money from the most vulnerable to the insiders, the sophisticated players, and the platforms collecting fees. The result is a world that amplifies gambling addiction, emotional decision-making, and financial stress, especially among younger users who tend to fall the hardest for these all without producing real economic value, building anything useful, or improving outcomes. As these markets proliferate and expand into a world where you can bet on anything, they risk turning public life into a casino where attention, anxiety, and addiction are monetized at scale.
