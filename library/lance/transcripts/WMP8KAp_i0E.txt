Title: One of the most incredible things about equity markets is how quickly they recover from events 📈
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=WMP8KAp_i0E
======================================================================

One of the most incredible things about equity markets is how quickly they recover from events that feel world-ending in the moment. Iran conflict, couple weeks. Trump tariff panic, about a month. Covid, roughly five months back to all-time highs after what was arguably the greatest global shock most of us will ever witness. Think about how insane that is. In real time, every one of these events felt catastrophic. The headlines made it sound like the financial system was ending. People were panic selling, everybody suddenly becoming macro experts explaining why this time is different. And then, you blink. Markets are ripping back towards all-time highs again. This is one of the biggest reasons why shorting broad equity markets based purely on fear narratives is so dangerous. History repeatedly shows that betting on permanent collapse has been one of the most expensive trades imaginable. The market's default direction over long periods is up, and the stronger the fear in the moment, the harder that can be to remember. Follow me for more trading lessons.
