One of the most important corlaries on the topic of expected value is wrapping our heads around the fact that your average price in a trade is irrelevant to the expected value. 100% irrelevant. Why do I make such a stink about this? Because so many traders, professional or otherwise, make this mistake all the damn time. How often have you heard something like, "I'm gonna hold on to a core big picture because my average price is so good." Or, "How often have you seen a trader hold a position way out of the money against them, the stock turns, then they sell just as they get close to their average price in relief after being down so much. Many times that can be the exact wrong decision." Well, besides the wrong decision to be holding the stock against you with no stop to begin with, let's explore why that is and talk about how that can affect the above scenarios.
