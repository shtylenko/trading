My biggest slumps have always come when I felt I was underperforming those around me on the trading floor. That comparison game crushed me until my work with Dr. Catz helped me break the cycle. And honestly, it's probably saved me millions of dollars. Here's why this matters. Traders who are underperforming are emotionally vulnerable. In those moments, instead of putting their energy into improving, they often look outward. They compare themselves to others. They seek affirmation. That's human. We are competitive, always looking to see where we stack up in the hierarchy. And trading is especially dangerous because the scorecard feels so black and white. Today, it's even worse with social media. Traders scroll through their highlight reels seeing only cherrypicked wins and polished narratives. Jonathan Catz views most of this as toxic, a major distraction from the real work needed for success. And he's right. Instead of diagnosing our mistakes and doing the hard work to improve, we get sucked into a loop that makes us feel worse.
