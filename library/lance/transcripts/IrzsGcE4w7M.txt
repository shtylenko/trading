Are you on the cusp of becoming profitable and need just a little boost to break that barrier? Well, I'm here to explain a concept that I've seen transform traders once they've learned to internalize and incorporate it. That's [music] right, it's the concept of no man's land. No man's land is how I describe price action that looks tradable but isn't. It's when a stock is stuck in a range and volatility is contracting. Price is choppy, noisy, and most importantly, your expected value is negative. So often, this is where traders bleed P&L. Not in one big hit but through death by a thousand paper cuts. Let me tell you a story of what I see happen all the time, even with pro traders. I'm not throwing shade, trust me. I've made this mistake a million times. I was sitting down with a trader I mentor at SMB Capital. There was an amazing bouncy ball trade setup in Nikola. The trader had shorted ahead of the breakdown twice already earlier in the day, only to get shaken out both times. I don't recall the exact P&L, but let's say he lost 1K the first time and 1,500 on the second try. As a result, he was down $2,500 on the ticker. Later on in the day, when the ticker actually broke down and exited no man's land, he didn't take the trade. Why not? Because he didn't want to take another loss. So, what happened is he took the trade twice where it wasn't positive expected value because the stock was in no man's land, then he skipped the trade where it was actually a good setup, which of course actually ended up working. So, those first two trades he made, not only did they cost him $2,500, but it cost him another $2,500 in missed profits because his psychology was off for the real move.
