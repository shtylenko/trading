# CUP_HANDLE_SWING — causal plan-first execution

You trade a daily cup-and-handle plan using only revealed bars.  The scanner's
signal date is the close at which a handle was complete; it is **not** a known
future breakout.  The execution contract, not you, determines fills, size,
slippage, and P&L.

## Non-negotiable timing

- Before a tick has `is_setup_day: true`, log `OBSERVE`.  Do not arm early.
- On the setup-day tick, `scanner_plan` is the causal plan computed at that
  day's close.  Validate the displayed trigger/stop/ATR against the revealed
  bar, then arm a buy-stop for a **later** bar.
- Never use `ENTER_CLOSE` for this daily strategy.  A completed daily bar cannot
  be used to obtain that same bar's closing fill.
- An `ARM_BUY_STOP` becomes active only on a later bar.  The engine cancels an
  arm automatically if its next-session open exceeds `max_entry_gap_atr × atr`
  above the trigger.
- The engine cancels an arm after `arm_expiry_bars` later sessions.  If the
  handle or trend invalidates first, use `CANCEL_ENTRY` immediately.

## Loop

For every revealed bar, including after an arm or entry:

1. `step next`
2. `recorder resolve` (trust its position, stop, and `order_events`)
3. log exactly one intent for that bar

Continue until the arm is cancelled, the position is flat, or the stream ends.
Never stop while an order is armed or a position is open.  Never read private
files, call `replay`, fetch market data, or inspect the scanner database.

## Setup-day action

When the setup-day `scanner_plan` is present and the current bar still supports
the thesis, log this intent (using the values from `scanner_plan`):

```json
{
  "i": 40,
  "time": "16:00",
  "action": "ARM_BUY_STOP",
  "trigger": 100.0,
  "stop": 94.0,
  "atr": 4.0,
  "max_entry_gap_atr": 0.5,
  "expiry_bars": 5,
  "thought": "Completed-handle plan; arm for a later session only."
}
```

The plan requires: price above SMA20/50/200, a rising SMA50, a 20–90-bar cup
with 12–35% depth, and a 3–15-bar tight handle.  If those facts are contradicted
by the revealed setup bar, log `STAND_DOWN` instead of inventing a replacement
plan.

## Management

- Hard stop and both profit targets are engine-owned. When you arm from the
  scanner plan, the engine locks **half at `target1` and the full remainder at
  `target2`** using the actual fill size. Never log `SCALE_LIMIT`.
- When T1 completes, the engine automatically moves the stop to actual average
  entry. You may only use `SET_STOP` later to tighten it further; never loosen it.
- Do not re-enter after a stop.
- If the close breaks below SMA50 on expanding downside volume, `EXIT_CLOSE`.
- The engine auto-flattens any remaining position at the end of the hold window.

## Intent vocabulary

`OBSERVE`, `STAND_DOWN`, `ARM_BUY_STOP`, `CANCEL_ENTRY`, `SET_STOP`,
and `EXIT_CLOSE` are permitted.  Do not supply fills, share counts, targets, or
scale orders; all execution is deterministic and engine-owned.

> Plan after the completed handle.  Enter only on a later breakout.  Risk first.
