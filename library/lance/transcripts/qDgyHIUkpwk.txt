One of the hardest questions in trading is this: When should you keep going and when should you quit? Many great traders blew up multiple times before they ever became consistently profitable. So, does that mean you should stick through it? The upcoming Market Wizards chapter on Colm O'Meara is a perfect example. Christian blew up several accounts before eventually turning a small account into tens of millions. And he's not the exception. A shocking number of elite traders struggled early. So, this creates a massive dilemma for developing traders. If failure is common on the path to mastery, how do you know whether you're persevering intelligently wasting years chasing a fantasy? Here's my answer. The key variable is not whether you're currently making money. The key variable is whether you are genuinely improving. Are you building process? Are you developing pattern recognition, emotional control, and risk management? Because trading mastery is often non-linear. Many traders look hopeless right before the breakthrough. But there's another side people ignore. If years go by and you're still gambling emotionally, still refusing accountability, and learning nothing from mistakes, then persistence becomes delusion. The best traders make progress and improve their process. The traders that fail tread water. That's the difference. Follow me for more trading lessons.
