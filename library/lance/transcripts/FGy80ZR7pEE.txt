At a very high level, we as traders are trying to help the market price securities based on an assortment of factors. What changes the fundamental value of a security? Breaking news. As a result, breaking news often leads to the largest moves stocks make. Think about earnings, mergers and acquisitions, or stock offerings. During periods of fresh breaking news, stocks are trying to find the new price equilibrium and can make sharp, massive trending moves. That stands in contrast to when stocks make sharp massive moves without the presence of a fundamental change to the value of the company. Statistically, these moves without news are far more likely to mean revert than moves with news. That does not mean all stock moves need to or will mean revert.
