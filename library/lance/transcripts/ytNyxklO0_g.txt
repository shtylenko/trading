Title: Most traders stay stuck for years because they’re solving the wrong problem ❌
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=ytNyxklO0_g
======================================================================

Most traders, they suck for years because they're solving the wrong problem. Here's one of the most important frameworks I've ever found. In trading, you are either edge constrained or opportunity constrained. And whichever one you are determines your entire strategy. If you're edge constrained, it means you do not yet have a proven system with positive expectancy. In that phase, trading more is actually dangerous. So, your job is not to scan 500 charts a day and force more trades hoping something clicks. Your job [music] is to build proof, back test, journal, collect data. You need to refine your executions and figure out whether your setup genuinely works over a large sample size. Because if you don't have edge, more activity just accelerates losses. But once you do have proven edge, the constraint completely changes. Now, you become opportunity constrained. The question is no longer, "Does my setup work?" Now, the question becomes, "How do I find more of my best setups [music] and maximize them?" That's when you focus on scanners, execution, sizing, options, finding new markets, and scaling the same edge across more opportunities. That's the mistake so many [music] traders make. They try to scale before they validate. They optimize before they prove. And they search for more trades before proving the first trade even has positive expectancy. And that's why they stay busy but never progress. The bottleneck determines the strategy. Diagnose the real [music] constraint first. Follow me for more trading lessons.
