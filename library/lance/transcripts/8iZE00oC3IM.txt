I'm an eight-figure trader, and this is every type of strategy explained. Number one, trend-following strategies. These strategies aim to ride moves that are already happening. They can work well, but only if you're selective and avoid chasing late entries. Number two, mean-reversion strategies. These strategies look for price to snap back after getting stretched. They require exceptional timing skills and strict risk management because price can stay extended longer than you expect. Number three, breaking news and momentum strategies. These strategies capitalize on reactions to catalysts like earnings or other headlines. They can offer opportunity, but conditions move fast and mistakes get punished quickly. So, if you want to find a strategy that fits your [music] trading style, hit that follow button.
