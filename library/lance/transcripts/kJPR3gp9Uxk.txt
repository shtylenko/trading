I cannot stress enough how important it is for you to be building out a basic playbook, finding consistency, and learning the nuances of your plays. This whole concept is premised on the idea of you being able to tell an A+ trade from a B trade, and so on. And not everyone can do that. It requires a close eye to nuance and a lot of experience. Personally, I'd have trades graded across the scale of A, B, C, and D. And here's how they would differentiate. An a trade, at least for me, occurs one to two times per month. These are memorable, amazing, standout trades that you end up looking back on at the end of the year. These are the trades that you really want to go big on and often bet exponentially if your trading skill allows for it. A B trade for me is something that can happen dozens of times per month. It could mean being the best trade of the day or one of the best couple trades of the day. Pretty common, but still overall pretty great. At the end of the week, you'd likely still review these opportunities. A C trade is just an average everyday trade. Maybe you have a bit of edge, maybe it's a small scalp, nothing to write home about, maybe a small positive expected value trade, but it's really more of a flyer than anything and certainly won't be remembered or reviewed. Finally, a D trade is a mediocre 50/50 toss-up trade you just get sucked into because you're bored and probably lose money, especially after commissions.
