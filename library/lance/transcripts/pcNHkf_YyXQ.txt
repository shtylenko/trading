Here's how getting good at trading too fast can cost [music] you millions. Most of the time, you're obsessing over your P&L and comparing yourself [music] to others. This pushes you to revenge trade and make more emotional trading decisions, and those cost you more money in the future. Instead, your focus should be on your expected [music] value. This is the number you profit on average if you were to run the same setup 100 times. [music] Think of a chart with time along the bottom axis and expected value going up the side. [music] Your goal is to move the line a little up and to the right every day as you improve. When you think this way, single wins and losses stop controlling your emotions. Every trade you take in the future that doesn't go your way, take a look at the setup and write down one fix you can do the next day. >> [music] >> If you stay disciplined and evaluate your process instead of chasing daily results, your expected value rises naturally over time.
