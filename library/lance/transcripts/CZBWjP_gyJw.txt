The first is exhaustion gaps. This is probably the most misunderstood version because beginners think every huge gap is automatically bullish. Sometimes massive gaps are actually exhaustion and the opening range foretells a move lower. A great example over the last couple years would be names like Supermicro Computer or MicroStrategy. These stocks gapped up massively higher after already going vertical for days or weeks. At the time social media was euphoric and retail was chasing. CNBC suddenly pretending they discovered the stock yesterday and everyone feels like a genius. But here's what experienced traders understand. Late stage momentum often creates trapped buyers. The last buyers into euphoric moves are usually the weakest hands. So what happens? The stock gaps up huge, everyone piles in emotionally at the open, and then the opening range becomes critical. If the stock fails to hold that opening range and breaks lower, suddenly all those euphoric late buyers are trapped simultaneously. That's when opening range breaks to the downside can become incredibly powerful. You get longs puking their positions, momentum funds de-risking, and short sellers pressing into weakness, all at once.
