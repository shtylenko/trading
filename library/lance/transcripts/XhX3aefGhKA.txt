People love to say shorting is unpatriotic or destructive, but research and history says otherwise. Short sellers help correct mispricings faster. They expose fraud. They add liquidity and counterbalance market euphoria. They stop bubbles from going unchecked. In one 2008 study, Bulmer Jones and Zang found that stocks with more short interest have significantly more efficient prices, meaning faster incorporation of information and less overvaluation. In a 2002 study, Chen, Hong, and Stein, no relation, showed that restrictions on short selling actually make bubbles worse by removing the counterbalance to bullish overpricing. So, when shorts take down a fraud, like Enron or Wirecard, they are not villains. They're market watchd dogs doing what regulators often fail to do. Day traders are often the ones shorting manipulative pump and dumps. They're the ones shorting the silly, lowflat, dilutive terms. Day traders are shorting hype and fraud so that our markets operate efficiently.
