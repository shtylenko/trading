Let's talk about what pre-market is actually good for. I find that the traders most commonly involved pre-market are those that trade micro cap stocks. Micro cap stocks tend to have a ton of early morning news releases. These stocks, because they're so small in market cap, can sometimes even move tens of percent or even hundreds of percent all before the market opens. Check out these charts for example. Micro cap traders love to take advantage of all that volatility. Also, because of lower liquidity in the very early hours, especially on the start of pre-market trading at 4:00 a.m., it is very common to see price spikes as the venues and exchanges open, which often times can be faded profitably if you are trading pre-market. You always want to be aware of every half hour increment during which it is very common for news to come out. The most common time for news to come out is 8:00 a.m., 8:30 a.m., and 9:00 a.m. in the pre-market. Breaking news traders love to be in their seat with their hands on the keys during those time increments. Pre-market levels often matter later during the regular session, even if you never place a single trade before the open. While I always don't consider pre-market levels in trading to be relevant during the regular session, if a stock is doing real volume and a decent percentage of volume compared to how much it normally trades during regular hours, I often find that those pre-market levels can in fact be meaningful, and they do have value when you trade off of them.
