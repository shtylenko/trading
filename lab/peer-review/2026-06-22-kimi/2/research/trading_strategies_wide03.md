## Facet: ETF Rotation & Tactical Asset Allocation

### Key Findings

1. **NO standard ETF rotation strategy achieves >3% monthly (36%+ CAGR)**. The highest-returning non-leveraged rotation strategies (Dual Momentum GEM) achieve ~14-17% CAGR [^26^][^18^]. Even aggressive sector rotation in Taiwan markets achieved only 28.58% annualized [^14^].

2. **Leveraged ETF strategies CAN achieve 36%+ CAGR**, but with extreme drawdowns. The UPRO/TQQQ VIX-filtered strategy achieved 24.4% CAR with 54% max drawdown [^120^]. The TQQQ/TMF 50/50 rebalancing delivered 23.8% CAGR with 38.7% max drawdown [^125^]. TQQQ with weekly MACD signals returned +11,194% over ~15 years (~36% CAGR) [^118^].

3. **The Hedgefundie Adventure (UPRO/TMF 55/45)** achieved 24.63% CAGR since inception (2009) with a 70.58% max drawdown [^134^][^135^]. In the 2000-2011 "lost decade" for US stocks, it would have delivered 11% CAGR [^135^].

4. **Simple momentum rotation among SPY/TLT/EEM** yields only 9.4-11.5% CAGR depending on lookback period (1-month vs 3-month) [^13^][^110^][^111^]. The 3-month lookback improves results to 11.5% CAGR with 32% max drawdown.

5. **Gary Antonacci's Dual Momentum GEM** returned 17.43% annually over 39 years (1974-2013) vs 8.85% for the global market, with max drawdown of only 22.7% vs 60.21% for the index [^18^]. Enhanced versions achieve 16.4% CAGR [^26^].

6. **The "Losers" (contrarian) rotation strategy** outperforms "Winners" (momentum) with quarterly rebalancing in some markets. In China ETF rotation, quarterly "Losers" turned $100 into $290 vs $198 for "Winners" [^15^]. In Canadian TSX 60 sector rotation, "Median" performer selection (avoiding extremes) achieved the best Sharpe ratio of 0.922 with 15.30% annualized returns [^123^].

7. **Tactical Asset Allocation with trend following** (10-month MA) significantly reduces drawdowns but limits upside. Faber's original TAA strategy achieves only 4.54% CAGR with 13.24% max drawdown [^113^]. SACEMS (Simple Asset Class ETF Momentum) has shown attractive but variable performance since 2006 [^117^].

8. **VIX-based allocation switching is problematic**. Academic research found that simple VIX-level strategies (buy when VIX>30, sell when VIX<20) produced negative returns [^115^]. However, adding 10% VIX Futures allocation during crisis periods (VIX>30) improved portfolio risk-adjusted returns with 7-25% annual returns depending on crisis identification method [^115^].

9. **Leveraged trend following** (using SSO 2x ETF) achieved 14.4% mean annual returns vs 9.4% for unlevered, but with 26% standard deviation and significant drawdown risk [^140^].

10. **The 3x leveraged ETF weekly MACD strategy** (TQQQ with QQQ weekly signals) achieved +11,194% profit from Feb 2010 to July 2025 (~36% CAGR), using a 2% exit buffer and consecutive entry conditions [^118^].
