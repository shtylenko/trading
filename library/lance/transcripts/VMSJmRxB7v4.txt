Everyone that watches sports or considers themselves a sports fan thinks they have edge versus the market. How many of your sports betting friends are doing real studies and quantitative analysis of these markets? Pretty much none of them. Yeah, I'd agree with you. Whether in trading, sports betting, or prediction markets, most of the participants lack any edge and shouldn't even be in the pool with what are a bunch of professional sharks. I know one friend who consistently wins sports betting. You want to know how he does it? He has a quantitative model that he developed and back-tested over multiple years that takes advantage of human psychology that tends to view randomness as a pattern. So, when one team is on a hot streak, he tends to fade it. Then, when another team is on a losing streak, he tends to fade that. But, all this is backed up by very specific data and applied only in very specific situations. The end result, he has a consistently profitable but highly unscalable strategy that has made him a decent supplemental income, but nothing more. Between the spread and commissions, his edge is amazingly thin. He needs to bet huge amounts and sustain large drawdowns to make the money he does.
