Here are my top 10 trading lessons from 2025 that you can use to level up your P&L this year. The biggest mistake I made was not having enough cash on hand as collateral in my trading account. Having worked for a firm most of my career, I hadn't yet had to learn this lesson. Every day I showed up and I could rely upon my buying power being there and even having more if I needed it. In my trading account, pretty much all of the collateral is long-term equity holdings. And during the sell-off, those long-term holdings got hit hard. Really, really hard. So, while the market was offering some of the best trading opportunities of the entire year, panic, dislocation, forcelling, insane riskreward, my long-term book going down meant my buying power got crushed. Lesson two, experience pays. Never forget about those old playbooks you've built up in past markets. And if you don't know what a trading playbook is yet, make sure you watch this video where I explain it. This year was a reminder that the best and most adaptive traders don't just have one strategy. A robust, resilient trader has a library of playbooks to utilize when the market calls for it. Lesson three, even an old dog can learn new tricks. There were two areas of improvement that I was particularly proud of this year. The first was huge progress I made in my ability to recognize changes in market structure and sentiment. I had taken time to study what the top looked like in all the frothy names back in 2021. Then this year when baskets of stocks started to get really crazy, I did an amazing job identifying that euphoria as the market structure started to break down beneath it. This wasn't a call on the overall market, but just on the most overvalued names like the quantum sector, rare earth metals, and names like Olo. Lesson number four, the biggest most scalable money is in swing trading. But that doesn't mean it's the best money for you. While probably the topic of a future video, it is my belief that intraday trading offers the best expected value. And if you are someone that is good at quick decision-making and can stay glued to the screens, which for most traders shouldn't be a problem, intraday trading is the best area to focus on for an assortment of reasons. That being said, swing trading is where a lot of the biggest and most scalable trading opportunities can be found because you can put on larger size without the same liquidity issues of intraday trading. Lesson five. This move has been apparent to me for a while and I've been tweeting the highlights in the news. But the move to 247 trading is coming and it's coming fast. We are moving toward a world where the financial markets are ubiquitous. You can trade any product any day of the week at any time. In a world that becomes more continuous, some of the old boundaries disappear. Market hours, overnight gaps, weekend breaks. Many traders have asked me what I think the implications of 24-hour trading are. The truth is, I myself don't fully know. I suspect the market will always need an opening price and a closing price for market structure reasons. But what will happen to certain technical strategies if there's no such thing as a gap up or a gap down? I don't know. But I do know traders will need to adapt.
