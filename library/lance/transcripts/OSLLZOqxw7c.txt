Hormozi is relentless about constraint identification. He defines the number one constraint as the portion of your business or trading that will generate the highest return for the amount of effort put in. What I found so amazing about this mindset is that this is exactly how I run my mentorship sessions with traders. I am always asking them, "What single change, if successfully made, would double or triple your annual P&L?" Because if you can answer that, you finally have a North Star to focus on. Hormozi forces deep thinking and a singular focus. It's like that old parable. List your top 10 priorities, then circle the top one and cross out the rest. Not because the rest don't matter, but because you can't attack 10 things with intensity. It isn't that the other ones aren't important. They are, and you will get to them, but only one at a time and after you solve the current top constraint first. So often in conversation with Hormozi and his team, without realizing it, you'll drift away from focusing on your number one constraint. They are so used to be dialed in that they immediately catch you and call you out on it. Is that actually the constraint right now? If you solve this, will it actually materially improve the business? Now, translate that to trading. Trading has literally infinite work you could be doing. Studying charts, reading about catalysts, backtesting, optimizing your technology, watching videos of your trading, building scanners, tweaking indicators. It never ends. And most traders get buried doing work that feels important, but is not effective. They do the comfortable work. They avoid the uncomfortable I've seen this pan out thousands of times.
