I started my career in 2011 at Trillium, one of the best proprietary trading firms for discretionary traders in the world. They've been around over 20 years. And when you trade at a firm like that, you're getting six major benefits right out of the gate. Benefit one, you get direct mentorship. At a place like Trillium, you're not learning from anonymous internet traders or some Discord room where nobody has to prove anything. You're learning from mentors with a verified, proven track record. That matters because in trading, the wrong teacher is worse than no teacher. It gives you false confidence and negative expected value habits. Benefit number two, you get capital. Capital is not just about trading bigger. Capital changes the entire psychological landscape of your development. First, you do not have the same emotional damage of losing your own money while you're still learning. Losing your own capital early creates a toxic feedback loop. Fear, hesitation, revenge trading, oversized risk because you're trying to catch up and then only more mistakes. At a prop firm, you're still accountable, but the psychological weight is different. You're paying tuition in a structured environment rather than potentially blowing yourself up and having to quit before you ever had the chance to get good. Benefit number three, you get an elite trading environment. This is the one you don't fully understand until you lived it. Trading alone is lonely, and loneliness is where bad habits grow. At Trillium, you get camaraderie. You're in the trenches with people who actually get it. You're sharing ideas, comparing notes, laughing off the pain, and staying sane through the inevitable draw downs. Benefit four, you get technology. Tech is not a luxury in trading. It can be part of your whole edge. You get reliable, lightning fast internet. You get optimized order routing. You get low commissions and fees. You get joined resources and news sources. All of that directly improves performance. Better routing and lower friction means less slippage, better average fills, and cleaner execution. All of which improve your average wins and reduce your average losses. Benefit five, and one of the most important, you get a salary, so you are getting paid to learn. This is a critical benefit because the hardest part of trading isn't just learning concepts. It's surviving long enough for the skill to compound. Benefit number six, you get a risk manager. This one is massively underappreciated until the day it saves your career. A good risk manager is basically a circuit breaker for your worst psychological states. When you're on tilt, when you're sleepd deprived, or when you just took a fat loss and your brain is screaming to make it back, a risk manager can do what most traders can't do in the moment.
