Title: Even the Best Traders Struggle #trading #marketwizards #investing
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=8hASo5q1guM

The first truth is that initially struggling or blowing your account does not mean you will not make it. There's this perception that elite traders just had some natural talent. They flipped a switch and they started printing money. But if you actually read the books closely, you see the opposite over and over again. Ed Seykota literally says everyone gets what they want out of the market. And what he meant by that is people unconsciously self-sabotage until they fix themselves, not their strategy. Even someone like Bruce Kovner admitted that he made huge mistakes early on before refining his process. These are not clean success stories. They're messy, painful learning curves filled with setbacks. And most market wizards have blown up or hit massive drawdowns or even gone through long periods where nothing works. And that doesn't just happen early. It happens throughout their careers. Marty Schwartz talks about struggling for years before becoming consistent. And even after success, he still had periods where he had to step back and reset. That lines up perfectly with what I've said before about why most traders fail, which is they don't give themselves enough runway and they underestimate how brutal the process is. If your expectation is that you'll just figure it out quickly, you're done before you even start. The traders who make it are not the ones that magically don't struggle. They're the ones who can take those punches, actually learn from them, and keep showing up.
