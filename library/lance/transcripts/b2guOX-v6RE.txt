Title: Is Ross Cameron (Warrior Trading) Legit? #trading #warriortrading #rosscameron
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=b2guOX-v6RE
======================================================================

Ross clearly knows trading. He clearly understands momentum, and he clearly is genuinely placing the trades. He has more real receipts than most of the internet's trading clowns combined. On that basis alone, yes, he is transparent in a way that most trading influencers are not. But here's where the conversation gets much more nuanced and much more important. Ross is live streaming his trading to thousands of traders in his chat room daily. On one hand, that looks like transparency. At the same time though, that also creates a massive conflict of interest in that traders in his community are piggybacking off of his live trades. When you are trading micro cap stocks, this can have very real impacts on the price movement. Imagine if every time you bought a stock, people bought 10 times your size after you. It is the same exact price dynamic of a pump and dump scheme. This is exactly what Zack Morris, a famous stock pump and dumper from the COVID era, and others in the past have been prosecuted for. So look, I am not accusing Ross Cameron of any pumping and dumping, but I do think when he enters a trade, he's doing it on its own merits, and all of it is legal. But I also strongly believe he is a massive tailwind when he does so because of his thousands of chat room members, some of whom piggyback him into the trade. This is especially problematic when these small cap momentum markets are really hot.
