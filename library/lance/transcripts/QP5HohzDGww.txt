That said, there are many real reasons why I suspect technical analysis works. The first one is market structure. Markets in their simplest form are really just people buying and people selling. If a huge seller keeps selling at the same price, that price becomes hard to get above, not because of a line on a chart, but because that big seller is dumping shares there. Smaller traders then notice this. They see that price cannot go higher. So, they sell before that big seller finishes. Someone else sells a little bit lower. Then another person sells a little lower than that. Over time, this behavior creates a pattern. That pattern is what we call resistance on a chart. Support works the same way, but just flipped. If there's a huge buyer buying a lot at one price, that price becomes hard to break below. Smaller buyers notice it and start buying in front of that big buyer. Their buying pushes price up.
