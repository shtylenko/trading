So, what is exponential bet sizing? In a nutshell, it's being able to scale your bet size dramatically with respect to the opportunity or the expected value of a play. There are a few variables that go into doing this successfully. And the first is having a proper understanding of your trades and the grading and sizing system to go along with it. Personally, I'd have trades graded across a scale of A, B, C, and D. And here's how they would differentiate. An A trade, at least for me, occurs one to two times per month. These are memorable, amazing, standout trades that you end up looking back on at the end of the year. These are the trades that you really want to go big on and often bet exponentially if your trading skill allows it. A B trade, for me, is something that can happen dozens of times per month. It could mean being the best trade of the day or one of the best couple trades of the day. Pretty common, but still overall pretty great. At the end of the week, you'd likely still review these opportunities. A C trade is just an average everyday trade. Maybe you have a bit of edge, maybe it's a small scalp, nothing to write home about. Maybe a small positive expected value trade, but it's really more of a flyer than anything and certainly won't be remembered or reviewed. Finally, a D trade is a mediocre 50/50 toss-up trade you just get sucked into because you're bored and probably lose money, especially after commissions. Having such a defined grading system allows me to be very systematic and intentional with my trading and sizing. If I knew in the moment I was just in a B trade, I would try to push it and get $10,000 worth of risk. But if all of the sudden I realized this was transitioning into something special, that it might be the best trade all month, I knew I'd have to push my sizing to be much larger. How would I define that? If liquidity allowed for it, I'd be pushing for an exponential increase and go up to maybe say a hundred grand worth of risk. What is great about this method is it allows you to gradually change your betting community believe in equal bet sizing, meaning whether it's an A or a D grade setup, you size the same every time. Personally, I don't believe that is the most optimal way to do it. Let's take a prime example out of poker. For the sake of simplicity, imagine sitting at the poker table with one other player over the course of two hands, and this is what you draw. First, 9-2, and then pocket aces for the other hand. The estimated win percentage, or pre-flop equity, for the above hands looks something like the following. 9-2 is 38.9% and pocket aces is 85.3%. If you, as the player sitting at the table, were to bet equally across each hand above, do you think you'd walk away with any winnings in the long run? The answer is a resounding no, due to the fact that you're not taking advantage of exponential bet sizing. When you draw pocket aces, you know that although it's not guaranteed, you have the strongest hand possible to start with, and it's time to start raising your bets. This is when you have the highest chance to make some money. The question begs, how often do you get dealt aces? Statistically, the probability of being dealt aces is one in 221 hands. Given how rarely this happens, if it happens, you need to not only recognize it, but capitalize on it. If you have less than a half percent chance of drawing pocket aces that give you close to an 85% probability of winning, you go for it every time. Does it mean you'll always win? Of course not. But over the long run, this will prove to be profitable. That's the equivalent of an A+ trade. You need to be able to recognize and capitalize by betting exponentially bigger. Imagine if the above player bet slightly more when drawing aces compared to drawing something like a 9-2. That would be insane.
