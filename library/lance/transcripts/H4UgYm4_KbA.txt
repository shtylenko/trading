Forcing trades on days when the conditions aren't favorable is poor for staying consistent. Trading less when it's slow is good for staying consistent. Using slow days to review past trades is excellent for staying consistent. Sizing randomly based on how you feel is poor for protecting your account. Trying to keep risk consistent on average days is good for protecting your account. [music] And using a fixed risk rule every trade is excellent for protecting your account. Trying to win it back fast after a loss is poor for avoiding emotional spirals. Taking a short pause after a loss is good for avoiding emotional spirals. Reducing your risk on every trade until the initial loss is recovered is excellent for avoiding emotional spirals. Not following me is poor for getting better as a trader. Watching more of my videos is good for getting better as a trader. And following me for more trading tips is excellent to get better as a trader.
