When is the right time to walk away from a trade? >> A question I get a lot is, "What's the maximum I should ever risk?" The real answer is, it depends on you, but you can still build rules that protect you. Let's break it down. Step one, set a daily loss limit before the market opens. This is the hard stop for your day. Once you hit it, you're done. No trying to make it back, no exceptions. Step two, know your stop before you enter the trade. If you decide your stop after you're in, emotions take over and discipline disappears. Step three, never let a goal number change your risk. The moment you're trading to hit a P&L target or make something back, you're no longer trading the setup. Step four, size your trades so you can stay emotionally neutral. If your size causes hesitation, panic, or early exits, it's already too big, no matter how good the setup is. Step five, have a clear walk away rule during tilt. Forcing trades, chasing losses, or feeling rushed are all signals to step away and protect your mental capital. Want to trade without giving all your profits back to the market?
