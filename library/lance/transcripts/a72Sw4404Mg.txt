This is me now, one of the world's top traders. But I used to be just like you. I was in college, young, hungry, and driven, desperate to make a life for myself that wouldn't involve always stressing out over money or spending my whole life working up some corporate ladder. Then I read the Market Wizard books by Jack Schwagger, and I fell in love with the idea of beating the markets. I dreamt of a job that would challenge me, one that offered big upside and no corporate BS. I started to dream of becoming a trader. Even in college, I was doing everything I could to make my dream happen. In fact, here's a photo of me hosting the legendary Mike Belluri, author of One Good Trade and founder of SMB Capital at the Indiana University Undergraduate Investment Club back in 2011. Breaking into Wall Street from Indiana University was not easy. Even my professor said he had never seen anyone put as much effort into the job search as I did. That effort paid off and I went on to get a job on Wall Street working for a firm called Trillium. Trillium is one of the top trading firms in the world and best of all they provide you with direct mentorship so you can learn from experienced credible traders. Despite having all the resources of a firm like Trillium, the mentorship, the technology, the capital, resources that most people will never have, I still could not figure trading out. I was truly giving it my all. I was working obsessively during the week and I was studying on weekends, but I just was not seeing progress. While I didn't know it at the time, I was starting to piece together everything I needed to become profitable. Even if the P&L wasn't there yet, I was building a process for how to understand markets, how to read the charts, how to manage risk, and most of all, how to make daily improvements. Those small daily improvements, they added up. By the end of my second year, I finally hit my $100,000 P&L milestone. Fast forward to over a decade later. Well, progress compounds and over a decade of daily improvements can lead to some crazy results. Some days it still doesn't feel real. And that same struggling trader, me, well, I'm now being profiled in the next market wizards book as one of the best day traders ever. And Mike Bellfury of SMB Capital, yep, I help mentor at his firm. Now, my point is this. Trading is really hard. Most traders really do fail. And if you're doing it without proper mentorship and firm resources, the odds are stacked against you. So, let me hit you with the truth. I cannot promise you success. Nobody can. And most people should not even get into trading. But if you are serious about putting the effort into this job, if you have realistic expectations of how long the learning curve is, then I am here to help you level the playing field. After 15 years grinding in the trenches, trading my health for wealth, my new passion is becoming the best educator I can be. It's time to level the playing field for the retail trader. Is now your time?
