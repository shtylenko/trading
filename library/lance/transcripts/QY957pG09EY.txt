Implementing a daily report card was the best practice that helped take me from being a top 10 trader to then take the number one spot at Trillium. First off, I'm starting with the date and a simple grade of A through F. Feel free to do whatever system makes sense to you. Figure out your goal and then break it down with solutions. If, for example, overtrading is your issue, you can create a goal that only allows you five trades each day. When you're going in and reviewing your daily report card at the end of the day, you grade yourself solely based on if you accomplished that one chosen goal for the day, not your P&L. Below, I have a small section of reminders or any apherisms you want to make to yourself. These were things that I just felt were always good to read over pre-market and refresh my mind on. This can be anything like listening to the intraday chart or the level two, game planning, various situations or etc. whatever is most applicable as a reminder to you and your trading. The following section is equally as important as the goal. A breakdown of the day. I would grade myself based on each block of time starting from 9:30 to 11:00 a.m. 11:00 a.m. to noon, noon to 2, and finally 2 to 4:00 p.m. The beauty of this is that it allows you to catch snowballing bad habits before they spiral into larger, more drastic negative outcomes. I'd add any relevant comments or notes I might have and do the same for the remaining segments throughout the day. At day's end, I would take the time to reflect on what I learned or improved upon.
