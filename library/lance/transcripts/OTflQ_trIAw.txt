Everybody on trading Twitter keeps talking about Claude and AI like it's the holy grail. But let me ask you something. If AI is such a game-changer for traders, why aren't most traders P&L's exploding higher? For the last 6 months, Claude has been the topic on every trader call imaginable. Yet, from what I've personally seen, the traders who crushed it during volatile periods like April didn't outperform because they had better prompts. They outperformed because they had better trading systems, better execution, and better emotional control. This is the uncomfortable truth most people don't want to hear. More information is usually not the bottleneck in trading. Most traders already know more than enough to make money. The bottleneck is execution. It's discipline. It's sizing correctly under pressure. And ironically, AI can sometimes make this worse. Because now traders feel productive spending 8 hours generating ideas, building dashboards, and asking Claude questions about optimizing workflows instead of doing the uncomfortable high-value actions that actually move the needle. Don't get me wrong. AI is incredibly useful. I use it myself. But if I were a betting man, I'd say AI has probably improved trader workflows overall while simultaneously reducing net traders P&L through distraction away from what actually matters. Tools do not create P&L on its own. Improved execution does. Follow me for more trading lessons.
