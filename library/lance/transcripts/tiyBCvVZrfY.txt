So, how do you know whether you are one swing away from diamonds or digging yourself deeper into a hole? I think the answer is that you need to stop evaluating yourself purely by P&L and instead evaluate your trajectory. Are you becoming more disciplined, more process-driven? Are your mistakes shrinking and is your understanding of markets improving? Because if so, then often you are much closer than you think even if the money has not arrived yet. Most people quit trading too early because they mistake temporary failure for permanent inability. But there's also wisdom in recognizing when the pursuit itself is damaging your life more than enriching it. That balance is the hard part. Trading can be a brutal profession because it constantly forces you to confront reality. Sometimes that reality is telling you to walk away. Other times it's telling you that you might be only a few swings away from diamonds.
