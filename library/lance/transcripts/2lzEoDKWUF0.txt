Trading without a playbook is like going to the gym without a workout plan. You'll never know if what you're doing is working. So, here's how to build your own playbook from scratch. Step one, you want to find a setup that works for you. [music] It can be an earnings breakdown, a VWOP reclaim, or a trend day breakout. Once you pick one, take the time to understand exactly how it works. Step two, capture the context. Take note of what's happening around the setup, the trend, catalysts, sentiment, and who's trapped to know why it works. Step three, screenshot the trades and the [music] charts. You want to log the charts with entries, exits, stop levels, and notes to visually study how your setup behaves. Step four, grade the setup. Label it in A, B, or C, and write down the reasons and what specifically would make it higher quality next time. For example, if it was a B breakout setup, maybe the stock hadn't consolidated enough. Step five, refine the setup. Back test and forward test 10 to 20 or more similar setups. Track the win rate and reward and only size up if the data justifies it.
