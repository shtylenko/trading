Equities are number one because they're the best blend of opportunity count, opportunity quality, scalability, and accessibility while still being inefficient in the exact way traders can exploit it. Number of opportunities enormous. There's thousands of names, constant sector rotation, and a neverending calendar of catalysts, including earnings, guidance, analyst changes, product news, lawsuits, M&A, you name it. As for the quality of opportunities, this is the big one. Stocks are at the intersection of where sentiment and emotion consistently create mispricings. That is not a motivational quote. There is deep literature on investor sentiment impacting stock returns, especially in stocks that are hard to value and hard to arbitrage. While not as scalable as forex or many futures, it's still far more than enough liquidity for most traders. 99% of traders will never run into scalability issues. Fees. In modern US equities, commissions went to effectively zero at many brokers. With the economic model shifting to things like payment for order flow, broker safety bestinclass, especially relative to crypto and many retail Forex experiences, SIP protection exists at member broker dealers up to $500,000, including a $250,000 cash limit. Leverage more controlled margin exists, but it's not the 100x leverage and prey. Competition quality. Yes, equities have a lot of big players involved, but here's the catch. Single name equities are still packed with emotional decision-making around company specific news. Humans panic, humans chase, and other market structure nuances can result in amazing opportunities like those caused by index rebalances, short squeezes, and liquidations. That behavior creates tradable opportunity.
