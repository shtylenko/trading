# Inside Bar Breakout Strategy
## Used by: Lance Ippolito, Rayner Teo

### Overview
An Inside Bar forms when a candle trades entirely within the prior candle's range (coiling/consolidation). The breakout above the inside bar high triggers the entry. Clean, mechanical, effective.

### Identification
- Current high < previous high
- Current low > previous low
- Works on ALL timeframes
- Best on daily chart for swings

### Types (Strongest to Weakest)
1. **3-bar contraction:** Multiple inside bars getting tighter = strongest
2. **2-bar inside bar at support:** Inside bar forming AT a support level = very strong
3. **2-bar inside bar in uptrend:** Normal, reliable pattern
4. **Inside bar after pullback:** Shows consolidation after a dip = trend continuation

### Trend Context (Required)
- Stock must be in uptrend (higher highs/lows, 50 > 200 MA)
- Inside bar after trend pause or pullback = continuation
- In choppy/sideways market = avoid inside bars

### Entry Methods

**Standard Entry (Recommended):**
- Place buy stop at inside bar high + $0.05-0.10
- Stop loss: below inside bar low
- Enter when breakout triggers
- Confirmation: volume higher on breakout than inside bar

**Aggressive Entry:**
- Enter at the lower trendline of a multi-bar inside bar range
- Tighter stop, better R:R, higher failure risk

### Stop Loss
- Below inside bar low (1-3% typical)
- Below recent swing low (wider, safer)

### Profit Targets
- **Measured move:** Height of inside bar projected upward from breakout price
- **Prior resistance:** Next technical level
- **2:1 R:R minimum**

### Volume Confirmation
- During inside bar(s): Low volume (consolidation)
- On breakout: Higher volume (renewed interest)
- Breakout on low volume = false breakout risk

### Risk Management
- Risk 1% of account per trade
- Position size = (1% × Account) / stop distance per share
- Works best with 9/21 EMA bullish alignment

### Adapting for $25-100k
- Scan for inside bars on daily charts
- Use buy-stop orders to automate entry
- 3-5 positions

### Common Mistakes
1. Trading inside bars in choppy/trendless markets
2. Ignoring the higher timeframe trend
3. Inside bar too large (range > ATR = not a compression signal)
4. Breakout on low volume
