Step one, does the stop make strategic sense? Your stop should be placed at the point where you perceive expected value to be going negative. Expected value drives all of our trading decisions. Often the place where expected value goes negative is where our thesis or reason for being in the play is invalidated. For example, an initial stop on a breakout of resistance can often be if the stock gets back below the resistance level. Or in trending stocks, if our thesis is that the stock is going to keep trending higher, we would get out where the trend is invalidated. In other words, if the stock has been trending and making higher lows and higher highs, you would place your stop below the relative lows as that would signify the end of the trend. Same for if a stock is surfing a moving average. A sensical stop would be then if the stock closes below the moving average. This is also my go-to stop for trend lines.
