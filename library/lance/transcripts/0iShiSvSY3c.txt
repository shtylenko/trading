The question of how much do traders make is a loaded one. Trading, just like sports, is one of the most unequal professions in the world, which is what makes sports one of the best analogies I have to answer how much traders make. Trading is like trying to become a pro alete. Think about it. Anyone can try to become a trader, just like anyone can pick up a basketball or swing a bat. There's no degree required, no license to start, no hiring manager saying yes or no. But just because anybody can try it, doesn't mean anybody can make it. Here's the cold truth. Some studies say over 95% of people who try trading will never make a dime. They'll lose money. They'll churn through strategies. They'll bounce between hype and frustration until they eventually give up. It's the same as how most kids who dream of the NFL or NBA never get anywhere near the league. What about the people who do make it? Sure, some will go on to be stars, but most they just grind it out. They're the guys on the bench making the league minimum. In trading terms, that's the person making 100k, maybe 150k a year. They're profitable. They're legit, but it's not glamorous. Some years they'll make money, some years they'll lose money. That's the nature of the game. But then, just like in sports, you only ever hear about the stars.
