Human beings are wired to move the goal posts. We adapt to success incredibly quick. The thing you once dreamed about eventually becomes your baseline. And once it becomes your baseline, your brain immediately starts searching for the next level. This is one of the oldest psychological traps in human history. The pursuit itself never really ends because evolution programmed us to constantly seek more resources, more status, more security, and more advantage. You see this everywhere in life. The person making 50k year thinks life will be solved at 250k. The person making 250k thinks freedom starts at 2 million. Then the person worth 20 million compares themselves against somebody worth 200 million. I mean, look at this picture from F1 Monaco this past year. Every yacht owner there is probably jealous of the person next to them. The target constantly moves because comparison and adaptation are deeply embedded into our psychology. Researchers call this the hedonic treadmill. No matter how much your circumstances improve, your emotional baseline eventually resets and you start wanting more again. Trading magnifies this tendency to an extreme because performance is quantified every single day. There's always another P&L milestone, another hot market, and another trader making more than you.
