Today, the risk of unfathomable short squeezes is way higher. Back in 2021, I took my biggest loss ever in Avis Group, a boring multi-billion dollar car rental company, when it went up over a 100% in a few hours for pretty much no reason. Another example is the move we saw in the recent IPO Circle. This IPO, priced in the 30s, is a stable multi-billion dollar company, yet within a few months, it was up over 10x. We've also seen crazy short squeezes in liquid Chinese names and all sorts of funky stuff. The takeaway, never say that something can't happen. Risk management and daily stop- losses are more important than ever to survive in this
