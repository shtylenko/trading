Collect your 25 best and your 25 worst trades overall. The best way to do this is to screenshot both the intraday and daily charts and save them in organized notebooks or tag them appropriately. Then also write up all the trade details and P&L data. I commonly recommend that good detailed trade write-ups include much of the following: details of the setup category, market environment, an analysis of the daily and intraday chart, whether there is a news catalyst, as well as what your entry, exit, and target were. You can also analyze whether you sized the trade properly and what more nuanced factors were that might have made this trade better or worse than you first thought. It's also worth logging your emotional state. Were you on tilt or overtrading? Did any external psychological factors affect how you traded it? Then most importantly, what are the key takeaways from the trade? If you've been a good student, you've already written up in detail the top 25 best and top 25 worst trades of your year. If not, well, Santa will be giving you cold this year because you've been a naughty, naughty boy, haven't you? Okay, that got weird for a second, but seriously, you really need to perform a deep write up detailing each of the trades, the lessons learned from them, and how you can better handle those setups in the future.
