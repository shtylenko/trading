# Ross Cameron's Momentum Day Trading Strategy

## Objective

* Trade small-cap momentum stocks that are moving because of fresh news.
* Hold trades for seconds to several minutes.
* Focus on many small, consistent gains ("base hits") instead of waiting for huge winners.
* Protect capital through strict risk management.

---

# 1. Trading Hours

* Primary trading window: **7:00 AM–10:00 AM Eastern Time**.
* Focus on the market open because:

  * Most news is released before or shortly after the open.
  * Trading volume and volatility are highest.
  * Momentum is strongest.

---

# 2. Find Stocks Using a Market Scanner

Look for the **largest percentage gainers** of the day.

The stock should ideally meet these criteria:

* Up at least **10%** on the day.
* Relative volume at least **5× average daily volume**.
* Trading **between $2 and $20**.
* Float less than **10 million shares**.
* Moving because of a legitimate news catalyst.

---

# 3. News Catalysts

Preferred catalysts include:

* FDA approvals
* Positive clinical trial results
* Earnings surprises
* Government contracts
* AI announcements
* Cryptocurrency-related announcements
* Major partnerships
* Private placements
* Recent IPOs
* Reverse stock splits

He generally avoids stocks moving without a clear catalyst.

---

# 4. Supply and Demand

Ross believes explosive moves happen when:

* Demand is extremely high (heavy buying volume).
* Float is very low (limited supply of shares).

This combination can produce rapid price increases.

---

# 5. Chart Setup

After identifying a candidate:

* Open the 1-minute chart.
* Look for:

  * Strong uptrend
  * Clean price action
  * Consecutive bullish candles
* Avoid:

  * Choppy charts
  * Sideways movement
  * Erratic price action

---

# 6. Wait for a Pullback

He **does not chase the initial spike**.

Instead he waits for:

* Strong upward move
* Brief pullback (typically 1–2 candles)
* Buyers step back in

This is called the **micro pullback**.

---

# 7. Entry

Buy when:

* The first green candle appears after the pullback, or
* Price breaks above the high of the pullback.

This confirms buyers have regained control.

---

# 8. Stop Loss

Place the stop:

* Just below the low of the pullback.

Example:

* Entry: $4.20
* Pullback low: $4.10
* Risk: $0.10/share

---

# 9. Profit Target

Primary target:

* Retest of the day's high.

If momentum remains strong:

* Hold part of the position for further upside.

Otherwise:

* Take profits.

---

# 10. Reward-to-Risk Ratio

Only take trades where expected reward is at least **2× the risk**.

Example:

* Risk: $0.10/share
* Target: $0.20/share or more

Skip trades with poor reward-to-risk.

---

# 11. Volume Analysis

Look for:

* Strong buying volume
* Green volume bars larger than red ones

Avoid:

* Heavy selling volume
* Large red candles
* Signs of distribution

---

# 12. MACD Confirmation

He uses the MACD as a trend filter.

Preferred:

* Positive MACD
* Expanding momentum

Avoid:

* Negative MACD crossover
* Weakening momentum

---

# 13. Candlestick Analysis

Bullish signals:

* Strong green candles
* Bottoming tails
* Continuation candles

Bearish signals:

* Long upper wicks
* Topping tails
* Large bearish candles

---

# 14. Level II Analysis

He watches the order book for:

* Large buyers (support)
* Large sellers (resistance)

Large sell orders may cause him to avoid entering.

---

# 15. Time & Sales (The Tape)

He monitors:

* Whether trades execute at the bid or ask
* Aggressive buying
* Aggressive selling
* Large individual orders

This helps judge whether momentum is strengthening or weakening.

---

# 16. Position Sizing

Position size is based on maximum acceptable loss.

Formula:

**Position Size = Maximum Dollar Risk ÷ Risk per Share**

Example:

* Maximum loss: $500
* Risk: $0.10/share
* Position size: 5,000 shares

---

# 17. Scaling

He increases:

* Number of shares traded

He does **not** increase:

* Risk per share
* Profit targets

Example:

Instead of trying to make bigger moves, he trades more shares while still targeting roughly 15–20 cents.

---

# 18. Icebreaker Rule

At the beginning of each trading day:

* Start with **25% of normal position size**.
* Only increase to full size after earning the first profit milestone (he uses $1,000 in his own trading).

If early trades lose:

* Continue trading at reduced size.

Purpose:

* Prevent large losses early in the session.

---

# 19. Market Condition Filter

He classifies markets as:

### Hot Market

* Many stocks up 50–100%+
* Strong follow-through
* High trading volume
* Trade more aggressively

### Cold Market

* Few momentum stocks
* Weak follow-through
* Lower volume
* Reduce position size or don't trade

---

# 20. Daily Performance Review

After trading he reviews:

* Win rate
* Average winner
* Average loser
* Profit/loss ratio
* Accuracy
* Trade history
* Common mistakes

He believes reviewing data is essential for improvement.

---

# 21. Core Philosophy

His main principles are:

* Don't chase stocks.
* Wait for pullbacks.
* Keep losses small.
* Focus on consistency.
* Take many small winners instead of aiming for huge gains.
* Scale only after proving long-term consistency.
* Adapt position size to market conditions.

## Strategy Workflow

* Scan the biggest percentage gainers.
* Verify the stock meets the five selection criteria.
* Confirm there is a strong news catalyst.
* Wait for a clean micro pullback.
* Buy the breakout from the pullback.
* Place the stop below the pullback low.
* Target at least a 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio.
* Monitor Level II, Time & Sales, volume, and MACD.
* Take profits as momentum weakens or targets are reached.
* Review all trades after the session to improve execution.
