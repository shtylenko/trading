Rule number one. Only sell options after volatility has blown out, not in no [snorts] man's land where we are range bound and volatility is calm. That analogy I gave about selling home insurance before the catastrophe is a good one, which is why the best time to sell insurance is actually right after the catastrophe when everyone else has gotten banged up. After the California wildfires in 2025, most insurers stepped away from that market. The few that remained were selling insurance at astronomical rates. That is how we want to operate in the options market. You generally want to step in and sell options after fear has spiked and implied volatility has exploded higher. You want to sell options after the big once-in-a-decade move. It is paradoxical, but true that after the huge blowout move is actually when it is safest to do so because at sky-high implied volatility is actually offering you more risk cushion. But if you're selling options in sleepy, compressed, range-bound markets, no man's land conditions as I like to call it, when implied volatility is low, you're often collecting pennies in front of a steamroller. And eventually the steamroller arrives. So rule one is simple. Sell options during times of extreme moves and extreme sentiment. Never ever sell options during times of complacency. That is by far the most common mistake people make.
