# Range Breakout with Volume Confirmation
## Used by: Rayner Teo (5,024% over 31 years)

### Overview
A pure systematic breakout strategy. Identify stocks breaking above well-defined consolidation ranges on strong volume. Rayner Teo's 50-week Donchian breakout system returned 5,024% over 31 years.

### Rayner Teo's Proven System
- **Market Filter:** Russell 1000 above 100-week MA
- **Entry:** Monday open when stock closed above 50-week high
- **Exit:** Close below 40-week low (Donchian Channel on weekly)
- **Ranking:** Stocks with highest 50-week price appreciation
- **Position Sizing:** 5% per stock, max 20 positions
- **31-Year Track Record:** 5,024% net profit, 48.75% win rate, avg win 62.42%, avg loss -11.81%, 13.53% annual return, -37% max DD

### Consolidation Criteria
- Minimum 3-8+ weeks sideways movement
- Range should narrow (contracting volatility)
- 2-3+ touches of resistance level
- Volume contracts during consolidation

### Volume Requirements
- Breakout: **1.5-2x** average daily volume
- Weekly chart: volume higher than prior week
- Low-volume breakouts = high failure rate

### Entry Rules
- Close above resistance (daily or weekly)
- Volume confirms
- Enter next day open (Monday open for weekly system)
- Don't chase if 5%+ above breakout level

### Measured Move Target
- Consolidation height = resistance - support
- Target = breakout level + consolidation height

### Stop Loss Placement
- Below consolidation low (10-15% for weekly system)
- Below breakout candle low (tighter for daily system)
- For Rayner's system: close below 40-week low

### Risk Management
- Risk 1-2% per trade
- 5% per position in 20-position system
- For $25-100k: 5-8 positions, 10-15% each
- Diversify across sectors

### Scanner Setup (TradingView)
- Donchian Channel (50) on weekly
- Price above upper channel band
- Volume > 1.5x average (50-day)
- Market cap > $500M
- Price > $10, ADR > 3%

### Common Mistakes
1. Chasing breakouts already 10%+ above range
2. Breakout on low volume (no conviction)
3. Consolidation too short (< 3 weeks)
4. Trading in bear market (index < 200 MA)
