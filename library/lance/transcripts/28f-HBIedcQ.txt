One of the biggest myths in trading is this idea that you can casually make an extra few grand a month on the side with some effort and YouTube videos. That is not how trading works. Trading is not like a normal career path. It's much closer to trying to become a professional athlete. And this is where most people completely misunderstand the skill gap. People look at elite traders making millions and think, "Well, obviously they're gifted, but surely making 50k or 100k trading consistently isn't that hard." Wrong. The hardest jump in trading is not going from $100,000 to a million dollars. The hardest jump is going from zero dollars to consistently profitable at all. Because the moment you can consistently extract money from the market year after year, you are already operating at a very rare level of competency. That already puts you in the league. This reminds me of Brian Scalabrine's famous quote when people called him trash in the NBA. He said, "I'm closer to LeBron than you are to me." And that perfectly explains trading. The trader making 100k per year consistently is way closer to the trader making a million dollars per year than the struggling break-even trader is to making 100 grand. But most people don't realize this because they think consistency is normal. It's not. Consistency in trading is elite. And that's why so many people stay stuck.
