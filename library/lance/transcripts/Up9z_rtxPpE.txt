Here's a genuine question people should ask themselves about funded trader programs. If these firms actually wanted traders to win, why are so many profitable methods banned? Why ban trading news headlines or volatile instruments? Why ban overnight risk? Think carefully about that. Because when you make money at a real proprietary trading firm using technology, automation, or systematic edge, do you know what happens? They celebrate it. They pay you more. They allocate you more capital. They encourage you to scale it harder. Because real trading firms make money when skilled traders make money. At many funded trader programs, the business model often appears far more dependent on churn, resets, and failed challenges. They're monetizing trader behavior rather than actually cultivating profitable traders for the long term. That doesn't mean every funded trader program is a scam, but it does mean traders should think critically about incentives rather than blindly trusting influencer marketing.
