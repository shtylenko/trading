# Support and Resistance Bounce Strategy
## Used by: Rayner Teo (1M+ Subs), The Trading Geek

### Overview
The most fundamental price action strategy. Identify key support/resistance levels, wait for price to test them, and enter on the bounce with candle confirmation. Used by virtually every successful swing trader.

### Key Concept
- **Support:** Price level where buying pressure overcomes selling
- **Resistance:** Price level where selling pressure overcomes buying
- **Role reversal:** Broken resistance becomes support, broken support becomes resistance
- More touches = stronger level (2-3 minimum)

### Where to Find Levels
1. **Horizontal:** Prior swing highs and lows
2. **Diagonal:** Trendlines (connecting higher lows / lower highs)
3. **Moving averages:** 50, 200 SMA especially
4. **Round numbers:** Psychological levels ($50, $100, $500)
5. **Gaps:** Previous unfilled gap zones
6. **Volume nodes:** High-volume nodes from volume profile

### Level Strength Assessment
- More touches = stronger (2-3x = good, 4-5x = very strong)
- More time at level = stronger
- Volume at level matters (reversal on high volume = stronger)
- Multiple timeframe alignment = very strong

### Trend Context (Critical!)
- **Buy at support in uptrend** = highest probability
- **Buy at support in downtrend** = lowest probability (avoid)
- Always check the higher timeframe trend first

### Entry Methods

**Method 1: Aggressive (at support touch)**
1. Price touches identified support level
2. Look for reversal candle: hammer, bullish engulfing, pin bar
3. Enter at close of reversal candle
4. Risk per share = entry - stop distance

**Method 2: Conservative (on confirmation)**
1. Wait for price to touch support AND close back above
2. Look for a follow-through bullish candle
3. Enter on the follow-through day
4. Lower risk, slightly worse entry price

### Bounce Confirmation Candles
- **Hammer:** Long lower wick, small body at top
- **Bullish Engulfing:** Green candle fully covers prior red candle
- **Pin Bar:** Long wick showing rejection of lower price
- **Volume spike:** Volume on bounce > volume on previous 2-3 candles

### Stop Loss Placement
- **Just below the support level** (allow for wick = 1-2% buffer)
- More conservative: Below the swing low that preceded the level
- Never wider than ADR (Average Daily Range)

### Profit Targets
- **Target 1:** Next resistance above (1:2 R:R minimum)
- **Target 2:** Measured move of the bounce
- **Target 3:** High timeframe resistance

### Risk Management
- Risk 1-2% of account per trade
- Minimum 2:1 R:R (3:1 preferred)
- Scale out: 50% at target 1, trail remainder

### Adapting for $25-100k Account
- Focus on SPY, QQQ, IWM for ETF S/R trades (lower risk)
- Individual stocks at well-known support zones
- Use limit orders at support levels (not market)
- 3-5 positions, each 2-5% of account

### Common Mistakes
1. Buying at support in a downtrend (catching falling knife)
2. Not waiting for candle confirmation (bounce might fail)
3. Stop too tight (wick takes it out before bounce)
4. Support with only 1 touch (unreliable)
5. No higher timeframe confirmation
