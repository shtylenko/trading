# Fibonacci Retracement Pullback Strategy
## Used by: TraderNick, Rayner Teo, The Trading Geek

### Overview
Fibonacci levels identify likely pullback endpoints in trending stocks. In uptrends, 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8% retracements act as support. Enter on bounce with candle confirmation.

### Key Levels
**Pullback (Entry) Levels:** 38.2% (shallow), 50% (common), 61.8% (golden ratio - strongest)
**Extension (Target) Levels:** 127.2%, 161.8% (most common target), 261.8% (extended)

### Drawing Correctly (Uptrend)
From **swing LOW → swing HIGH** of the most recent impulse. Wait for pullback to begin before drawing.

### Entry Rules
1. Identify uptrend (higher highs/lows, rising EMAs)
2. Draw Fib from impulse low to impulse high
3. Wait for pullback into 38.2-61.8% zone
4. Look for reversal candle (hammer, bullish engulfing, pin bar)
5. Volume spike on bounce = confirmation
6. Enter on close of confirmation candle

### Confluence (THE KEY TO SUCCESS)
**Strongest setups have 2+ levels at same price:**
- Fib 61.8% + 50 EMA = extremely strong
- Fib 50% + horizontal support = very strong
- Fib 61.8% + prior swing low = very strong
- Fib + VWAP = strong institutional level

### Stop Loss
- Below next Fib level below entry (e.g., below 61.8% if buying at 50%)
- Below the swing low (wider, safer)
- Standard risk: 2-5%

### Profit Targets
- **T1:** Prior high (top of impulse)
- **T2:** 127.2% extension
- **T3:** 161.8% extension (most common target)

### Risk Management
- Minimum 2:1 R:R
- Risk 1% of account per trade
- Only in trending markets (ADX > 25)
- Avoid in choppy/sideways conditions

### Adapting for $25-100k
- SPY/QQQ on daily chart for Fib pullback entries
- Individual stocks: liquid, >$10, rising EMAs
- Use limit orders at Fib levels

### Common Mistakes
1. Wrong swing points in Fib calculation
2. Ignoring trend context (buying Fib in downtrend)
3. No candle confirmation at Fib level
4. Stop too tight (wick takes it out)
