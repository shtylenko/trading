You can recover from any mistake except the one that takes you out of the game. Every trader who's lasted in this business takes measures to make sure it's never game over. What might some of those be? Some traders wire out a certain percent of profits. Yes, doing so will slow your growth, but if you're doing well and growing, you'll end up where you need to be eventually. Most traders make the mistake of trying to grow too fast, and most traders would benefit from being far more conservative than they think. All traders should keep a rainy day fund. I know this sounds extreme, but I genuinely believe traders should work towards building reserves that'll cover them at least 2 years and ideally even build up a whole other capital base invested in safe assets where if, god forbid, one account blows up, you can always reload. The saying that you only need to get rich once is so true. But as traders addicted to growth and a silly scorecard, many fail to live by that motto.
