Another area I would try to initially avoid if I were starting over is the beginners obsession with micro caps. Many new traders love micro caps because they see these crazy percentage moves. A stock goes up 50, 100, or 300% in a day and suddenly everybody thinks that's the holy grail. They think to themselves, I only have a thousand dollars so I need something that can double my capital quickly. That mindset is what gets people destroyed. You should not optimize for percentage moves. You should optimize for expected value. And if you're new, chances are you need to learn what expected value is. Now look, micro caps are an incredible area of the market full of opportunities. I know traders that have made their whole career trading them, but they're also one of the most dangerous areas, particularly if you're shorting them. Micro caps come with halt risk, weird dilution dynamics, and you can encounter a ton of slippage trading them. Beginners look at the upside without respecting the downside, and that's amateur thinking. Professionals ask, "What happens if I'm wrong?" And in small caps, being wrong can get very expensive very quickly.
