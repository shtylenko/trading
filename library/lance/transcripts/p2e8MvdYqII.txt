If I were starting over with only a thousand dollars, here's the truth. I would stop obsessing over that thousand dollars entirely. I'd focus all my energy on building a repeatable edge. I'd trade in simulation, I'd journal relentlessly, and I'd avoid forcing urgency onto the process. I'd understand that trading is a skill that compounds money over long periods of time. And you want to know what I think of those small account challenges and the beginner traders making thousands of percent? They're just a flash in the pan. Every single one of those traders that I've seen get hired ends up becoming a nobody. Why? Because those traders are not learning the right mindset. And often what they're doing isn't even scalable for any real amount of capital. Sure, it might get you some likes on social media, but it is not a real career. The people who survive in this business are not the people who post crazy beginner returns. They're the people who stay in the game long enough to become truly exceptional. And once you become exceptional, the money part will take care of itself.
