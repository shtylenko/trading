Most traders don't understand this, but aligning multiple time frames can completely shift your chances of making money. Here's a breakdown. Number one, you got the daily time frame. This is where you see the big trend, major levels, and if the stock is truly in play, so that your intraday trades align with the bigger move. This ensures you're not fighting the trend and that every trade has a higher chance of working. Number two, the 60 or 30 minute time frame. This is where you see multi-day moves, key zones, and whether today's action fits the overall trend. This helps you trade with the bigger picture again, but instead of getting chopped up by random intraday noise. Number three, the 15-minute time frame. This time frame is useful if you want to start to zone in on the intraday price action. Have we been strong or weak? Are we above or below VWAP? Number four, the 5-minut time frame. Many intraday traders will use this time frame to execute their trades and execute trades off of this chart. It's granular enough for most day traders. Number five, and the one I use the most, the twominut time frame. This is by far my preferred time frame. No time frame is special, but this has always been the right level of granularity for my style of trading, especially for doing things like catching the right side of the V for an intraday reversal. I like catching that next leg over the following minutes rather than trying to catch the leg that unfolds over hours.
