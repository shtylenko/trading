If I'm down, I should try to recover my losses right away. >> False. That's revenge trading and it usually turns a bad day into a worse one. >> You need to predict where price is going to be a good trader. >> False. Good traders bet on probabilities, not predictions. It's the same way poker players don't know the outcome of any given hand. >> Good traders focus more on risk than being right. >> True. [music] If risk is controlled, being wrong doesn't knock you out of the game. No trader is right all the time. >> More trades usually means more money. >> False. Overtrading usually leads to more mistakes, not better results. More money only comes from taking more great setups. >> You can lose money on a good trade and make money on a bad one. >> That is absolutely true. The same way poker players can lose with pocket aces, outcomes do not define quality, your process does. >> Once you find the right strategy, trading becomes easy. >> False. [music] Trading never, ever becomes easy. Fake influencers are the only ones that ever say trading is easy. >> People should follow you to learn trading without all the hype. >> True. Because trading is a skill, not a shortcut.
