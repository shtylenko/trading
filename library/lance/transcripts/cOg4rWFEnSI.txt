TIGR, which is a stock brokerage, really exploded higher. On October 7th, the stock gapped up significantly. The equity was worthwhile for shorting, but the options were also incredible. That day, I sold the October 18th, 2024 call option with a $16 strike for almost $2. That is the advantage of waiting for the implied volatility to really blow out. The stock had already nearly quadrupled. Yet, while the stock was at $14, you could give yourself another $4 cushion. It isn't that the stock couldn't have gone higher. Of course, it could have. My point is though that the probabilities were far in my favor, and should I have ended up getting assigned short the stock, that would have been a position I wanted anyways. Those that follow my trading course will recognize this as one of my favorite exhaustion gap technical patterns. Remember how in rule two, I mentioned that little trick of annotating my options break-even on the chart so that I can visually see what the move would look like? Well, look where the $18 break-even is on this chart. Yep, for just over a week of exposure, I like selling those calls very much.
