You must be able to focus on size without letting it alter your system or change your psychology behind your entries and exits. If you are confident in your skill set to the point of being able to focus consciously on your size without letting it change the expected value of your trade, only then can we now proceed. So, what is exponential bet sizing? In a nutshell, it's being able to scale your bet size dramatically with respect to the opportunity or the expected value of the play. There are a few variables that go into doing this successfully. And the first is having a proper understanding of your trades and the grading and sizing system to go along with it. Personally, I'd have trades graded across the scale of A, B, C, and D. And here's how they would differentiate. An A trade, at least for me, occurs one to two times per month. These are memorable, amazing, standout trades that you end up looking back on at the end of the year. These are the trades that you really want to go big on and often bet exponentially if your trading skill allows for it. If I knew in the moment I was just in a B trade, I would try to push it and get $10,000 worth of risk. But, if all of a sudden I realized this was transitioning into something special, that it might be the best trade all month, I knew I'd have to push my sizing to be much larger. How would I define that? If liquidity allowed for it, I'd be pushing for an exponential increase and go up to maybe say a hundred grand worth of risk.
