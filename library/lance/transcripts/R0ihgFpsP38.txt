Everyone says you need to work on your mindset to become a profitable trader, but that's not where 99% of traders need to start. Finding edge is much more important. So, here's how to find yours. Step one, identify your setups. Write down the patterns you take, what they look like, how you find them, and the conditions that must be present. Because documenting them helps you repeat only the setups that actually make you money. Step two, playbook these setups for each strategy. List the criteria, ideal entries, stops, and exits for each setup so that you can use each framework repeatedly. Step three, back test and collect data. Get 10 to 20 past examples per setup to record the win rate, mistakes, and conditions to see if the setups actually work. This is just a start. You want to keep building your database and the data set over time. Step four, trade small or trade in a demo account to test these strategies. You want to run the setup live with small size, maybe just like 0.25 or even half a percent of your account or ideally just in a demo account so that you can see how it behaves in real time and track the results. Step five, analyze your performance. This is where you check whether you followed the rules appropriately and then you start to assess whether the data proves the strategy viable. You also want to see whether you made any mistakes or any losses came from user error. This is going to help you learn from each trade and improve your decision-making over time.
