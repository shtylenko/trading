Title: One of the biggest mistakes traders make 🚫
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=XF4FGM7cbVQ
======================================================================

One of the biggest mistakes traders make is taking massive risk in both their career and personal life at the same time. That combination destroys people. Discretionary trading is already an extremely high variance profession. Because of that, your personal life should become more conservative, not less. This is one of the most important frameworks I believe in. For every additional unit of career risk you take, you should generally take less risk elsewhere in life. That means living well below your means, keeping a large emergency cash reserve, and investing conservatively outside your trading account. You want to avoid unnecessary financial pressure. Why? Because external pressure destroys your decision-making. If your mortgage, [music] lifestyle, or family obligations require you to make money trading every month, you are no longer trading freely. That pressure can take talented traders completely out of the game. This is why I think the barbell approach to risk is so important. Take calculated aggression where you actually have edge, or trading, but offset that with stability and conservatism everywhere else. The goal is longevity, because survival is what allows edge and compounding to eventually work in your favor. Follow me for more trading lessons.
