Mean reversion scalps occur after price [snorts] stretches too far, too fast. You are stepping in when a motion peaks and order flow begins to shift. That shift is visible in the tape before it's obvious on the chart. On a sharp down move into support, you will often see a moment where volume stalls and it's this pause where people stop hitting the bid. That pause is often the hesitation of sellers to push their hand and signifies a turn in momentum. As a scalper, you then take the offer and get long in front of that support level using the bids as at the support as your protection. While then seeking to capture the bounce we get off that level. Often these mean reversion scalps work best when the stock quickly and sharply shoots down to a support level with some massive bids or quickly and sharply shoots up to a resistance level with large offers. The sharper and bigger the move into really big bids or offers, the better it is to scalp a reversal.
