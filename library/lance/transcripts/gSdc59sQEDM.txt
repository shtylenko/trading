Pros use MACD as momentum gauge, not a magic signal. If price is trending up and the MACD line is above zero with growing bars, that confirms strength. The runner is not just ahead, he's speeding up. One powerful use of MACD is spotting divergence. Price makes a new high, but MACD makes a lower high. That shows the uptrend is losing strength under the surface. Price makes a new low, but MACD makes a higher low. That shows sellers are losing strength. Divergence doesn't mean you blindly fade the trend, but it tells you the current trend might be near exhaustion and you should tighten risk or look for a reversal setup using price action. MACD gets much more useful when you combine it with traditional technical setups. For example, if a stock is holding above VWAP, breaks out of a strong resistance level on big volume, and MACD is expanding above the zero line, that's a very different signal than MACD crossing in the middle of a choppy range with no confirmation anywhere else. There are also situations where MACD should be ignored completely, like ultra choppy range bound markets. In those cases, MACD will whipsaw back and forth, giving you endless crossovers with no follow-through. All that does is add stress and volatility to your P&L.
