Before you quit your day job, here's a framework you need from skill set to mindset to savings to runway to make it happen. Much like analyzing a trade, we can think of this decision in terms of expected value. Not just the expected value financially, but also emotionally. It's worth doing the self-reflecting on these variables. What is your current salary and future earnings potential? Also known as your opportunity cost versus your earnings potential as a trader. What is your job safety like versus your odds of success as a trader? What is the cost of failure? How reversible is the decision? Can you immediately get your old job back? And most of all, how unhappy are you at your current job versus how passionately do you want to trade? Let me add examples to some of those considerations I mentioned. If you're currently making a million bucks as a doctor, your opportunity cost is far higher than if you were a student. If you're supporting a family, your cost of failure would be far higher than if you're living with your parents. If you're one year away from a 10-year teaching position or a big pension, the job security and financial security trade-off could be huge. Additionally, if you hate your job, staying might not be the right option regardless of pay. Similarly, if you love your current job, trading might just be best as a hobby, and there is nothing wrong with that. It's also worth gauging how much you have saved up, what your expenses are, and whether you ideally can give yourself a 2 plus year runway.
