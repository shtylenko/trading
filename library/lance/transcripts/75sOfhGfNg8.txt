You don't become a profitable trader just by trading. You do it by finding an edge. And I'm going to tell you how. Step one, back testing. You want to run your setup through past data, log every instance, and check if the strategy generated positive expected value. That means that on average, it makes money over time so that you know it's a good setup to trade. Step two, forward testing. This is where you trade setups that you encounter going forward as a test, either in a demo account or using very small risk. You want to track your entries, exits, outcomes, and the context of the trade so that you build your very own data set. Step three, predictive variables. What you want to do here is reverse engineer what variables in a trade lead to being able to better predict what the chart does next. Step four, structural edge. You want to reflect on what reasons you might have for being able to beat the market. Perhaps you have better analytical skills or take advantage of opportunities when other players are getting liquidated or margin called. Step five, you then want to combine all of these methods.
