Title: How to actually learn how to trade 💸

Here's how to actually learn trading, coming from someone who has spent over a decade on Wall Street. Number one, before you even think about setups, look if the stock is moving from actual news. Earnings, mergers, investor stakes, or just everyday order flow. Number two, read major headlines and check the timestamps so you know what just changed. When a stock breaks out with news behind it, don't fight the trend. News-driven moves tend to keep going. Number three, if you can't find a fresh headline, treat the move as just moving on technicals, but be cautious. If the market makes a huge move for no reason, it's more likely to snap back. These technical moves can reverse because nothing fundamentally changed. Number four, pick one free tool or platform and make it your default information source. The pros tend to use Bloomberg, but you can use free tools like X by searching the ticker or cheap services like briefing.com. Number five, tag every trade before logging it. Before logging a trade, mark whether it was driven by fresh news or not to keep your data clean. Follow to learn more trading insights from somebody who actually worked on Wall Street.
