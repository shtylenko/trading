Survival matters more than any single hot streak. The traders who build real long-term wealth are usually not the traders who maximize every single opportunity cycle perfectly. They're the traders who are okay struggling back in the late stages of a market cycle if that means it'll help them survive enough cycles to keep compounding over decades. Avoiding catastrophic givebacks is essential to trader longevity. This means staying humble while everyone else becomes euphoric. At the highest levels, trading becomes less about finding setups and more about managing yourself correctly while opportunities fluctuate around you. The market environment will always change. The question is whether your psychology adapts before the market forces humility onto you. Because eventually every hot streak ends. The traders who survived are the ones who prepared for that reality before it arrived instead of after.
