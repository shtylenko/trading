So, how do we escape the cycle and break this period of booms and busts? Here are my suggestions. First, we want to develop a set of habits and best practices that are sustainable in good times and bad. What are the essential trading practices you need to do each day that won't burn you out or won't get skipped on a busy or tough day? It needs to be easy enough that it gets done, but difficult enough to help you continue to make progress. Perhaps a daily report card, a weekly review, and video watching. Or for the dieters, what are the essential foods you must avoid eating on a stressful day or while vacationing? Sometimes it's easier to start with eliminating bad practices to move forward rather than to constantly implement new ones. Whether it is hiring or budgeting, working out or trading, what are the minimum habits that you can do to always stay consistent and have some standard bar that you can hit? You want to make these habits as frictionless as possible. That often means making them fun. That means automating decisions or even removing any easy outs. All these habits need to be as thoughtless and enjoyable to execute as possible. Sometimes that means having a daily report card that includes just charts and no writing. Sometimes that means having non-p perishable, healthy snacks always on hand. Don't get me wrong, there will always be highs and lows, periods of more motivation and periods of less. Having these minimum standards that you stick with no matter what's happening in your life ends up dampening the negative amplitude of the boom bus cycle.
