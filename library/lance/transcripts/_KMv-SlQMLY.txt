What? Selling when the market is crashing? Three out of 10. That is emotional trading, not strategic trading. Generally, [music] we want to be buying during times of panic, except one main exception, which is that risk rules always come first. If you're trading without a stop or past your risk limits, get out. Diversifying among asset classes. Four out of 10. It's great for investors, but traders, [music] like poker players, generally do best by betting big on their best opportunities to win. That said, unlike poker players, we almost never want to go all in and want to always live to trade another day. Trading during major news event. >> Eight out of 10. Breaking news offers some of the most lucrative opportunities in markets. That is because breaking news can lead to large moves and tons of volatility that traders thrive in. But if you don't have a system to trade it, it's best to avoid. >> Adding to a losing position. >> Two out of 10. For most traders, averaging down is a dangerous mistake. One of my favorite sayings is that our best trades immediately go in our favor, so we want to be careful of when they don't. >> Taking a break after a losing streak. Nine out of 10. Resetting your mindset is underrated. Trading well starts with mental clarity, and if your psychology is leading to poor decisions, taking a break can be best.
