Depending on the severity of your hole, you might need to reduce your size, your lockout, or totally stop trading altogether while taking some days off. Almost every horror story I hear about stems from one fact: the trader did not have any predetermined loss limits. Every day trader should have a daily loss limit, and every swing trader should also have weekly or monthly loss limits. In an ideal world, these need to be instituted at your broker so that you cannot overrule them in the moment. Good brokers that help traders succeed allow you to set daily loss limits and help protect you from your worst self. Again, having some form of loss limit as a trader is non-negotiable. We all learn this the hard way, though. Kyle Williams, an amazing seven-figure trader that I'm privileged to be friends with, also had to learn it the hard way. David Handlin, another amazing seven-figure trader that I'm friends with, had to learn it the hard way, as well. There is an endless amount of failed traders that never got a second chance because they told themselves these were not necessary. They are. If you think otherwise, you just have not found out the hard way yet.
