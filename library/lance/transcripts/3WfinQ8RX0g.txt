Not to flex, but I'm pretty freaking good at trading. Here's exactly how I build the trading playbook that helped me make over a hundred million dollars in verified profits. Step one, reverse engineer the best charts. Document stocks making the biggest moves. Log the setup and daily and intraday chart, time of day, catalyst, entry and exit, risk, and how you felt. Your log is your data. Data tells you what actually works. Step two, grade the setups. Score each setup A+ to [music] D based on how good you think that trade was. Judge the setup, not just the outcome. Study the nuances that make some setups better than others. Step three, build a trading playbook. Categorize the charts into specific plays with entries, exits, and other important nuances. Review a few images daily. Patterns repeat. Your eyes learn fast. Step four, simplify monthly. Once a month, delete what doesn't pay and tighten your rules into oneliners. Update your playbook doc so it's short, clear, and easy to follow. Step five, scale only A+ plays. When an A+ setup shows positive expectancy over 20 plus trades, try sizing up 10 to 20%. Never ever add to losers. Add only on confirmation when the trades are working. Most traders wing it.
