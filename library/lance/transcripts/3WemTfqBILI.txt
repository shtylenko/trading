What should you use instead of fibs? Let me give you the real playbook I use, which is a general framework for viewing retracements. And this also ties into why I said earlier that the 50% retracement is actually important to me. So why is that? By framing market movements around a 50% retracement, we can then have a standard to view price action against. An 80% retracement of a down move would be a strong bounce. A 20% retracement would be a very weak bounce. A stock retracing 80% of a down move would be a stronger stock, while a stock retracing 20% of a down move would be a weaker stock. Of course, at the most extreme levels, we get a 100% retracement and a 0% retracement. Both of these extremes are often huge indicators in themselves. Remember when stock markets retraced the Trump tariffs in spring of 2025 and then kept bouncing? That was a sign of strength. Similarly, for continuation plays, an 80% pullback would be a deep pullback and a 20% pullback would be a shallow pullback. A stock that is only pulling back 20% of its breakout would be a stronger stock while a stock that is pulling back 80% of its breakout would be a weaker stock. Same here. At the most extreme levels, we get a 100% retracement and a 0% retracement. Both of these extremes are again often huge indicators in themselves.
