You've been sold this idea that selling options is steady, safe income. That's because options trading influencers are lying to you, and what I'm about to share could save you from losing all your money. I want you to think of selling options much like selling home insurance. Most of the time, the insurance company will pocket the premium, and at first glance, it looks like they win. That is a premise behind selling options. The influencers are quick to highlight that you can consistently capture options premium by selling options. But using the insurance analogy, what happens to the insurance company if all of the sudden there is a massive wildfire or hurricane? They spent years collecting premium only to have to pay out a massive loss in the event that the fire or hurricane destroys a bunch of homes. Insurance companies only win over the long run if they price the insurance high enough to more than cover all the occasional, but severe losses. That is the key question to always ask yourself when selling options. Is the premium you receive priced high enough to offset the inevitable big loss in the future?
