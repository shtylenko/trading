prior uptrend first the stock should have experienced an uptrend of at least 30 percent this indicates strong market performance second cup formation after the uptrend the stock pulls back to form the left side of the cup profit taking and selling pressure creates a temporary downtrend third cup bottom the bottom of the cup is formed when selling pressure eases and institutional investors step in to support the stock the right side of the base is then formed as the stock starts climbing higher fourth handle formation within the upper half of the cup a handle forms this represents weaker holders who bought near the previous uptrend speak they seek to minimize their losses causing a slight decline in price importantly the volume during the handle formation should be light indicating that committed investors are holding on to their shares let's take a look at an example to see the cup with handle pattern in action in 2020 Dixon Technologies exhibited this pattern Dixon had a significant prior uptrend followed by a pullback that formed the left side of the cup the stock found support at the Cup's bottom suggesting a potential Turning Point as it climbed higher the right side of the base formed accompanied by the development of a handle the ideal buy point for the cup with handle pattern is in the handles peak in Dixon's case it was rupees 957.96 pay close attention to the volume during the Breakout Dixon broke past the ideal buy point with higher volume than average this signaled strong institutional buying interest and presented an opportunity for investors to
