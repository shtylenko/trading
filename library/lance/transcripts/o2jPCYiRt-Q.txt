If your Instagram page is filled with videos like this, you've probably thought trading looks so easy. But after trading for [music] 15 years, I can tell you it's not. Most of those videos skip the boring parts, risk rules, journaling, and all that review time because those don't go viral. The reality is trading is not easy money. It is a skill that punishes shortcuts. I only became profitable when I treated trading like a process instead of gambling. That meant fewer trades, tighter rules, and constant review even when it wasn't exciting. So this is how I actually structure my trading. Number one, trading rewards discipline, not motivation. Showing up tired, bored, or emotional without rules gets you punished fast in the markets. Number two, you do not get paid for activity. You get paid for execution with edge. More trades usually mean more mistakes, not more money. Number three, learning comes before earning. Skipping training to chase profits is why most traders never last.
