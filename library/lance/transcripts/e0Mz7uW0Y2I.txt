What's the difference between an unprofitable trader and a profitable one? Most people who struggle with trading aren't bad at analysis. They're stuck [music] thinking like analysts instead of traders. Analysts focus on understanding why something should happen. This looks like long explanations, detailed charts, and waiting for certainty. Traders, on the other hand, focus on probabilities and what to do next. They usually define risk first, then act when conditions line up. That leads to more accurate decisions and less emotional attachment. [music] As a trader who's made over a hundred million dollars in profits, I didn't rely on predictions. I relied on execution and risk management. That said, great traders [music] still analyze.
