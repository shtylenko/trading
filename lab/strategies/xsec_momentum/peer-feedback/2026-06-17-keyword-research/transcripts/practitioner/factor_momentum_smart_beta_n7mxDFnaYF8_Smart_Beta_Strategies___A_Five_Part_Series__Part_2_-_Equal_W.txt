equal weight is an investment strategy in which a portfolio assigns an equal weight to each security within a selected universe this strategy can be applied to any selection of securities regardless of size geography or other selection criteria an equal weighted portfolio offers enhanced diversification in its risk return profile relative to a market cap weighted portfolio of the same universe the strategy can be a useful resource for investors seeking exposure to a selection of securities without over exposing themselves to the largest securities within the selected universe performance of equal weighted strategies are most often compared to their market cap weighted counterparts and the disparity is largely driven by the over weighting of the largest equities in a market cap weighted portfolio in other words the relative performance of an equal weighted portfolio reflects the relative performance of its largest components equal weighting strategies are usually employed in the context of highly liquid markets given that smaller equities tend to experience lower trading volumes it is important to consider the feasibility of moving in and out of large positions in the comparatively small equities when constructing an equal weighted portfolio given sufficient liquidity equal weighting investing can provide investors with a mix of well balanced exposure to the equity markets while broadly broadening portfolio risk beyond that which can be achieved with a market cap weighted strategy you
