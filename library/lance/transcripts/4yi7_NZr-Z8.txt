If I only had 30 days to make you a more profitable trader, here's exactly what I would do to make it happen. Step one, focus on improvement. I'd spend the first few days reviewing your mistakes and finding ways to optimize your daily process. A strong daily process helps you maximize how fast you can learn. Step two, stick to in-play tickers. I'd restrict your trading to only using tickers with real volume, volatility, and ideally breaking news so that you don't waste your energy trading random noise. For example, you would trade tickers with big earnings movers or stocks with fresh catalysts. Step three, refine your easy money setups. I'd help you choose two to [music] three replicable plays where you have well-defined risk and worked on gradually increasing your size if the data supports it. For example, focusing on simple breakouts or slow-moving reversal plays. Step four, build strict loss limits. I'd help you set clear daily, weekly, and monthly loss limits and actually enforce them so you avoid drawdowns that can spiral you in a bad place. Step five, review and always stay improving. I'd make you journal what you did well each day, where you slipped up, and what you should adjust at the end of each day for your long-term growth.
