The job never gets easy. I still have draw downs in a normal year. I mean, I once lost $2 million in 60 minutes. Seriously, you constantly battle regret. You dwell on the trades you butchered, the ones you missed, the ones you mised. Every single day could have been a killer day if you had just done XYZ differently. And that loop, that whatif loop plays in your head forever. And worst of all, say goodbye to peace of mind. I check the markets before bed, at the gym, at dinner. First thing when I wake up, I'm always watching, always plugged in, always managing risk. You're never truly off. You always wonder if a headline's going to drop, if your position is imploding, if some market you're exposed to is about to crater. That is a reality. So, yeah, trading might look sexy on the surface. It might offer freedom, leverage, and unlimited upside. But behind the curtain is a lifestyle filled with stress, loneliness, obsessive monitoring, constant self-doubt, and the very real possibility that you'll walk away with nothing.
