You think trading is just about price action and indicators? Well, it's not. It's about building the right routines, rules, and strategies that will help you profit in the long term. Here's how to get started. Step number one, fully grasp the trade-off of this job. Before you ever quit a normal job, compare your salary to likely $0 in trading income. Then divide your savings by your monthly expenses. If you don't have 24 months of runway, I highly recommend you don't yet quit. Step two, build your skills early on. When you're still employed, treat trading as a second job. Schedule weekly sessions on the charts, track your metrics, and focus on consistency. This builds discipline and shortens your learning curve. Step three, stress test your runway. Plan for making zero money for 12 to 24 months. Run scenarios where you lose, stagnate, or break even. Then see if your savings can handle it. Step four, if possible, see if you can transition gradually. Go part-time first and use this period to test whether your passion grows under pressure or completely collapses. Step five, develop real trader habits. Make sure you're doing daily reviews, build a playbook, start your trade journal, and build emotional discipline to help manage the shock of losing your paycheck safety net.
