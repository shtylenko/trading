What is an opening range break strategy? An opening range break or ORB is exactly what it sounds like. You define a range during the opening period of the trading day, generally the first 30 minutes or so of trading, and then you trade the break of that range once price confirms momentum through it. To define the range, you would take the wick of the high and the wick of the low during that time period. If price breaks above the opening range highs, momentum traders pile in long. If price breaks below the lows, momentum traders pile in short. It sounds almost stupidly simple, which is exactly why most people dismiss it before understanding the underlying psychology.
