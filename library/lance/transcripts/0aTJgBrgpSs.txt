Title: There’s no magic indicator 🚨
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=0aTJgBrgpSs
======================================================================

In trading, there's no magic indicator that's going to tell you when to buy and sell. [music] So, instead of relying on random indicators, let me show you a learning process that actually works. Step one, stop obsessing over your P&L. Don't let your P&L determine if you traded well. Instead, judge yourself based on whether you followed your trading plan and improved that day. Step two, know the type of trading day. Some days the market gives big opportunities, others it gives nothing. Take that into consideration instead of just the outcome of the day. Make sure you adapt your risk and how much you trade to how active the market is for your playbook. Step three, learn from the bad days. When you lose, treat it like precious feedback. Find one mistake to fix instead of being emotional. For example, if you jumped into a trade too fast, write it down and set rules to wait for your signal next time. Step four, focus on what you can control. You can't control whether the market goes up or down, but you can control your plan, your entries, exits, and behavior by writing your rules ahead of time or logging out of your trading account when you begin trading emotionally. Step five, take intentional actions to improve every single day. Stop trying to win big. You want to try to improve little by little every day so your long-term results get better no matter what the training day looks like. Aim for consistent positive days as your target first. Then you can look towards scaling.
