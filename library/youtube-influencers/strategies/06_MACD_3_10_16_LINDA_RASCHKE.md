# MACD 3-10-16 / 3/10 Oscillator Strategy — Linda Bradford Raschke
## Market Wizard — Former Floor Trader

### Overview
Linda Bradford Raschke, featured in Jack Schwager's "New Market Wizards" (1992), developed the **3/10 Oscillator** — a faster version of MACD using (3, 10, 16) simple moving averages instead of (12, 26, 9) exponential. This captures swing momentum more responsively.

### Settings
**Indicator Setup (TradingView/ThinkorSwim):**
- MACD with: Fast Length = 3, Slow Length = 10, Signal Smoothing = 16
- Use **Simple** (SMA) for all three, not Exponential (EMA)
- Fast line = 3 SMA - 10 SMA  
- Signal line = 16-period SMA of the fast line
- Histogram = Fast line - Signal line

### Supporting Indicators
- **14-period ADX** > 30 confirms strong trend
- **20-period EMA** for dynamic support/resistance
- Price action for candlestick confirmation

### Core Patterns

**1. Holy Grail Setup (Most Reliable)**
Condition: ADX > 30 (strong trend) + 3/10 made new momentum high + price pulls back to 20 EMA
Long: ADX > 30 + new momentum high + pullback to 20 EMA → Buy on bounce
Stop: Below the 20 EMA or below the last swing low in 3/10 oscillator
Target: Prior highs / next resistance

**2. First Cross Pattern**
Buy when trend line first crosses above zero AND 3/10 oscillator then pulls back below zero
This is a "failure test" — first retracement after trend reversal
Condition: Identifies potential reversal from downtrend to uptrend

**3. Intermediate Buy/Sell**
Price has been above 20 EMA + trend (slow) line above zero for multiple bars
Price retraces to 20 EMA ON same bar as 3/10 oscillator retraces below zero
Best when accompanied by shallow bull/bear flag on bar chart

**4. Anti Pattern** (for trading ranges)
Occurs when slope of trend line has just turned positive (may be above or below zero)
Fast line pulls toward trend line BUT price does NOT give up ground
Expect move equal in size to preceding move
Works in range-bound markets

### Multiple Timeframe Progression
When the market breaks out of a range and makes new momentum highs:
1. First pullback forms on **15-min chart** at 20-period EMA
2. Then on **30-min chart**
3. Then **60-min chart**
4. Then **120-min chart**
Each is a separate entry opportunity with tighter risk each time

### Risk Management
- Stop loss: At the last swing high/low of the 3/10 oscillator
- Alternate: Volatility-based stop (1.5-2x ATR)
- Time stop: Exit if position doesn't work within expected timeframe
- Account risk: 1-2% per trade

### Profit Targets
- First target: Prior high or resistance level
- Second target: Measured move of the pattern (flagpole projection)
- Use trailing stop once in profit

### Scanner Setup
Look for stocks with:
- ADX(14) > 30 (strong trend in either direction)
- Price above 20-period EMA (for longs)
- 3/10 oscillator pulling back toward zero line from above

### Adapting for $25-100k Account
- Best on daily charts for swing trades on liquid stocks
- Works on indices (SPY, QQQ) for lower risk
- Combine with volume confirmation for higher probability
- 30-min to 60-min charts for intraday entries

### Common Mistakes
1. Using MACD 3-10-16 in choppy/sideways markets (false signals)
2. Ignoring ADX reading (don't trade when ADX < 20)
3. Not using price action for confirmation
4. Only using one timeframe (multi-timeframe confirmation is key)
