What is an opening range break strategy? An opening range break or ORB is exactly what it sounds like. You define a range during the opening period of the trading day, generally the first 30 minutes or so of trading, and then you trade the break of that range once price confirms momentum through it. To define the range, you would take the wick of the high and the wick of the low during that time period. If price breaks above the opening range highs, momentum traders pile in long. If price breaks below the lows, momentum traders pile in short. It sounds almost stupidly simple, which is exactly why most people dismiss it before understanding the underlying psychology. The open is where market participants reposition and psychologically accept the new price range versus the prior range at the close. Overnight information gets priced in and everyone is reacting to fresh information at the same time. That concentrated decision-making creates volatility, volume, and emotion. And volatility plus emotion is where edge lives for many discretionary traders. During the first 30 minutes or so of trading is when market participants are trying to decide whether that new range is too high, too low, or just right. Kind of like the Goldilocks situation, except instead of porridge, we're dealing with stocks.
