This is one of the key differences in a trading floor versus being a retail trader at home. Operating on a trading floor, you can control the environment and build a culture. I was a strong believer that if traders work together, the whole will always be greater than the parts. The Trillium Chicago office operated like a Navy Seal team thanks to the culture I helped construct. We held each other to a high standard. We shared intel and split up work responsibilities. And most of all, we supported each other during the lows while celebrating with each other during the highs. Trading is too hard of a job to go at alone. That's why I have long stressed that traders at home do what they can to link up and learn with other traders. Many traders now use Discord and other tools to replicate the environment of an office. In fact, this team first mindset and working in a team-based culture is one of the biggest lessons I learned in my 15 years of trading.
