Title: Why real trading change happens after rock bottom #trading #tradingpsychology #investing
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=8V_X0eVBuRo
======================================================================

Real behavioral change generally tends to only occur at rock bottom. I had seen so many other traders blow up that I had a self-awareness that I didn't [music] want to be one of them. I had made a hundred million dollars over 15 years of trading and everyone told me to keep going. I was the furthest thing from rock bottom, but I wasn't going to fall for the trap. I had got my bag and I was done trading my health for wealth. The human brain is fundamentally designed to preserve behaviors that are producing reward, status, pleasure, or emotional stimulation. As long as a current behavior still feels good enough, the brain has very little incentive to deeply reassess it. This is one of the biggest misconceptions people have about discipline and self-improvement. They assume humans change logically. Most of the time we change emotionally. Pain is what changes behavior far faster than wisdom does. That's why addiction researchers often describe hitting bottom as the moment where the cost of continuing finally exceeds the emotional payoff. The alcoholic doesn't quit after the fun nights. The gambler doesn't quit during the heater. The trader does not usually walk away after a record year. Why would they? The brain is currently receiving dopamine, validation, excitement, and social reinforcement. From the brain's perspective, the behavior appears beneficial. Evolutionarily speaking, humans are designed to repeat behaviors that appear rewarding. And this is exactly why most traders never seriously think about walking away while they're still ascending.
