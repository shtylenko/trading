This is how I would use $10,000 if I was starting trading from scratch. Step one, learn the game before you play it. Start by studying how markets actually move. Watch content from real traders, take notes, and learn the language before you put any [music] money on the line. Step two, build a trading playbook. Outline what setups you'll trade, what times you'll trade, and your rules for risk. Most traders fail because they wing it instead of following a plan. Step three, trade in a demo account and collect data. Focus on learning, not [music] earning. Start on simulated demo accounts to build your foundation without risking real money. You want to track every trade, journal your results, and look for patterns that actually work for you. That's how you develop consistency before ever scaling up. Step four, review, reflect, and repeat. Every great trader studies their performance daily. This is where growth happens, in review, not in the trade itself. Step five, scale slowly. Once you've proven consistency, go live, but increase size carefully. The goal isn't to double profits overnight, it's to stay in the game for decades. The fastest way to get rich trading is by growing slowly, but safely. I post weekly breakdowns to help you trade like a pro, and avoid the rookie mistakes that wipe out most traders.
