I met up with the legendary Alex Hormozi for advice on building my trading education business. What I didn't expect was that his business advice aligned with becoming an elite trader. Lesson one, you need to be all in to become exceptional. One of the first things Hormozi pressed me on was focus. Nowadays, I'm pulled in a dozen different directions from following the markets, mentoring traders, creating content, and running a non-profit. My schedule is always slammed with a bunch of commitments. Alex called me out on that and asked me how I managed to become one of the best traders in the world with over a hundred million dollars in verified profits. I told him the truth, that I did so by obsessively working. I sacrificed my health, my social life, and poured my heart and soul into becoming the best trader I could be. Alex asked me how good I would have become if I fragmented my attention and pulled myself in as many directions as I pull myself in now. And I said, without hesitation, I would not have even made it. Alex was not surprised. A saying I always think about is this, you can be exceptional at one skill, you can be great at two skills, or you can be good at three. Any more than that, you are mediocre at everything. So, be intentional about how thin you spread yourself.
