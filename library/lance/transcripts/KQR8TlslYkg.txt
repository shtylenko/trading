Title: What Traders Get Wrong about MACD #macd #trading #tradingindicators
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=KQR8TlslYkg
======================================================================

Traders will blindly buy or sell every crossover. MACD line crosses above the signal line, they buy. MACD line crosses below, they sell or short. No context, no levels, no trend analysis. If only trading were so easy that you could just blindly buy and sell these crossovers. The problem is MACD is lagging. It reacts to price. It doesn't predict price. By the time you see a clean crossover, a lot of the move has often already happened. You'll never be able to catch the top of this, but most MACD users are hoping to hop on a change in trend and ride that wave. The issue with that though, is you can't ignore all the other details. A bullish MACD crossover on a 5-minute chart means very little if the daily chart is in a brutal downtrend or sitting under a massive resistance level. You might get a tiny bounce inside a bigger downtrend, but that's not the kind of move that pays traders long-term. MACD is designed to help you see momentum and gauge the strength of a trend. It's not meant to decide your entries and exits all by itself.
