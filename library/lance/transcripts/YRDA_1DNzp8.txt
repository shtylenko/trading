The bobblehead concept is a fundamental visualization technique that helps you focus on what matters and forget about what doesn't. So, what does this concept really mean? If you're like most traders, you have an obsession about your daily P&L. Were you green today? Were you red? What did everyone else in your pod put up today? The list can be endless about what traders choose to obsess over. Regardless of what it is, it isn't healthy. It's demotivating. If you have a bad day, you think you did something wrong. By default, you think you failed or aren't a successful trader. Well, that's not necessarily true, especially in a game as probabilistic as trading. So often, your actual performance has nothing to do with whether you were red or green on the day and more simply with the way the market happened to unfold. Obsessing about P&L doesn't normalize for the opportunity set. Is $200 on the day considered good? Well, I don't know. If it was a very difficult, tricky day filled with traps and everybody else lost money, maybe you traded very well. But if you put up $200 on one of the biggest days of the year and everybody else put up 10 grand, then maybe you didn't perform so well. At the end of the day, P&L doesn't tell the whole story.
