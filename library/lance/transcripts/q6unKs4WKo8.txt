Let's take Herz on April 16th, 2025. >> Welcome back to Squawkbox. Shares of Herz jumping in the pre-market. This follows a more than 50% gain yesterday. Source telling CBC's Scott Robner that Bill Aman stake in the car rental company now close to 20% via shares and swaps. >> This stock went up multiple average trading ranges in a single day. That means it's overextended. It would make a great short candidate, right? Wrong. Not only is the stock breaking out, but that day it was announced that Bill Aman, a famous hedge fund manager who can move markets in a big way, took a big stake in the company and touted how undervalued it is. That is a type of fresh breaking news that can bring in tons of other investors and traders. As a result, what a newbie trading blind to the news might classify as overextended actually becomes an incredible continuation bet. instead. This was an incredible stock to trade long and it was one of the best opportunities in April 2025. Both intraday and as an overnight candidate given how strong we closed and how much volume we did that day. Sure enough, we gapped up a massive amount the next day, which would have been horrendous to be short into. That key variable of breaking news is what changes the whole spectrum of outcomes on the stock. If there wasn't fresh news, the magnitude and likelihood of that move would have been far less.
