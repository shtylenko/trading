Trading like pro sports has exponential pay scales. As your skill improves and you move up the ranks, your earnings don't just increase linearly. Your earnings can go vertical. At Trillium, the trading firm where I spent my career, I saw this firsthand. The number one trader at the firm would make multiples of the 10th best trader, and the 10th best trader would make multiples of the average trader. Just like in the NBA, where the superstar might make 100 times what the bench player makes. A great trader can make 10 times a good trader. And the best traders, they're in another stratosphere entirely. I've had years where I've made tens of millions trading. Even saying that out loud still feels surreal. But crazily enough, I know traders who have made hundreds of millions of dollars over the course of their careers. That's the ceiling. There really isn't one.
