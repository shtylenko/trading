The first sign you should quit is if your mental health can't take it. Trading is one of the hardest professions you will ever do, and the ups and downs come with a very real mental cost. There is no doubt that trading takes a massive psychological, physical, and emotional toll. That's part of why I only trade part-time now, and why I'm done trading my health for wealth. The second sign you should quit is if you are pursuing trading as a profession, rather than just a hobby or entertainment, but you still are not treating it as such. If you are pursuing this as a profession, but are unable to give it dedicated time each week, and are not building structure into your process, then so often you will not see the progress you need. What this means is you need to be doing things like a daily report card, as I explain in this video. You need to be doing trade reviews, and doing the hard work to develop as a trader. If you are months or years into this job, and you still are not pursuing it with a formal process to improve, then that is not a good sign. The third sign you should quit is if, no matter how hard you try, you are unable to follow basic trading rules. If you have a system of risk management or a playbook, but no matter what you do, you are unable to follow it, then that is not a good sign. Some people, no matter what, are unable to follow the rules and have the discipline necessary to succeed at this job. Fourth sign you should quit is if you do not enjoy the job anymore, or you have no passion for it. Now, look, you need to recognize that nobody enjoys the job during periods of struggle. Everybody feels frustrated, and there will be slow periods or tough stretches where you might even hate the job. That is totally normal. I've been there, too. But if for a really sustained period of time, maybe three, four, six months or longer, you have no passion to do the work and no desire to improve, then it might be time to quit, or at least to take a break. The fifth sign you should quit trading is if, when being honest with yourself, you recognize that you got into trading for the wrong reasons. You might have had misguided expectations, or been sold a fake dream by modern social media marketing. You were told that in just a couple hours a day, you could trade from your laptop on the beach and replace your income. As we all know, that just isn't the reality of trading. While you told yourself you had a passion for this stuff, the reality is you had a passion for lying on the beach drinking daiquiris, and not the actual trading part of the fantasy. There's nothing wrong with that. More than ever, many young adults are getting into trading because they are sold the dream. They pursue this profession for the money, the cars, and the supposed models. Let me tell you, trading is not a get-rich-quick scheme. In fact, it is the exact opposite.
