Stage one, total newbie. We all start somewhere. During the newbie stage, this is where we learn the rules of the game and how to operate within it. You start to develop a basic idea of what trading is. You are learning the lingo, how bids and offers work, hotkeys, software, market structure, halt rules, economic event dates, what tickers make up what sectors, basic technical analysis patterns, and a whole assortment of other fun and exciting beginner topics. Sorry to burst your bubble, but at this stage, we absolutely do not want to be trading live or putting any capital at risk. This stage is meant for learning and observing. Our goal is to be an open-minded sponge taking in all the basics of stock trading. There are a dozen beginner trading courses and technical analysis courses out there on the internet. Please go seek those out because your host, me, does not want to sit here for hours teaching what a bid or an offer is. Oftent times at the professional level, this stage would take up the first couple weeks of full-time work. Within 2 weeks, I would expect my new hires to have mastered all the lingo, the software, and much more. For those that can't devote the 60 hours per week, obviously we expect much less, and it's normal for this stage to take maybe 3 to 6 months.
