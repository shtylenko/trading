Title: Stop playing the Comparison Game #stocks #trading #tradingpsychology
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=GzsecC80djQ
======================================================================

Trillium is a hyper-competitive trading firm. And I was trying to be in the top 10 and then eventually the top trader every single year. That dynamic pushes people to their potential, but it also adds a level of stress and pressure that's hard to describe. The better I performed over time, the higher the standard I ended up having for myself on any given day. I always expected myself to be the top P&L on the desk, regardless of whether that was rational or not. Any failure to do so left me feeling inferior and feeling less than. Nowadays, having left Trillium and being independent the last few years, I've only opened myself up to an even bigger pond of comparisons. While I don't have my old co-workers around me to compare myself to, I now have the whole internet and even greater self-imposed pressure. The truth is, there was and always will be a bigger fish out there. And as humans we must come to accept that we cannot find our self-worth by constantly comparing ourselves to others. Confidence and sense of self must come from within. Part of the toxicity of the online world today and social media is this culture of comparisons. And I know how many traders out there it pushes to quit, irrationally so. That's why I felt it's so important for me to bring this up. To survive in this game, you need to do your best to shut those comparisons out and only focus on you making forward progress.
