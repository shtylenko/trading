Let me first show you how Bollinger Bands can be used as a measure of the pendulum concept. Take this daily chart of Palantir. One of my best trades of 2025 was buying Palantir on August 20th. The reasoning behind it was this. The stock gapped up on earnings on August 5th and was steadily strong above the upper Bollinger Band. Note that the bars weren't accelerating higher and being above the upper Bollinger alone doesn't lead me to short or be bearish. We do though end up selling off and then quickly accelerate lower towards that lower Bollinger Band. Now, context is key here. This stock was steadily strong and uptrending which leads to upward angled Bollinger Bands. We then went from steady strong above the bands. In other words, if we were to think of the pendulum as being -10 peak panic, zero is equilibrium, and +10 is peak euphoria, we were a steady strong +6 or so. In a matter of a few days, we melt all the way to the lower Bollinger Band in what I would describe as sentiment resembling -8 or so. In fact, in the final 2 days, the stock lost almost 20% in value despite being a large established company. To summarize, we went from +6 steady strong on the sentiment pendulum all the way to -8 bearish panic in a matter of days. Even better, want to know how I timed my entry on the intraday? I used the 20-period moving average, that middle line from my Bollinger Bands, to time an entry because the stock had been using the moving average as resistance and then that break of the moving average I viewed as a break of the trend lower. It's worth emphasizing again that Bollinger Bands were just one tool. I was also factoring in the capitulatory volume that occurred that day. Tons of nuances involving the intraday pattern to time my entry and it is a confluence of these systems and indicators that lead to successful trading.
