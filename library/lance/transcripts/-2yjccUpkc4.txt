But when does a trend end? In my eyes, I am looking for characteristics like huge volume and price exhaustion that are capitulary signals. Sometimes stocks run too bullish or too bearish and climax with the real flush out. Those are my favorite signals to the end of a trend. Now, that doesn't always happen. There are other ways a trend can end. Here are a few additional examples. After multiple legs in one direction, the larger the legs are, especially if they accelerate in size, it becomes increasingly likely the trend will end. Another example is breaking news or fundamental catalysts that are counter to the trend or when there is no or little consolidation. Consolidation is considered price acceptance where people find a little equilibrium and where price is agreed upon. Check out this chart of Baba. At the time it was a darling stock that ended up getting crushed when President Xi got reelected. We had this massive puke lower where everyone thought China was done for. Nobody can own any Chinese names anymore. What's notable is that volume bar. It's multiples of any volume we had done previously on the year and is in essence a massive price dump. This is an example of the signals in peak pessimism that provided the foundation for this to counter trend and move higher.
