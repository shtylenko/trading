Breaking news leads to repricing of contracts that traders can take advantage of. This is where prediction markets start to resemble real trading. Breaking news creates discrete jumps in probability. For example, a court ruling leaks or a health announcement breaks or a corporate announcement drops. If you are fast, informed, and able to interpret the headline better than the market, you can have an advantage. Yes, there are algos in some contracts, but many headlines are convoluted or complicated. Some headlines have second-order effects that take a ton of time for people to grasp the implications of. That is where sophisticated human traders still do best, whether in stocks or prediction markets.
