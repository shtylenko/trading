Fundamental analysis is any attempt to estimate what something is worth based on the underlying business or underlying value. For stocks, that usually means studying how much money the company makes and what the future prospects of the company's earnings will be. Fundamentals are basically saying, "Here's what I think the value of the company is in the real world and the stock price should eventually reflect that." That word eventually is where the problem start for traders. And traders like us are often using technical analysis, which is the study of what the chart pattern is saying about the stock. Technicals measure behavior through that chart. Price, volume, trend, and how the stock's reacting at key levels. The cleanest comparison is this. Fundamentals assess what something should be worth. Technicals assess what market participants are actually doing right now.
